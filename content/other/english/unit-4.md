:::derivation{label=plunge /plʌndʒ/ v.}
**书本义：** to move or make sb/sth move suddenly and violently downward; to push sth firmly and deeply into sth

**词形：** plunges / plunged / plunging

**常用搭配：**
- *plunge into* 跳入／投入（水中或某状态）
- *plunge down* 急剧下降
- *take the plunge* （经过犹豫后）采取果断行动

**例句：**
1. The diver **plunged** into the icy water to retrieve the lost equipment.
2. Stock prices **plunged** dramatically after the company announced its losses.

✍️ Despite widespread skepticism, the young scientist decided to **take the plunge** and dedicate her career to deep-sea exploration.

:::callout{kind=note label=注意}
*plunge* 既可作及物动词（将某物推入），也可作不及物动词（自己跳入、骤降）。在课文语境中取不及物用法，形容深潜器急速沉入海底。
:::

## 二、课文精讲

### Reading 1 · The Ocean's Deepest Secrets

#### 段落 1

> For centuries, the ocean depths were a realm of myth and imagination. Sailors spoke of sea monsters and bottomless pits, while scientists believed the deep ocean was a barren void, devoid of life and light. Today, we know better — yet the truth is stranger than any legend. The deep sea is a world of breathtaking diversity, where creatures thrive under conditions that would instantly destroy most life on the surface. It is, quite simply, the largest habitat on Earth and the least explored.

**中文翻译：** 几个世纪以来，海洋深处一直是神话与想象的领域。水手们讲述着海怪和无底深渊的故事，而科学家们则认为深海是一片荒芜的虚空，没有生命和光。如今我们有了更清醒的认识——然而真相比任何传说都更为离奇。深海是一个生物多样性令人惊叹的世界，生物在足以瞬间摧毁地表大多数生命的条件下繁衍生息。简而言之，它是地球上最大的栖息地，也是被探索最少的地方。

**讲解：** 本段以对比手法开篇，将古代水手的神话想象与现代科学认知对照，引出深海探索的主题。短语 *devoid of*（完全没有）和 *barren void*（荒芜虚空）共同渲染了人们对深海的旧有偏见。句式上，*where creatures thrive under conditions that would instantly destroy most life on the surface* 使用了定语从句嵌套，突出深海生物的极端适应性。最后一句以 *the largest habitat* 与 *the least explored* 并列，形成强烈的反差，为全文定下基调。

:::callout{kind=note label=教材注释}
The ocean covers about 71% of Earth's surface, yet more than 80% of the ocean floor remains unmapped and unexplored. The deepest point, the Challenger Deep in the Mariana Trench, reaches approximately 10,935 meters — deeper than Mount Everest is tall.
:::

---

#### 段落 2

> Sunlight penetrates only the upper 200 meters of the ocean. Below this zone, called the sunlight zone or epipelagic zone, lies a world of perpetual darkness. As one descends further, the pressure increases by one atmosphere for every 10 meters — reaching over 1,000 times atmospheric pressure at the deepest points. Temperatures hover near freezing. Yet far from being a lifeless wasteland, the deep ocean teems with organisms that have evolved extraordinary adaptations to survive and even flourish in this harsh environment.

**中文翻译：** 阳光只能穿透海洋最上层200米。这一区域被称为阳光带或表层带，其下方是一个永恒黑暗的世界。每下降10米，压力就增加一个大气压——在最深处达到大气压的1000多倍。温度徘徊在冰点附近。然而，深海远非一片没有生命的荒原，它充满了各种生物，这些生物进化出了非凡的适应能力，得以在这种恶劣环境中生存甚至繁盛。

**讲解：** 本段以数据说明深海的极端环境。*penetrates only the upper 200 meters* 用具体数字界定阳光带的范围；*perpetual darkness*（永恒黑暗）与上段的 *devoid of light* 呼应。压力描述 *one atmosphere for every 10 meters* 让读者直观感受深海压力的急剧增长。关键转折由 *Yet far from being* 引出，形成欲扬先抑的修辞效果：先铺垫极端条件，再揭示生命的顽强。*teem with* 是本单元核心词组，意为"充满"，强调深海生物的丰富性。

:::callout{kind=note label=教材注释}
The ocean is divided into five vertical zones based on depth and light penetration: the epipelagic (sunlight) zone (0–200 m), the mesopelagic (twilight) zone (200–1,000 m), the bathypelagic (midnight) zone (1,000–4,000 m), the abyssopelagic (abyss) zone (4,000–6,000 m), and the hadalpelagic (trenches) zone (6,000–11,000 m).
:::

---

#### 段落 3

> One of the most revolutionary discoveries in deep-sea biology came in 1977, when scientists exploring the Galápagos Rift found hydrothermal vents — cracks in the seafloor from which superheated, mineral-rich water gushes into the cold ocean. Around these vents, they found thriving communities of giant tube worms, clams, and shrimp — all living in complete darkness, at temperatures and pressures that would kill most surface organisms. The key to their survival was chemosynthesis: a process by which bacteria convert chemicals from the vent water into energy, forming the base of a food web entirely independent of sunlight.

**中文翻译：** 深海生物学中最具革命性的发现之一出现在1977年，当时科学家在加拉帕戈斯裂谷探索时发现了热液喷口——海床上的裂缝，过热的富含矿物质的水从中涌入寒冷的海洋。在这些喷口周围，他们发现了繁盛的生物群落：巨大的管虫、蛤蜊和虾——它们全部生活在完全黑暗中，处于足以杀死大多数地表生物的温度和压力之下。它们生存的关键是化能合成：一种细菌将喷口水中的化学物质转化为能量的过程，构成了一个完全独立于阳光的食物网基础。

**讲解：** 本段叙述了深海生物学的里程碑事件。*revolutionary discoveries* 点明发现的重要意义。长句 *cracks in the seafloor from which superheated, mineral-rich water gushes into the cold ocean* 用介词 + 关系代词结构 *from which* 精确描述喷口的物理机制。核心词 *chemosynthesis*（化能合成）是本单元重点概念，与光合作用 *photosynthesis* 形成对比——前者依赖化学能，后者依赖光能。*entirely independent of sunlight* 是理解这一发现革命性的关键：它打破了生命必须依赖阳光的传统认知。

:::callout{kind=note label=教材注释}
Before the 1977 discovery, scientists believed all life on Earth ultimately depended on photosynthesis. The hydrothermal vent communities proved that chemosynthesis — using chemical energy from Earth's interior — could sustain complex ecosystems independently. This finding also influenced the search for life on other planets, such as Jupiter's moon Europa, where similar conditions might exist beneath its icy surface.
:::

---

#### 段落 4

> Bioluminescence is another marvel of the deep sea. In the darkness of the midnight zone, where no sunlight reaches, many organisms produce their own light through chemical reactions within their bodies. This living glow serves multiple purposes: it can lure prey, confuse predators, communicate with mates, or serve as a camouflage strategy called counter-illumination. Scientists estimate that over 90% of deep-sea organisms are bioluminescent, making it one of the most common forms of communication on the planet — yet it remains largely unstudied.

**中文翻译：** 生物发光是深海的另一大奇迹。在没有阳光到达的午夜带黑暗中，许多生物通过体内的化学反应产生自己的光。这种活生生的光芒有多种用途：它可以引诱猎物、迷惑捕食者、与配偶交流，或者作为一种被称为反照明的伪装策略。科学家估计，超过90%的深海生物具有生物发光能力，使其成为地球上最常见的交流方式之一——然而它仍在很大程度上未被研究。

**讲解：** 本段聚焦深海生物的另一关键适应机制。*marvel*（奇迹）一词定调，强调生物发光的令人惊叹。*serve multiple purposes* 后接四个并列功能，结构清晰。*counter-illumination*（反照明）是一个专业术语，指生物利用腹部发光来消除自身阴影、融入上方微弱光线的伪装策略。最后一句以数据 *over 90%* 强调生物发光的普遍性，又以 *yet it remains largely unstudied* 点明研究空白，呼应全文"探索未知"的主题。

---

#### 段落 5

> Exploring this realm requires extraordinary technology. Submersibles must withstand crushing pressure, and their instruments must operate in total darkness and near-freezing temperatures. Remotely operated vehicles (ROVs) and autonomous underwater vehicles (AUVs) have become essential tools, deploying high-resolution cameras, robotic arms, and sophisticated sensors to map the seafloor and collect specimens. Each dive brings the possibility of discovering species entirely new to science — and sometimes entirely new ways of living. The deep ocean, it turns out, is not merely a place to study; it is a frontier that challenges our very definition of what life can be.

**中文翻译：** 探索这一领域需要非凡的技术。潜水器必须承受巨大的压力，其仪器必须在完全黑暗和接近冰点的温度下运行。遥控潜水器（ROV）和自主水下航行器（AUV）已成为不可或缺的工具，它们部署高分辨率相机、机械臂和精密传感器来绘制海底地图并采集标本。每一次潜水都有可能发现对科学而言全新的物种——有时甚至是全新的生存方式。事实证明，深海不仅仅是一个值得研究的地方；它是一个挑战我们对生命定义本身的前沿。

**讲解：** 本段转向技术层面，说明深海探索的工程挑战。*withstand crushing pressure* 中的 *withstand* 是核心词汇，意为"承受、经受住"。*deploying* 作为现在分词修饰 ROV 和 AUV 的操作方式，展示了具体的技术手段。最后一句是全文的点睛之笔：*not merely a place to study; it is a frontier that challenges our very definition of what life can be*，使用 *not merely... it is...* 的递进结构，将深海从研究对象升华为认知边界，呼应单元标题 *Explore the Unknown*。

---

#### 长难句解析

:::parse{label=As one descends further, the pressure increases by one atmosphere for every 10 meters — reaching over 1,000 times atmospheric pressure at the deepest points.}
**结构分析：** *As one descends further* 为时间状语从句；主句为 *the pressure increases by one atmosphere for every 10 meters*；破折号后 *reaching over 1,000 times atmospheric pressure at the deepest points* 为现在分词短语作结果状语，补充说明压力的极端值。

**翻译：** 随着进一步下潜，每10米压力增加一个大气压——在最深处达到大气压的1000多倍。

**考点：** 现在分词作结果状语；倍数表达法 *1,000 times*。
:::

:::parse{label=The key to their survival was chemosynthesis: a process by which bacteria convert chemicals from the vent water into energy, forming the base of a food web entirely independent of sunlight.}
**结构分析：** 主句为 *The key to their survival was chemosynthesis*；冒号后 *a process* 为 *chemosynthesis* 的同位语；*by which bacteria convert chemicals from the vent water into energy* 为介词 + 关系代词引导的定语从句修饰 *process*；*forming the base of a food web entirely independent of sunlight* 为现在分词短语作结果状语，其中 *entirely independent of sunlight* 为形容词短语后置修饰 *food web*。

**翻译：** 它们生存的关键是化能合成：一种细菌将喷口水中的化学物质转化为能量的过程，构成了一个完全独立于阳光的食物网基础。

**考点：** 介词 + 关系代词定语从句（*by which*）；同位语结构；现在分词作结果状语。
:::

#### 主旨与写作手法

本文以"深海探索"为主线，采用"旧观念→新发现→技术挑战→哲学启示"的递进结构。写作手法上，作者善用对比（古代神话 vs 现代科学、阳光带 vs 深海黑暗、光合作用 vs 化能合成）和数据支撑（200米、1000倍压力、90%生物发光）来增强说服力。结尾将深海从科学对象升华为认知前沿，呼应标题 *Explore the Unknown*，体现科普文章"以事实激发想象"的写作策略。

### Reading 2 · Are We Alone in the Universe?

#### 段落 1

> Since the dawn of civilization, humans have looked up at the stars and wondered: are we alone? For most of history, this question belonged to philosophy and religion. In the twentieth century, however, it became a scientific one. With the development of radio telescopes and space probes, humanity began its first serious search for extraterrestrial intelligence — an endeavor that combines astronomy, biology, engineering, and a generous dose of optimism.

**中文翻译：** 自文明诞生以来，人类便仰望星空，思考一个问题：我们是孤独的吗？在大部分历史中，这个问题属于哲学和宗教领域。然而到了二十世纪，它变成了一个科学问题。随着射电望远镜和太空探测器的发展，人类开始了对地外智慧的首次严肃搜索——这是一项结合了天文学、生物学、工程学和大量乐观主义的事业。

**讲解：** 本段以 *Since the dawn of civilization* 开篇，将视野拉至人类文明起点，赋予话题历史纵深感。*belonged to philosophy and religion* 与 *became a scientific one* 形成时代转折，标志从思辨到实证的转变。*a generous dose of optimism*（大量的乐观主义）以幽默笔调点明搜索地外文明的困难与希望并存。核心词 *endeavor*（事业、努力）概括了 SETI 项目的跨学科性质。

---

#### 段落 2

> The numbers are certainly encouraging. Our Milky Way galaxy contains between 100 and 400 billion stars, and current estimates suggest that a significant fraction of these stars host planets. Of these exoplanets, astronomers have identified dozens that orbit within the habitable zone — the region around a star where temperatures allow liquid water to exist. Given these figures, the probability that at least one of these planets harbors life seems overwhelmingly high. As the astronomer Carl Sagan famously put it, "The universe is a pretty big place. If it's just us, it seems like an awful waste of space."

**中文翻译：** 这些数字确实令人鼓舞。我们的银河系包含1000亿到4000亿颗恒星，目前的估计表明其中相当一部分恒星拥有行星。在这些系外行星中，天文学家已经确认了数十颗运行在宜居带内——即恒星周围温度允许液态水存在的区域。鉴于这些数据，至少有一颗行星孕育生命的概率似乎压倒性地高。正如天文学家卡尔·萨根那句名言所说："宇宙是个相当大的地方。如果只有我们，那似乎是对空间的极大浪费。"

**讲解：** 本段以数据论证开始。*100 and 400 billion stars* 给出银河系恒星数量范围，体现科学估算的不确定性。*habitable zone*（宜居带）是核心概念，后接同位语 *the region around a star where temperatures allow liquid water to exist* 进行解释。*overwhelmingly high*（压倒性地高）强调概率之高。引用卡尔·萨根的名言为段落收尾，既增强说服力又增添人文色彩，是科普写作中"数据 + 名言"的经典组合。

:::callout{kind=note label=教材注释}
The "habitable zone," also known as the "Goldilocks zone," refers to the orbital region around a star where conditions are neither too hot nor too cold for liquid water to exist on a planet's surface. The concept was introduced in 1953 by astronomer Harlow Shapley. However, being in the habitable zone does not guarantee that a planet is actually habitable — atmospheric composition, geological activity, and magnetic fields also play crucial roles.
:::

---

#### 段落 3

> In 1961, astronomer Frank Drake proposed an equation to estimate the number of detectable civilizations in our galaxy. The Drake Equation multiplies together factors such as the rate of star formation, the fraction of stars with planets, the number of habitable planets per star system, the probability of life evolving, the probability of intelligence emerging, the probability of communicative technology developing, and the expected lifespan of a civilization. While we can now estimate some of these factors with reasonable confidence, others remain pure speculation. The result is a number that ranges from one (we are alone) to millions.

**中文翻译：** 1961年，天文学家弗兰克·德雷克提出了一个方程来估算银河系中可探测文明的数量。德雷克方程将以下因素相乘：恒星形成率、拥有行星的恒星比例、每个星系中宜居行星的数量、生命进化的概率、智慧出现的概率、通信技术发展的概率，以及文明的预期寿命。虽然我们现在可以合理自信地估算其中一些因素，但其他因素仍纯属推测。其结果是一个从一（我们是孤独的）到数百万不等的数字。

**讲解：** 本段介绍德雷克方程——SETI 领域的核心理论框架。作者列举了七个因子，使用 *multiplies together factors such as...* 的列举结构，清晰呈现方程的逻辑。*reasonable confidence* 与 *pure speculation* 形成对比，体现科学的诚实态度：已知与未知并存。最后一句 *ranges from one to millions* 以极端值对比收束，既展示不确定性，又暗示两种可能性的巨大差异。

:::callout{kind=note label=教材注释}
The Drake Equation: N = R* × fp × ne × fl × fi × fc × L, where N = the number of civilizations in our galaxy with which communication might be possible. The equation was formulated as a way to stimulate scientific discussion at the first SETI meeting at the Green Bank Observatory in 1961.
:::

---

#### 段落 4

> In 1974, scientists used the Arecibo radio telescope in Puerto Rico to transmit a message into space aimed at the globular star cluster M13. The message contained information about our solar system, the chemical elements of DNA, a human figure, and the radio telescope itself. It will take 25,000 years to reach its destination and 25,000 more for any reply to return — a timeline that humbles our sense of urgency. Yet the Arecibo message was a symbolic milestone: humanity's first deliberate attempt to announce its existence to the cosmos.

**中文翻译：** 1974年，科学家使用波多黎各的阿雷西博射电望远镜向太空发射了一条信息，目标指向球状星团M13。这条信息包含了关于我们太阳系、DNA的化学元素、人体形象以及射电望远镜本身的信息。它需要25000年才能到达目的地，还需再过25000年才能收到任何回复——这一时间跨度令我们的紧迫感显得渺小。然而，阿雷西博信息是一座象征性的里程碑：人类首次有意向宇宙宣告自身存在的尝试。

**讲解：** 本段以具体事件——阿雷西博信息——将理论讨论落地为实际行动。*transmit a message into space* 中的 *transmit* 是核心词汇。两个 *25,000 years* 的重复使用强调了星际通信的时间尺度，与人类文明的短暂形成鲜明对比。*a timeline that humbles our sense of urgency* 中的 *humbles*（使……显得渺小）用词精妙，将客观时间转化为主观感受。最后一句以 *symbolic milestone* 收束，既肯定其意义，又暗示其实际效果有限。

---

#### 段落 5

> This leads to what is known as Fermi's paradox: if the probability of extraterrestrial intelligence is so high, why have we found no evidence of it? Proposed solutions range from the plausible to the fantastic. Perhaps intelligent civilizations are so far apart that their signals have not yet reached us. Perhaps they exist but have no desire to communicate. Perhaps, more unsettlingly, civilizations tend to destroy themselves before they can achieve interstellar communication. Or perhaps — as some scientists speculate — life itself is far rarer than the Drake Equation suggests, and we may be the only intelligent species in our galaxy, at least for now.

**中文翻译：** 这就引出了所谓的费米悖论：如果地外智慧的概率如此之高，为什么我们找不到任何证据？提出的解决方案从合理的到离奇的都有。也许智慧文明相距如此遥远，以至于它们的信号尚未到达我们这里。也许它们存在但无意交流。也许更令人不安的是，文明往往在实现星际通信之前就自我毁灭了。又或者——正如一些科学家推测的那样——生命本身远比德雷克方程所暗示的更为稀有，我们可能是银河系中唯一的智慧物种，至少目前如此。

**讲解：** 本段是全文的思想高潮。*Fermi's paradox*（费米悖论）是核心概念，以设问句 *if... why...* 引出矛盾。四个 *Perhaps* 排比句构成层递结构，从距离因素→意愿因素→自我毁灭因素→生命稀有因素，逐步深入，最后一个 *Perhaps* 以 *more unsettlingly*（更令人不安地）修饰，将情感推向最深。*speculate*（推测）为核心词，强调科学中不确定性的一面。结尾 *at least for now* 留有余地，暗示这一结论可能随时间改变。

:::callout{kind=note label=教材注释}
Fermi's paradox is named after physicist Enrico Fermi, who reportedly posed the question in 1950 during a casual lunch conversation: "Where is everybody?" The paradox highlights the contradiction between the high probability of extraterrestrial civilizations (as suggested by the Drake Equation) and the lack of any evidence for or contact with such civilizations.
:::

---

#### 段落 6

> Whether we are alone or not, the search itself has value. It drives technological innovation, deepens our understanding of life's origins, and forces us to reflect on humanity's place in the cosmos. The instruments built to listen for alien signals have also led to discoveries about pulsars, quasars, and the structure of the universe. And perhaps most importantly, the search for extraterrestrial intelligence reminds us that our own planet, with its fragile biosphere and its remarkable diversity of life, is a treasure worth protecting — whether or not anyone else is out there to notice.

**中文翻译：** 无论我们是否孤独，搜索本身就有价值。它推动了技术创新，加深了我们对生命起源的理解，并促使我们反思人类在宇宙中的位置。为了监听外星信号而建造的仪器也带来了关于脉冲星、类星体和宇宙结构的发现。或许最重要的是，对地外智慧的搜索提醒我们，我们自己的星球——拥有其脆弱的生物圈和非凡的生物多样性——是一件值得保护的珍宝，无论是否有其他人在那里注意到它。

**讲解：** 本段为全文总结，将搜索地外文明的意义从"是否找到"转向"搜索过程本身的价值"。三个并列动词 *drives / deepens / forces* 构成层递，从技术→科学→哲学逐层升华。*whether or not anyone else is out there to notice* 作为让步状语收束全文，将视角从宇宙拉回地球，赋予文章环保意识。这种"由远及近、由外而内"的收束方式是优秀科普文章的常见策略。

---

#### 长难句解析

:::parse{label=Given these figures, the probability that at least one of these planets harbors life seems overwhelmingly high.}
**结构分析：** *Given these figures* 为过去分词短语作条件状语（意为"鉴于这些数据"）；主句为 *the probability... seems overwhelmingly high*；*that at least one of these planets harbors life* 为同位语从句修饰 *probability*。

**翻译：** 鉴于这些数据，至少有一颗行星孕育生命的概率似乎压倒性地高。

**考点：** *Given* 引导的条件状语；同位语从句（*that* 引导，修饰抽象名词 *probability*）。
:::

:::parse{label=Perhaps, more unsettlingly, civilizations tend to destroy themselves before they can achieve interstellar communication.}
**结构分析：** *Perhaps* 为副词，表推测；*more unsettlingly* 为比较级副词短语作插入语；主句为 *civilizations tend to destroy themselves*；*before they can achieve interstellar communication* 为时间状语从句。

**翻译：** 也许更令人不安的是，文明往往在实现星际通信之前就自我毁灭了。

**考点：** *tend to do*（倾向于做某事）；*before* 引导时间状语从句的翻译处理（"在……之前就……"）；插入语 *more unsettlingly* 的修辞效果。
:::

#### 主旨与写作手法

本文围绕"人类是否孤独"这一终极问题展开，采用"历史回顾→数据论证→理论框架→实践行动→悖论反思→价值升华"的六段递进结构。写作手法上，作者善用设问（*are we alone?* / *why have we found no evidence?*）、排比（四个 *Perhaps*）、引用（卡尔·萨根名言）和数据（100-400亿恒星、25000年）来构建论证。结尾从宇宙回归地球，将科学探索与人文关怀结合，体现科普写作"以理性激发感性"的高级策略。

## 三、知识点总结

### 核心词汇对比

| 词汇 | 释义 | 课文语境 |
|---|---|---|
| *plunge* | 急速沉入 | 深潜器沉入海底 |
| *withstand* | 承受、经受住 | 潜水器承受深海压力 |
| *deploy* | 部署、调配 | 部署水下航行器 |
| *transmit* | 发射、传输 | 向太空发射信号 |
| *emit* | 发出、释放 | 恒星发出辐射 |
| *sustain* | 维持 | 维持生命存在 |

### 核心概念

- **化能合成（chemosynthesis）**：深海热液喷口附近细菌利用化学能合成有机物的过程，独立于阳光。
- **生物发光（bioluminescence）**：深海生物通过体内化学反应产生光的现象，用于引诱猎物、伪装和交流。
- **宜居带（habitable zone）**：恒星周围温度允许液态水存在的区域，是搜索地外生命的关键指标。
- **德雷克方程（Drake Equation）**：估算银河系中可探测文明数量的公式，由七个因子相乘得出。
- **费米悖论（Fermi's paradox）**：地外文明高概率存在与零证据之间的矛盾。

### 语法要点

- **介词 + 关系代词定语从句**：*from which / by which*（Reading 1 段落3、Reading 2 段落3）
- **同位语从句**：*the probability that...*（Reading 2 段落2）
- **现在分词作结果状语**：*reaching over 1,000 times... / forming the base of...*（Reading 1 段落2、3）
- **Given 引导条件状语**：*Given these figures...*（Reading 2 段落2）
- **排比与层递修辞**：四个 *Perhaps* 排比句（Reading 2 段落5）

### 主题升华

两篇课文从不同维度诠释了"探索未知"这一单元主题。Reading 1 聚焦**向内探索**——深海是地球上最后的未知前沿；Reading 2 聚焦**向外探索**——宇宙是人类最宏大的未知领域。两者共同揭示：探索未知的意义不仅在于发现新事物，更在于重新认识我们自身和我们所处的位置。
