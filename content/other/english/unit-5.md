## 二、课文精讲

### Reading 1 · Does Language Shape the Way We Think?

#### 段落 1

> For centuries, philosophers and linguists have debated a deceptively simple question: does the language we speak shape the way we think? The idea that language determines thought — known as linguistic determinism — was first proposed by Benjamin Lee Whorf and his mentor Edward Sapir in the early twentieth century. Their hypothesis, often called the Sapir-Whorf hypothesis, suggested that speakers of different languages perceive the world in fundamentally different ways because their languages impose different categories on reality.

**中文翻译：** 几个世纪以来，哲学家和语言学家一直在争论一个看似简单的问题：我们所说的语言是否塑造了我们的思维方式？语言决定思维的观点——即语言决定论——最早由本杰明·李·沃夫及其导师爱德华·萨丕尔在二十世纪初提出。他们的假说通常被称为萨丕尔-沃夫假说，认为不同语言的使用者以根本不同的方式感知世界，因为他们的语言对现实施加了不同的范畴。

**讲解：** 本段以设问句开篇，直接点出全文核心问题。*deceptively simple*（看似简单实则复杂）暗示这一问题的深度。随后引入学术背景：*linguistic determinism*（语言决定论）和 *Sapir-Whorf hypothesis*（萨丕尔-沃夫假说），为后文的论证奠定理论基础。*impose different categories on reality*（对现实施加不同范畴）是理解该假说的关键——语言不仅是表达工具，更是认知框架。

:::callout{kind=note label=教材注释}
The Sapir-Whorf hypothesis has two versions: the "strong" version (linguistic determinism) claims that language completely determines thought and limits what we can think; the "weak" version (linguistic relativity) claims that language influences thought tendencies but does not determine them. Modern research largely supports the weak version.
:::

---

#### 段落 2

> Consider how different languages describe colors. In English, the basic color terms include separate words for "blue" and "green." In some languages, however, a single word covers both colors. Research has shown that speakers of languages with separate terms for blue and green can distinguish between the two shades more quickly than speakers whose language uses one word. This suggests that language does not merely label what we see — it actively shapes how we categorize and remember visual information.

**中文翻译：** 想想不同的语言是如何描述颜色的。在英语中，基本颜色词包括分别表示"蓝色"和"绿色"的独立词汇。然而，在某些语言中，一个词就涵盖了两种颜色。研究表明，使用分别表示蓝色和绿色词汇的语言使用者，能比使用一个词的语言使用者更快地区分这两种色调。这表明语言不仅仅是给我们所见之物贴标签——它还在积极地塑造我们如何分类和记忆视觉信息。

**讲解：** 本段以颜色词为例证，是全文最直观的论证段落。*Consider how...* 以祈使句引入例证，拉近与读者的距离。对比结构 *separate words for "blue" and "green"* 与 *a single word covers both colors* 清晰呈现语言差异。关键句 *language does not merely label what we see — it actively shapes how we categorize and remember visual information* 是段落核心论点，其中 *not merely... it actively shapes...* 的递进结构强调了语言的主动建构作用。

:::callout{kind=note label=教材注释}
The color-categorization experiment was conducted by Paul Kay and Brent Berlin in 1969. They studied 100 languages and found that color terms follow a universal hierarchy: if a language has only two color terms, they are always black and white; if three, red is added; and so on. This finding challenged strict linguistic determinism while supporting linguistic relativity.
:::

---

#### 段落 3

> Spatial language provides another striking example. Some languages, such as Guugu Yimithirr spoken in northern Australia, do not use words like "left" and "right." Instead, speakers use absolute directions — north, south, east, and west — even when describing the position of an object inside a room. As a result, speakers of such languages maintain a constant awareness of cardinal directions that most speakers of English or Chinese never develop. Language, it seems, can train the mind to attend to aspects of the world it would otherwise ignore.

**中文翻译：** 空间语言提供了另一个引人注目的例子。一些语言，如澳大利亚北部使用的古古伊米斯语，不使用"左"和"右"这样的词。相反，使用者使用绝对方位——东、南、西、北——即使在描述房间内物体的位置时也是如此。因此，这类语言的使用者保持着对基本方位的持续意识，而大多数英语或汉语使用者从未发展出这种意识。语言似乎可以训练大脑去关注它原本会忽略的世界层面。

**讲解：** 本段以空间语言为第二例证，进一步论证语言对认知的影响。*striking example*（引人注目的例子）承接上段的色彩例证，形成层递。*Guugu Yimithirr* 是真实存在的澳大利亚原住民语言，增加论证的可信度。*maintain a constant awareness*（保持持续意识）与 *never develop* 形成对比，突出语言对认知习惯的塑造。段末 *Language, it seems, can train the mind to attend to aspects of the world it would otherwise ignore* 是对两个例证的总结升华。

:::callout{kind=note label=教材注释}
The Guugu Yimithirr language was studied by linguist Stephen Levinson at the Max Planck Institute. He found that speakers of absolute-direction languages have extraordinary spatial memory and can point to cardinal directions with near-compass accuracy even in unfamiliar indoor environments.
:::

---

#### 段落 4

> Critics of the Sapir-Whorf hypothesis argue that the effects of language on thought are modest and limited to specific domains. They point out that bilinguals can easily translate between languages, which suggests that thoughts exist independently of any particular language. Moreover, pre-linguistic infants and non-human animals demonstrate sophisticated cognitive abilities — recognizing faces, solving problems, and navigating space — without possessing language at all. If thought requires language, how could infants and animals think?

**中文翻译：** 萨丕尔-沃夫假说的批评者认为，语言对思维的影响是有限的，且局限于特定领域。他们指出，双语者可以轻松地在语言之间翻译，这表明思维独立于任何特定语言而存在。此外，前语言期的婴儿和非人类动物展示出复杂的认知能力——识别面孔、解决问题和导航空间——而根本不拥有语言。如果思维需要语言，婴儿和动物又如何思考呢？

**讲解：** 本段引入反方论点，使论证更加平衡。*Critics of...* 标志视角转换。三个反驳论据层层递进：①翻译能力证明思维独立于语言；②婴儿的认知能力证明无语言也可思维；③动物的认知能力进一步佐证。最后以设问句 *If thought requires language, how could infants and animals think?* 收束，修辞有力，将逻辑矛盾推向高潮。

---

#### 段落 5

> The current consensus among researchers is more nuanced than either extreme. Language does not imprison thought, but it does bias it. The categories and distinctions encoded in our language subtly influence what we notice, how we categorize information, and what we remember — but they do not determine what we can think. As the linguist Roman Jakobson famously put it: "Languages differ essentially in what they must convey, not in what they may convey." In other words, language shapes the default settings of our attention, but we can always override those settings when we choose to.

**中文翻译：** 研究者目前的共识比任何一种极端观点都更为微妙。语言并不囚禁思维，但确实会对其产生偏向。我们语言中编码的范畴和区分微妙地影响着我们注意到什么、如何分类信息以及记住什么——但它们并不决定我们能思考什么。正如语言学家罗曼·雅各布森的名言："语言的本质区别在于它们必须传达什么，而不在于它们可以传达什么。"换言之，语言塑造了我们注意力的默认设置，但当我们选择时，我们总是可以覆盖这些设置。

**讲解：** 本段提出平衡的结论。*more nuanced than either extreme*（比任何极端都更微妙）定调。核心句 *Language does not imprison thought, but it does bias it* 用 *not... but...* 结构精炼地概括了现代共识。引用雅各布森的名言增强学术权威性。*default settings*（默认设置）的比喻生动形象——语言像电脑的默认设置，影响我们的倾向，但不限制我们的可能性。*override*（覆盖）一词与 *default settings* 呼应，强调人类认知的自主性。

---

#### 长难句解析

:::parse{label=Speakers of languages with separate terms for blue and green can distinguish between the two shades more quickly than speakers whose language uses one word.}
**结构分析：** 主语为 *Speakers of languages with separate terms for blue and green*（带有复杂介词短语修饰）；谓语为 *can distinguish*；宾语为 *between the two shades*；比较状语为 *more quickly than speakers whose language uses one word*，其中 *whose language uses one word* 为定语从句修饰 *speakers*。

**翻译：** 使用分别表示蓝色和绿色词汇的语言使用者，能比使用一个词的语言使用者更快地区分这两种色调。

**考点：** 比较结构 *more... than...*；定语从句 *whose* 引导，修饰人；介词短语作后置定语 *of languages with separate terms for...*。
:::

:::parse{label=Language shapes the default settings of our attention, but we can always override those settings when we choose to.}
**结构分析：** 并列复合句。第一分句 *Language shapes the default settings of our attention*；连词 *but* 表转折；第二分句 *we can always override those settings*，*when we choose to* 为时间状语从句，*to* 后省略了动词 *override*。

**翻译：** 语言塑造了我们注意力的默认设置，但当我们选择时，我们总是可以覆盖这些设置。

**考点：** *but* 引导的并列转折句；*when* 引导时间状语从句；不定式省略 *to*（省略了 *override*）。
:::

### Reading 2 · The Bilingual Brain

#### 段落 1

> In a world where more than half the population speaks at least two languages, bilingualism is not the exception — it is the norm. Yet for decades, researchers debated whether growing up with two languages was a cognitive advantage or a cognitive burden. Early studies in the mid-twentieth century suggested that bilingual children lagged behind their monolingual peers in vocabulary and verbal intelligence. These findings fueled concerns that bilingualism confused children and hindered their academic progress.

**中文翻译：** 在一个超过半数人口会说至少两种语言的世界里，双语不是例外——而是常态。然而几十年来，研究者一直在争论：在两种语言中成长是一种认知优势还是认知负担。二十世纪中叶的早期研究表明，双语儿童在词汇量和言语智力方面落后于单语同龄人。这些发现引发了人们的担忧，认为双语会混淆儿童并阻碍其学业进步。

**讲解：** 本段以全球数据开篇，将双语定位为常态而非例外。*not the exception — it is the norm* 用破折号强调，翻转读者可能持有的偏见。随后回顾早期研究的负面结论：*lagged behind*（落后）、*cognitive burden*（认知负担）、*confused children*（混淆儿童），为后文的反转做铺垫。这种"先抑后扬"的结构是科普写作的经典策略。

:::callout{kind=note label=教材注释}
The early negative studies of bilingualism (1930s-1960s) were later found to have methodological flaws: they compared bilingual immigrants living in poverty with monolingual middle-class children, confounding language status with socioeconomic status. When controlled for socioeconomic factors, the bilingual "disadvantage" largely disappeared.
:::

---

#### 段落 2

> Modern research has overturned those early findings. Using brain-imaging techniques such as fMRI, scientists have discovered that bilinguals and monolinguals process language differently. In bilinguals, both languages are constantly active, even when only one is being used. This means that a bilingual brain is perpetually managing two linguistic systems — selecting the appropriate language and inhibiting the other. This constant juggling act, researchers believe, is what gives bilinguals their cognitive edge.

**中文翻译：** 现代研究已经推翻了那些早期发现。利用功能性磁共振成像等脑成像技术，科学家发现双语者和单语者处理语言的方式不同。在双语者的大脑中，两种语言始终处于活跃状态，即使只使用其中一种。这意味着双语大脑在持续管理两个语言系统——选择合适的语言并抑制另一种。研究者认为，这种持续的切换正是赋予双语者认知优势的原因。

**讲解：** 本段以 *overturned*（推翻）一词标志范式转变。*fMRI*（功能性磁共振成像）增加科学可信度。核心发现 *both languages are constantly active* 是理解双语认知的关键——双语不是简单的"开关"模式，而是"并行"模式。*perpetually managing*（持续管理）、*selecting... and inhibiting...*（选择和抑制）描述了大脑的执行控制过程。*juggling act*（杂耍般的切换）是生动的比喻，将抽象的认知过程形象化。

:::callout{kind=note label=教材注释}
fMRI (functional Magnetic Resonance Imaging) measures brain activity by detecting changes in blood oxygen levels. Studies by Ellen Bialystok at York University have shown that bilinguals use more brain regions for language processing, particularly the prefrontal cortex, which is associated with executive function.
:::

---

#### 段落 3

> The cognitive benefits of bilingualism are most evident in what psychologists call "executive function" — the brain's control system for managing attention, suppressing distractions, and switching between tasks. In laboratory tests, bilinguals consistently outperform monolinguals on tasks that require ignoring irrelevant information or switching rules. For example, in the Stroop test — where participants must name the color of a word while ignoring the word's meaning — bilinguals are faster and more accurate, suggesting superior inhibitory control.

**中文翻译：** 双语的认知优势在心理学家所称的"执行功能"中最为明显——执行功能是大脑的控制系统，负责管理注意力、抑制干扰和在任务之间切换。在实验室测试中，双语者在需要忽略无关信息或切换规则的任务上始终优于单语者。例如，在斯特鲁普测试中——参与者必须说出单词的颜色而忽略单词的含义——双语者更快更准确，表明他们具有更强的抑制控制能力。

**讲解：** 本段具体阐述双语认知优势的表现。*executive function*（执行功能）是核心概念，三个并列短语 *managing attention, suppressing distractions, and switching between tasks* 精确定义其内涵。*Stroop test*（斯特鲁普测试）是经典实验范式，增加论证的具体性。*consistently outperform*（始终优于）和 *faster and more accurate*（更快更准确）用比较级强调双语优势的稳健性。

:::callout{kind=note label=教材注释}
The Stroop test was invented by J. Ridley Stroop in 1935. In the classic version, color words (e.g., "red," "blue") are printed in incongruent ink colors (e.g., the word "red" printed in blue ink). Participants must name the ink color while suppressing the automatic tendency to read the word. The task measures inhibitory control and attention.
:::

---

#### 段落 4

> Perhaps even more remarkably, bilingualism appears to offer protection against cognitive decline in old age. Studies have found that bilingual patients develop symptoms of dementia an average of four to five years later than monolinguals — a delay more effective than any known drug. Researchers believe this is because the enhanced executive function built up over a lifetime of bilingualism creates a "cognitive reserve" — a buffer that helps the brain compensate for age-related deterioration.

**中文翻译：** 也许更引人注目的是，双语似乎能提供对抗老年认知衰退的保护。研究发现，双语患者平均比单语者晚四到五年出现痴呆症状——这一延迟比任何已知药物都更有效。研究者认为，这是因为终身双语所积累的增强执行功能创造了"认知储备"——一种帮助大脑补偿与年龄相关衰退的缓冲。

**讲解：** 本段将双语优势从实验室延伸到临床，是全文最具说服力的段落。*Perhaps even more remarkably* 以递进副词引入更强的论据。*four to five years later* 和 *more effective than any known drug* 用具体数据和比较突出双语的保护效果。*cognitive reserve*（认知储备）是核心概念，*buffer*（缓冲）的比喻使其易于理解。*built up over a lifetime* 强调双语优势是长期积累的结果。

---

#### 段落 5

> The implications of these findings extend far beyond individual health. In an increasingly globalized world, bilingualism is an asset not only for communication but for cognitive resilience. Schools that promote bilingual education are not merely teaching a second language — they are strengthening their students' minds. And in a world where mental sharpness is increasingly valued, the bilingual brain may be one of our most powerful tools for navigating the complexities of modern life.

**中文翻译：** 这些发现的意义远远超出了个人健康。在日益全球化的世界中，双语不仅是一种沟通资产，更是认知韧性的来源。推广双语教育的学校不仅仅是在教授第二语言——它们还在强化学生的大脑。在一个越来越重视思维敏锐度的世界里，双语大脑可能是我们应对现代生活复杂性的最有力的工具之一。

**讲解：** 本段将科学发现上升为社会意义，是全文的升华段。*extend far beyond*（远远超越）标志视角从个体到社会的转换。*not merely... they are...* 的递进结构与 Reading 1 段落2的 *not merely... it actively shapes...* 遥相呼应。*cognitive resilience*（认知韧性）和 *navigating the complexities*（应对复杂性）将双语优势与当代生活需求连接。结尾 *one of our most powerful tools* 以最高级收束，赋予文章前瞻性和号召力。

#### 长难句解析

:::parse{label=In bilinguals, both languages are constantly active, even when only one is being used.}
**结构分析：** 介词短语 *In bilinguals* 作状语；主句为 *both languages are constantly active*；*even when only one is being used* 为让步状语从句，其中 *one* 指代 *one language*，*is being used* 为现在进行时被动语态。

**翻译：** 在双语者的大脑中，两种语言始终处于活跃状态，即使只使用其中一种。

**考点：** *even when* 引导让步状语从句；代词 *one* 的指代；现在进行时被动语态 *is being used*。
:::

:::parse{label=Studies have found that bilingual patients develop symptoms of dementia an average of four to five years later than monolinguals — a delay more effective than any known drug.}
**结构分析：** 主句为 *Studies have found*；宾语从句 *that bilingual patients develop symptoms of dementia an average of four to five years later than monolinguals*；破折号后 *a delay more effective than any known drug* 为同位语，补充说明 *four to five years later*。

**翻译：** 研究发现，双语患者平均比单语者晚四到五年出现痴呆症状——这一延迟比任何已知药物都更有效。

**考点：** *that* 引导宾语从句；比较结构 *later than*；破折号后同位语 *a delay more effective than...*；形容词比较级短语。
:::

## 三、知识点总结

### 核心词汇对比

| 词汇 | 释义 | 课文语境 |
|---|---|---|
| *shape* | 塑造 | 语言塑造思维 |
| *perceive* | 感知 | 不同语言使用者感知世界 |
| *acquire* | 习得 | 儿童习得语言 |
| *inhibit* | 抑制 | 双语者抑制非目标语言 |
| *navigate* | 切换、应对 | 双语者在语言间切换 |
| *decline* | 衰退 | 双语延缓认知衰退 |

### 核心概念

- **语言相对论（linguistic relativity）**：语言影响但不决定思维的弱版萨丕尔-沃夫假说，现代研究的共识立场。
- **执行功能（executive function）**：大脑的控制系统，负责管理注意力、抑制干扰和任务切换，双语者在此方面表现更优。
- **认知储备（cognitive reserve）**：终身双语积累的认知能力缓冲，可延缓老年痴呆症状的出现。
- **斯特鲁普测试（Stroop test）**：测量抑制控制能力的经典实验，双语者表现优于单语者。
- **语言决定论（linguistic determinism）**：强版萨丕尔-沃夫假说，认为语言完全决定思维，已被现代研究否定。

### 语法要点

- **比较结构**：*more quickly than* / *later than* / *more effective than*（Reading 1 段落2、Reading 2 段落4）
- **让步状语从句**：*even when only one is being used*（Reading 2 段落2）
- **同位语从句**：*the idea that language determines thought*（Reading 1 段落1）
- **破折号后同位语**：*a delay more effective than any known drug*（Reading 2 段落4）
- **not merely... but/it... 递进结构**：两篇课文均使用

### 主题升华

两篇课文从不同维度诠释了"语言的力量"这一单元主题。Reading 1 聚焦**语言与思维的关系**——语言如何微妙地塑造我们的认知方式；Reading 2 聚焦**双语与大脑的关系**——掌握多种语言如何增强认知能力并保护大脑健康。两者共同揭示：语言不仅是交流工具，更是塑造心智的力量。学习一门新语言，不仅是掌握一种新的表达方式，更是打开一扇认知世界的新窗口。
