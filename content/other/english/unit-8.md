## 二、课文精讲

### Reading 1 · The Sixth Extinction

#### 段落 1

> Earth has experienced five mass extinction events in its 4.5-billion-year history. The most famous, the Cretaceous extinction 66 million years ago, wiped out the dinosaurs and nearly 75% of all species. Each of these events was caused by natural catastrophes — asteroid impacts, volcanic eruptions, or climate shifts. Today, scientists warn that we are in the midst of a sixth mass extinction, and this time the cause is not an asteroid or a volcano. It is us.

**中文翻译：** 地球在其45亿年历史中经历了五次大规模灭绝事件。最著名的是6600万年前的白垩纪灭绝，消灭了恐龙和近75%的物种。这些事件均由自然灾难引起——小行星撞击、火山爆发或气候变迁。如今，科学家警告我们正处于第六次大规模灭绝之中，而这一次的原因不是小行星或火山。是我们。

**讲解：** 本段以历史对比开篇，将第六次灭绝置于地球历史的宏大背景中。*five mass extinction events* 和 *4.5-billion-year history* 用具体数据建立时间尺度。*wiped out the dinosaurs and nearly 75% of all species* 以白垩纪灭绝为参照，突出大规模灭绝的严重性。*Each... was caused by natural catastrophes* 列举三个自然原因，与最后的 *It is us* 形成强烈对比。最后两句以短句收束，*It is us* 仅三个词，却如锤击般有力，将全文的道德重量落在人类肩上。

:::callout{kind=note label=教材注释}
The "Big Five" mass extinctions are: Ordovician-Silurian (440 million years ago, 85% of species lost), Late Devonian (375 mya, 75%), Permian-Triassic (252 mya, 96% — the largest), Triassic-Jurassic (201 mya, 80%), and Cretaceous-Paleogene (66 mya, 76%). The current extinction rate is estimated to be 100-1,000 times the natural background rate.
:::

---

#### 段落 2

> The current extinction rate is staggering. According to the Intergovernmental Science-Policy Platform on Biodiversity and Ecosystem Services (IPBES), approximately one million species face extinction, many within decades. The primary drivers are well documented: habitat destruction, overexploitation, pollution, invasive species, and climate change. What makes the sixth extinction unique is not its causes — which are familiar — but its pace. Species that evolved over millions of years are disappearing in a single human lifetime, a speed that leaves no time for evolutionary adaptation.

**中文翻译：** 当前的灭绝速度令人震惊。据生物多样性和生态系统服务政府间科学政策平台（IPBES）统计，约一百万个物种面临灭绝，许多将在数十年内消失。主要驱动因素有充分记录：栖息地破坏、过度开发、污染、入侵物种和气候变化。使第六次灭绝独特的不是其原因——这些原因众所周知——而是其速度。在数百万年中进化而来的物种在一个人的一生中消失，这种速度没有给进化适应留下任何时间。

**讲解：** 本段以数据为核心，建立灭绝危机的紧迫感。*one million species face extinction* 是震撼性的数字。五个并列驱动因素 *habitat destruction, overexploitation, pollution, invasive species, and climate change* 以列举结构呈现。关键句 *What makes the sixth extinction unique is not its causes... but its pace* 用 *not... but...* 结构将焦点从原因转向速度。*Species that evolved over millions of years are disappearing in a single human lifetime* 用时间对比（数百万年 vs 一个人的一生）突出速度的惊人。*leaves no time for evolutionary adaptation* 解释了速度之所以致命的原因——进化无法跟上。

:::callout{kind=note label=教材注释}
The IPBES Global Assessment Report (2019) is the most comprehensive assessment of biodiversity ever conducted, compiled by 145 experts from 50 countries over three years. It found that the average abundance of native species in most major terrestrial ecosystems has fallen by at least 20% since 1900.
:::

---

#### 段落 3

> The consequences of biodiversity loss extend far beyond the disappearance of individual species. Ecosystems depend on complex webs of interaction, and when key species are removed, the effects cascade throughout the system. The loss of pollinators, such as bees and butterflies, threatens global food production. The disappearance of apex predators destabilizes entire food chains. The degradation of coral reefs, which support 25% of all marine species, endangers the livelihoods of 500 million people who depend on them. Biodiversity is not merely a matter of preserving beautiful animals — it is the infrastructure of life itself.

**中文翻译：** 生物多样性丧失的后果远远超越个别物种的消失。生态系统依赖于复杂的互动网络，当关键物种被移除时，影响会在整个系统中级联扩散。传粉昆虫（如蜜蜂和蝴蝶）的消失威胁着全球粮食生产。顶级捕食者的消失使整个食物链不稳定。珊瑚礁的退化——支撑着25%的海洋物种——危及5亿依赖它们的人的生计。生物多样性不仅仅是保护美丽动物的问题——它是生命本身的基础设施。

**讲解：** 本段阐述灭绝的系统性后果。*extend far beyond* 标志视角从个体到系统的转换。*complex webs of interaction* 和 *cascade throughout the system* 用网络和级联比喻描述生态系统的相互依赖性。三个具体例证（传粉昆虫→粮食生产、顶级捕食者→食物链、珊瑚礁→人类生计）以相同结构呈现，形成排比。关键句 *Biodiversity is not merely a matter of preserving beautiful animals — it is the infrastructure of life itself* 用 *not merely... it is...* 递进结构将生物多样性从审美问题提升为生存基础设施，是全文的核心论点。

---

#### 段落 4

> The question is not whether we can stop the sixth extinction — we cannot, for many species are already beyond saving. The question is whether we can slow it down enough to preserve the essential functions of Earth's ecosystems. This requires urgent action on multiple fronts: protecting remaining habitats, reducing carbon emissions, transforming agriculture and fishing practices, and combating the illegal wildlife trade. Each of these actions is challenging, but together they offer a path toward what scientists call "bending the curve" — slowing the rate of biodiversity loss and giving ecosystems time to adapt.

**中文翻译：** 问题不在于我们能否阻止第六次灭绝——我们不能，因为许多物种已经无法挽救。问题在于我们能否将其减缓到足以保存地球生态系统基本功能的程度。这需要在多个方面采取紧急行动：保护剩余栖息地、减少碳排放、转变农业和渔业实践、打击非法野生动物贸易。这些行动各自都充满挑战，但它们共同提供了一条通向科学家所称的"弯曲曲线"的道路——减缓生物多样性丧失的速度，给生态系统适应的时间。

**讲解：** 本段从问题转向解决方案。*The question is not whether... The question is whether...* 的对比结构先承认局限（无法阻止），再提出可行目标（减缓）。四个并列行动 *protecting... reducing... transforming... combating...* 构成多维度方案。*bending the curve*（弯曲曲线）是核心比喻——不是逆转灭绝趋势，而是减缓其速度。*giving ecosystems time to adapt* 呼应第二段的 *leaves no time for evolutionary adaptation*，形成首尾呼应。

---

#### 段落 5

> Ultimately, the sixth extinction is not just an environmental crisis — it is a moral one. It raises questions about what kind of species we want to be: one that destroys the web of life that sustains it, or one that learns to live in harmony with the natural world. The choices we make in the next few decades will determine not only the fate of millions of species but the legacy we leave for future generations. For the first time in Earth's history, a single species has the power to cause a mass extinction — and the power to prevent one. Whether we use that power wisely is the defining question of our age.

**中文翻译：** 最终，第六次灭绝不仅是环境危机——它是道德危机。它提出了关于我们想成为什么样的物种的问题：是破坏维持自身生存的生命之网的物种，还是学会与自然世界和谐共处的物种。我们在未来几十年做出的选择不仅将决定数百万物种的命运，还将决定我们留给后代的遗产。在地球历史上，单一物种首次拥有引发大规模灭绝的力量——也拥有阻止一次的力量。我们是否明智地使用这种力量，是我们这个时代的决定性问题。

**讲解：** 本段将环境问题提升为道德和哲学问题，是全文的思想升华。*not just an environmental crisis — it is a moral one* 用递进结构提升问题层次。设问句 *what kind of species we want to be* 将人类置于自我审视的位置。*one that destroys... or one that learns to live in harmony* 用 *or* 提供两条道路，形成道德选择。*a single species has the power to cause a mass extinction — and the power to prevent one* 用 *and* 连接两种力量，强调人类的双重角色——既是危机的制造者也是解决者。结尾 *the defining question of our age* 以最高级收束，赋予文章时代意义。

#### 长难句解析

:::parse{label=What makes the sixth extinction unique is not its causes — which are familiar — but its pace.}
**结构分析：** 主语为 *What makes the sixth extinction unique*（*What* 引导主语从句）；谓语为 *is not... but...*；*its causes* 和 *its pace* 为并列表语；*which are familiar* 为非限制性定语从句修饰 *causes*。

**翻译：** 使第六次灭绝独特的不是其原因——这些原因众所周知——而是其速度。

**考点：** *What* 引导主语从句；*not... but...* 并列表语结构；非限制性定语从句 *which are familiar*；破折号插入语。
:::

:::parse{label=Species that evolved over millions of years are disappearing in a single human lifetime, a speed that leaves no time for evolutionary adaptation.}
**结构分析：** 主语为 *Species*，后接 *that evolved over millions of years* 定语从句；谓语为 *are disappearing*；状语为 *in a single human lifetime*；*a speed that leaves no time for evolutionary adaptation* 为同位语，补充说明 *in a single human lifetime*，其中 *that leaves no time* 为定语从句修饰 *speed*。

**翻译：** 在数百万年中进化而来的物种在一个人的一生中消失，这种速度没有给进化适应留下任何时间。

**考点：** *that* 引导定语从句修饰 *Species*；时间对比 *millions of years* vs *a single human lifetime*；同位语 *a speed that...*；嵌套定语从句 *that leaves no time*。
:::

---

#### 主旨与写作手法

本文围绕"第六次大规模灭绝"展开，采用"历史背景→现状数据→系统性后果→解决方案→道德升华"的五段递进结构。写作手法上，作者善用对比（自然原因 vs 人类原因、数百万年 vs 一个人的一生）、数据（一百万物种、75%、25%）、列举（五个驱动因素、四个行动方案）和比喻（*infrastructure of life*、*bending the curve*、*web of life*）来构建论证。结尾将环境危机提升为道德选择，体现科普写作"从科学到伦理"的升华策略。

### Reading 2 · Climate Change and Human Migration

#### 段落 1

> Climate change is no longer a distant threat — it is a present reality, and one of its most visible consequences is the displacement of human populations. Rising sea levels, prolonged droughts, intensifying storms, and changing rainfall patterns are making some regions of the world increasingly uninhabitable. The World Bank estimates that, without urgent action, climate change could displace more than 200 million people by 2050. These climate migrants are not fleeing war or persecution — they are fleeing the environment itself.

**中文翻译：** 气候变化不再是遥远的威胁——它是当下的现实，而其最可见的后果之一是人口的流离失所。海平面上升、长期干旱、加剧的风暴和变化的降水模式正在使世界上一些地区日益不宜居住。世界银行估计，如果不采取紧急行动，到2050年气候变化可能导致超过2亿人流离失所。这些气候移民不是在逃避战争或迫害——他们是在逃离环境本身。

**讲解：** 本段以 *no longer a distant threat — it is a present reality* 的对比开篇，消除"气候变化是未来问题"的误解。四个并列原因 *rising sea levels, prolonged droughts, intensifying storms, and changing rainfall patterns* 以列举结构呈现气候变化的多种表现。*200 million people by 2050* 用具体数据建立规模感。关键句 *These climate migrants are not fleeing war or persecution — they are fleeing the environment itself* 用 *not... they are...* 结构重新定义"难民"概念——气候移民的驱动力不是政治而是环境。

:::callout{kind=note label=教材注释}
The World Bank's "Groundswell" report (2021) projects that climate change could lead to 216 million internal migrants by 2050 under a pessimistic scenario. The report identifies six regions at risk: Sub-Saharan Africa, South Asia, North Africa, Latin America, Eastern Europe and Central Asia, and East Asia and the Pacific. Currently, there is no international legal framework specifically protecting "climate refugees."
:::

---

#### 段落 2

> Climate migration takes many forms. In the Pacific Islands, rising seas are swallowing entire communities, forcing residents to relocate to higher ground or abandon their homelands altogether. In the Sahel region of Africa, desertification is destroying farmland, driving rural populations to cities that are already struggling to provide basic services. In Bangladesh, saltwater intrusion from rising seas is contaminating drinking water and destroying rice paddies, pushing farmers to migrate to urban centers. In each case, the pattern is the same: environmental degradation destroys livelihoods, and people move to survive.

**中文翻译：** 气候移民有多种形式。在太平洋岛屿，上升的海平面正在吞噬整个社区，迫使居民迁往高地或完全放弃家园。在非洲萨赫勒地区，沙漠化正在摧毁农田，驱使农村人口涌入已经在努力提供基本服务的城市。在孟加拉国，海水入侵正在污染饮用水并摧毁稻田，迫使农民迁移到城市中心。在每种情况下，模式都相同：环境退化摧毁生计，人们为了生存而迁移。

**讲解：** 本段以三个地理案例展示气候移民的多样性。太平洋岛屿（海平面上升）、萨赫勒（沙漠化）、孟加拉国（海水入侵）三个案例分别代表不同气候驱动因素。每个案例以相同结构呈现：地点→环境问题→对人的影响→迁移行为。最后的总结句 *the pattern is the same: environmental degradation destroys livelihoods, and people move to survive* 以冒号引出共性，将三个案例归纳为统一模式——环境退化→生计丧失→被迫迁移。

---

#### 段落 3

> The consequences of climate migration are complex and far-reaching. When large populations move, they place enormous pressure on host communities, straining infrastructure, housing, and resources. In some cases, climate migration can exacerbate existing social and political tensions, potentially leading to conflict. The International Organization for Migration warns that climate displacement could become one of the greatest humanitarian challenges of the twenty-first century. Yet climate migrants often fall through the cracks of international law, as the 1951 Refugee Convention does not recognize environmental factors as grounds for refugee status.

**中文翻译：** 气候移民的后果复杂而深远。当大量人口迁移时，他们对接收社区施加巨大压力，使基础设施、住房和资源紧张。在某些情况下，气候移民可能加剧现有的社会和政治紧张局势，可能导致冲突。国际移民组织警告说，气候流离失所可能成为二十一世纪最大的人道主义挑战之一。然而，气候移民往往落入国际法的缝隙，因为1951年难民公约不承认环境因素为难民身份的依据。

**讲解：** 本段分析气候移民的后果。*complex and far-reaching* 定调。*straining infrastructure, housing, and resources* 三个并列名词描述对接收社区的压力。*exacerbate existing social and political tensions, potentially leading to conflict* 揭示气候移民与冲突的潜在联系。关键句 *climate migrants often fall through the cracks of international law* 用 *fall through the cracks*（落入缝隙）比喻，指出法律保护的缺失。*the 1951 Refugee Convention does not recognize environmental factors as grounds for refugee status* 解释了法律缺失的具体原因，将问题从人道主义层面上升到法律制度层面。

:::callout{kind=note label=教材注释}
The 1951 Refugee Convention defines a refugee as someone who has a "well-founded fear of being persecuted for reasons of race, religion, nationality, membership of a particular social group, or political opinion." Environmental factors are not included. The term "climate refugee" is widely used in media but has no legal standing. Some countries, such as New Zealand, have explored creating special visa categories for people displaced by climate change.
:::

---

#### 段落 4

> Addressing climate migration requires both mitigation and adaptation. Mitigation means reducing the greenhouse gas emissions that drive climate change, thereby slowing the environmental changes that force people to move. Adaptation means helping vulnerable communities prepare for and cope with the impacts that are already inevitable — building seawalls, developing drought-resistant crops, and creating planned relocation programs for communities that cannot be saved. Both strategies are essential: mitigation addresses the root causes, while adaptation addresses the consequences. Neither alone is sufficient.

**中文翻译：** 应对气候移民需要减缓和适应两种策略。减缓意味着减少驱动气候变化的温室气体排放，从而减缓迫使人们迁移的环境变化。适应意味着帮助脆弱社区准备和应对已经不可避免的影响——建造海堤、开发抗旱作物、为无法挽救的社区创建有计划的搬迁方案。两种策略都是必要的：减缓针对根本原因，而适应针对后果。单独一种都不够。

**讲解：** 本段提出双轨解决方案。*both mitigation and adaptation* 以并列结构引出两个概念。*Mitigation means... Adaptation means...* 的平行定义结构清晰区分两者。三个适应措施 *building seawalls, developing drought-resistant crops, and creating planned relocation programs* 以列举结构呈现。关键句 *mitigation addresses the root causes, while adaptation addresses the consequences* 用 *while* 对比两者的功能定位。*Neither alone is sufficient* 以简短否定句收束，强调两者的互补性。

---

#### 段落 5

> The story of climate migration is ultimately a story about justice. Those who contribute least to climate change — the poorest people in the poorest nations — are often the ones most affected by its consequences. A farmer in Bangladesh produces a tiny fraction of the carbon emissions of an average American, yet it is the farmer who loses his land to rising seas. Addressing this injustice requires international cooperation on a scale humanity has never achieved: wealthy nations must reduce emissions dramatically, provide financial support for adaptation in vulnerable countries, and develop legal frameworks to protect those who are displaced. The alternative — a world where hundreds of millions of climate migrants have nowhere to go — is too dire to contemplate.

**中文翻译：** 气候移民的故事最终是一个关于正义的故事。对气候变化贡献最小的人——最贫穷国家中最贫穷的人——往往是其后果受影响最大的人。孟加拉国的一个农民产生的碳排放仅为一个普通美国人的一小部分，然而失去土地于海平面上升的却是这个农民。解决这一不公正需要人类从未实现过的国际合作的规模：富裕国家必须大幅减少排放，为脆弱国家的适应提供资金支持，并制定法律框架保护流离失所者。另一种选择——一个数亿气候移民无处可去的世界——太过可怕而不敢想象。

**讲解：** 本段将气候移民从环境问题提升为正义问题，是全文的思想升华。*ultimately a story about justice* 定调。核心对比 *Those who contribute least... are often the ones most affected* 揭示气候不公正的本质。具体例证 *A farmer in Bangladesh produces a tiny fraction... yet it is the farmer who loses his land* 用 *yet* 转折和 *it is... who...* 强调句式突出不公正。三个并列义务 *reduce emissions dramatically, provide financial support, and develop legal frameworks* 构成对富裕国家的行动要求。结尾 *too dire to contemplate* 以 *too... to...* 结构收束，用恐惧驱动紧迫感。

**考点：** *who* 引导定语从句修饰 *Those*；破折号内同位语；*the ones* 后接过去分词短语 *most affected by*；*contribute least* 与 *most affected* 的对比。

**结构分析：** 主语为 *The alternative*；破折号内 *a world where hundreds of millions of climate migrants have nowhere to go* 为同位语，其中 *where* 引导定语从句修饰 *world*；谓语为 *is*；表语为 *too dire to contemplate*（*too... to...* 结构）。

## 三、知识点总结

### 核心词汇对比

| 词汇 | 释义 | 课文语境 |
|---|---|---|
| *extinction* | 灭绝 | 第六次大规模灭绝 |
| *degradation* | 退化 | 环境退化迫使移民 |
| *displacement* | 流离失所 | 气候导致人口流离失所 |
| *irreversible* | 不可逆转 | 不可逆转的环境变化 |
| *resilience* | 韧性 | 建立气候韧性 |
| *sustainable* | 可持续的 | 可持续发展 |

### 核心概念

- **第六次大规模灭绝（sixth mass extinction）**：由人类活动驱动的当前生物多样性危机，速度远超自然背景灭绝率。
- **气候移民（climate migration）**：因气候变化导致的环境恶化而被迫迁移的人口，目前缺乏国际法律保护。
- **减缓与适应（mitigation and adaptation）**：应对气候变化的两大策略——减缓针对原因（减少排放），适应针对后果（调整基础设施和行为）。
- **气候正义（climate justice）**：气候变化对贡献最小的贫困人口影响最大的不公正现象，需要国际合作解决。
- **弯曲曲线（bending the curve）**：减缓生物多样性丧失速度的策略，虽不能逆转趋势但可为生态系统争取适应时间。

### 语法要点

- **What 引导主语从句**：*What makes the sixth extinction unique is not... but...*（Reading 1 段落2）
- **not... but... 并列表语**：*not its causes... but its pace*（Reading 1 段落2）
- **同位语 + 定语从句嵌套**：*a speed that leaves no time for evolutionary adaptation*（Reading 1 段落2）
- **it is... who... 强调句式**：*it is the farmer who loses his land*（Reading 2 段落5）
- **too... to... 结构**：*too dire to contemplate*（Reading 2 段落5）

### 主题升华

两篇课文从不同维度诠释了"自然与人类社会"这一单元主题。Reading 1 聚焦**生物多样性危机**——第六次大规模灭绝是人类活动对自然世界的毁灭性影响；Reading 2 聚焦**气候移民**——气候变化对人类社会的直接冲击和正义问题。两者共同揭示：人类与自然不是分离的两个世界，而是相互依存的统一体。破坏自然就是破坏人类自身的生存基础；保护自然不仅是环境责任，更是道德义务和正义要求。我们既是地球上唯一能够引发大规模灭绝的物种，也是唯一能够阻止它的物种——这一双重力量定义了我们时代的责任。
