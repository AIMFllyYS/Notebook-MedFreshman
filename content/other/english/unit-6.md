## 二、课文精讲

### Reading 1 · Alone Together

#### 段落 1

> We live in an age of unprecedented connectivity. Smartphones, social media, and instant messaging allow us to reach anyone, anywhere, at any time. Yet beneath this surface of constant connection, a quieter story is unfolding. Sherry Turkle, a psychologist at MIT, calls it "alone together" — a state in which we are increasingly connected to devices and disconnected from each other. We text during dinner, scroll during conversations, and check notifications during meetings. The very technology designed to bring us closer, Turkle argues, is pushing us apart.

**中文翻译：** 我们生活在一个前所未有的互联时代。智能手机、社交媒体和即时通讯让我们可以随时随地联系任何人。然而，在这种持续连接的表象之下，一个更安静的故事正在展开。麻省理工学院心理学家雪莉·特克尔称之为"群体性孤独"——一种我们越来越多地与设备连接、却与彼此断开的状态。我们在晚餐时发短信，在交谈时刷手机，在会议中查看通知。特克尔认为，正是旨在拉近我们距离的技术，正在将我们推开。

**讲解：** 本段以对比手法开篇：*unprecedented connectivity*（前所未有的互联）与 *alone together*（群体性孤独）形成强烈反差。*beneath this surface*（在这层表象之下）暗示深层问题。Turkle 的概念 *alone together* 是全文核心——"在一起却各自孤独"。三个并列短句 *We text during dinner, scroll during conversations, and check notifications during meetings* 以具体行为刻画现代人的数字习惯。最后一句 *The very technology designed to bring us closer... is pushing us apart* 用 *designed to* 与 *is pushing* 的对比收束，点明悖论。

:::callout{kind=note label=教材注释}
Sherry Turkle is the Abby Rockefeller Mauzé Professor of the Social Studies of Science and Technology at MIT. Her book "Alone Together: Why We Expect More from Technology and Less from Each Other" (2011) is a foundational text in the study of technology's impact on human relationships. She argues that technology offers the "illusion of companionship without the demands of friendship."
:::

---

#### 段落 2

> Turkle's research reveals a troubling pattern. In interviews with hundreds of families, she found that children often feel competing with a parent's phone for attention. "My dad texts during my soccer games," one teenager reported. "He's physically there, but he's not really there." This phrase — "physically there, but not really there" — captures the essence of the problem. Physical proximity no longer guarantees emotional presence. We can be in the same room yet inhabit different worlds, each absorbed in our own screen.

**中文翻译：** 特克尔的研究揭示了一个令人不安的模式。在对数百个家庭的访谈中，她发现孩子们常常觉得自己在与父母的手机争夺注意力。"我爸爸在我足球比赛时发短信，"一个青少年报告说。"他人在那里，但他并不真正在那里。"这句话——"人在那里，但并不真正在那里"——抓住了问题的本质。物理上的接近不再保证情感上的在场。我们可以在同一个房间却身处不同的世界，各自沉浸在自己的屏幕中。

**讲解：** 本段以具体访谈数据支撑论点。*competing with a parent's phone for attention*（与父母的手机争夺注意力）将抽象问题具象化。引语 *physically there, but not really there* 是段落核心金句，以简洁的对比概括了数字时代的在场危机。*Physical proximity no longer guarantees emotional presence* 是对引语的学术提炼，*no longer guarantees* 暗示这是一种新现象。最后一句 *inhabit different worlds, each absorbed in our own screen* 用 *inhabit*（居住）一词将屏幕世界比作独立的生活空间。

---

#### 段落 3

> The consequences extend beyond family dynamics. In the workplace, constant connectivity erodes the boundary between work and personal life. Employees feel pressure to respond to emails and messages at all hours, leading to burnout and diminished job satisfaction. Among friends, superficial digital interactions — likes, emojis, and brief comments — increasingly replace deeper forms of communication. We mistake the frequency of contact for the quality of connection, confusing the number of messages with the depth of understanding.

**中文翻译：** 后果超越了家庭动态。在职场中，持续的连接侵蚀了工作与个人生活之间的边界。员工感到必须在所有时间回复邮件和消息的压力，导致倦怠和工作满意度下降。在朋友之间，表面的数字互动——点赞、表情符号和简短评论——越来越多地取代了更深层的交流形式。我们将联系的频率误认为连接的质量，将消息的数量与理解的深度混为一谈。

**讲解：** 本段将问题从家庭扩展到职场和友谊，使用层递结构。*erodes the boundary*（侵蚀边界）中的 *erodes* 暗示渐进的破坏。*superficial digital interactions*（表面的数字互动）中三个并列名词 *likes, emojis, and brief comments* 具体列举了浅层互动的形式。关键句 *We mistake the frequency of contact for the quality of connection* 是段落核心论点，*mistake A for B*（将A误认为B）揭示了认知错误。*confusing the number of messages with the depth of understanding* 以平行结构强化论点。

:::callout{kind=note label=教材注释}
Research by Jean Twenge at San Diego State University found that adolescents who spend more than three hours per day on social media are 35% more likely to report symptoms of depression. The study, published in 2017, analyzed data from over 500,000 teenagers and linked the rise of smartphone use to declining mental health among young people.
:::

---

#### 段落 4

> Turkle is not anti-technology. She acknowledges that digital tools have made life more convenient, more efficient, and more accessible in countless ways. Her concern is not with technology itself but with the way we let it mediate our most important relationships. The solution, she argues, is not to abandon our devices but to develop more mindful habits — creating tech-free zones in our homes, setting aside dedicated time for face-to-face conversation, and teaching children that some moments are too important to interrupt with a notification.

**中文翻译：** 特克尔并不反对技术。她承认数字工具在无数方面使生活更加便利、高效和可及。她关注的不是技术本身，而是我们让它介入最重要关系的方式。她认为，解决方案不是放弃我们的设备，而是培养更有意识的习惯——在家中创建无科技区域，留出专门的面对面交谈时间，并教导孩子有些时刻太重要了，不应被通知打断。

**讲解：** 本段平衡了批判与建设性建议。*not anti-technology* 先消除误解，*acknowledges* 体现公正态度。*not with technology itself but with the way we let it mediate* 用 *not... but...* 结构将焦点从技术转向使用方式。三个并列建议 *creating tech-free zones... setting aside dedicated time... teaching children...* 构成行动方案，从空间→时间→教育三个层面递进。*too important to interrupt* 以不定式表结果，强调某些时刻的不可侵犯性。

---

#### 段落 5

> Ultimately, Turkle's message is a call for intentionality. Technology is a powerful tool, but it is a tool that should serve human needs, not the other way around. By choosing when to connect and when to disconnect, when to text and when to talk, we can reclaim the genuine connections that make life meaningful. The goal is not to choose between the digital and the physical, but to integrate them in a way that honors our deepest need: to be truly present with one another.

**中文翻译：** 最终，特克尔传递的信息是一种对有意识生活的呼唤。技术是强大的工具，但它应该是服务于人类需求的工具，而非反过来。通过选择何时连接、何时断开，何时发短信、何时交谈，我们可以重新获得让生活有意义的真实连接。目标不是在数字和物理之间二选一，而是以尊重我们最深需求——真正与彼此同在——的方式将两者整合。

**讲解：** 本段是全文总结和升华。*a call for intentionality*（对有意识的呼唤）是核心主张。*not the other way around*（而非反过来）强调技术应服务于人而非控制人。两组 *when to... and when to...* 的对比结构将抽象理念转化为具体选择。*not to choose between... but to integrate* 用 *not... but...* 结构提出整合而非对立的解决方案。结尾 *to be truly present with one another* 以不定式短语收束，将全文的批判升华为人文关怀。

#### 长难句解析

:::parse{label=The very technology designed to bring us closer, Turkle argues, is pushing us apart.}
**结构分析：** 主语为 *The very technology*（*very* 作形容词表强调，意为"正是"）；*designed to bring us closer* 为过去分词短语作后置定语修饰 *technology*；*Turkle argues* 为插入语；谓语为 *is pushing*；宾语为 *us apart*。

**翻译：** 特克尔认为，正是旨在拉近我们距离的技术，正在将我们推开。

**考点：** *the very + 名词* 表强调；过去分词短语 *designed to* 作后置定语；插入语 *Turkle argues*；*push apart*（推开）与 *bring closer*（拉近）形成反义对比。
:::

:::parse{label=We mistake the frequency of contact for the quality of connection, confusing the number of messages with the depth of understanding.}
**结构分析：** 主句为 *We mistake the frequency of contact for the quality of connection*；*confusing the number of messages with the depth of understanding* 为现在分词短语作伴随状语，补充说明主句内容。

**翻译：** 我们将联系的频率误认为连接的质量，将消息的数量与理解的深度混为一谈。

**考点：** *mistake A for B*（将A误认为B）；现在分词 *confusing* 作伴随状语；*confuse A with B*（将A与B混淆）；*frequency* 与 *quality*、*number* 与 *depth* 的对比。
:::

#### 主旨与写作手法

本文围绕"技术如何影响人际关系"展开，采用"悖论呈现→具体证据→后果扩展→建设性建议→价值升华"的五段递进结构。写作手法上，作者善用对比（*connected/disconnected*、*physically there/not really there*、*bring closer/push apart*）、引用（青少年访谈、Turkle 理论）、并列结构（三个 *We text/scroll/check*、三个建议）和悖论修辞（*alone together*）来构建论证。结尾以 *intentionality*（有意识）为核心主张，体现批判性写作"提出问题—分析原因—给出方案"的经典策略。

### Reading 2 · The Digital Divide

#### 段落 1

> While much of the world takes internet access for granted, a significant portion of humanity remains unconnected. According to the International Telecommunication Union, approximately 2.6 billion people — roughly one-third of the global population — have never used the internet. This gap between the connected and the unconnected is known as the "digital divide," and its consequences extend far beyond the inconvenience of being unable to stream videos or check social media.

**中文翻译：** 当世界大部分地区将互联网接入视为理所当然时，仍有相当一部分人类处于未连接状态。据国际电信联盟统计，约26亿人——大约全球人口的三分之一——从未使用过互联网。这种已连接者与未连接者之间的差距被称为"数字鸿沟"，其后果远远超出了无法观看视频或查看社交媒体的不便。

**讲解：** 本段以全球数据开篇，将读者从个人体验拉到全球视角。*takes internet access for granted*（将互联网视为理所当然）与 *remains unconnected* 形成对比。*2.6 billion people* 和 *one-third of the global population* 用具体数据震撼读者。*digital divide*（数字鸿沟）是核心概念。*extend far beyond the inconvenience* 用递进结构暗示问题的严重性——不是"不便"而是"根本性不平等"。

:::callout{kind=note label=教材注释}
The International Telecommunication Union (ITU) is the United Nations' specialized agency for information and communication technologies. Its 2023 report estimated that 2.6 billion people remain offline. The digital divide is most severe in least developed countries, where only 36% of the population has internet access, compared to 89% in developed countries.
:::

---

#### 段落 2

> The digital divide is not a single gap but a complex web of inequalities. It separates rich from poor, urban from rural, young from old, and educated from uneducated. In developing nations, the cost of devices and data plans puts internet access out of reach for millions. In rural areas, the physical infrastructure — fiber-optic cables, cell towers, reliable electricity — simply does not exist. And even where infrastructure is available, digital literacy — the skills needed to use technology effectively — remains a significant barrier.

**中文翻译：** 数字鸿沟不是单一的差距，而是一张复杂的不平等之网。它将富人与穷人、城市与农村、年轻与年老、受过教育与未受教育者分隔开来。在发展中国家，设备和数据套餐的成本使数百万人无法接入互联网。在农村地区，物理基础设施——光缆、基站、可靠的电力——根本不存在。而即使在基础设施可用的地方，数字素养——有效使用技术所需的技能——仍然是一个重大障碍。

**讲解：** 本段以层递结构剖析数字鸿沟的多维性。*not a single gap but a complex web* 用 *not... but...* 结构强调复杂性。四组对比 *rich/poor, urban/rural, young/old, educated/uneducated* 以排比呈现鸿沟的多个维度。随后从经济（*cost of devices*）、物理（*infrastructure*）、技能（*digital literacy*）三个层面分析原因，形成层递。*simply does not exist* 中的 *simply* 强调基础设施的完全缺失。*even where... remains a significant barrier* 用让步结构引出第三层障碍。

---

#### 段落 3

> The consequences of the digital divide are profound. In an era when essential services — banking, healthcare, education, government services — increasingly move online, those without internet access are effectively excluded from full participation in society. Children without home internet cannot complete online homework assignments, falling behind their connected peers. Job seekers without digital skills cannot apply for positions that require online applications. Patients without internet access cannot use telemedicine services that have become crucial, especially since the COVID-19 pandemic.

**中文翻译：** 数字鸿沟的后果是深远的。在一个银行、医疗、教育、政务服务日益线上化的时代，那些没有互联网接入的人实际上被排斥在社会的全面参与之外。没有家庭互联网的儿童无法完成在线作业，落后于有网络连接的同龄人。没有数字技能的求职者无法申请需要在线申请的职位。没有互联网接入的患者无法使用远程医疗服务，而这些服务自新冠疫情以来变得至关重要。

**讲解：** 本段以四个并列例证展示数字鸿沟的具体后果。*essential services* 后列举四个领域 *banking, healthcare, education, government services*，以破折号插入。*effectively excluded from full participation* 是段落核心论点——数字鸿沟导致社会排斥。三个具体例证（儿童作业、求职申请、远程医疗）以相同结构呈现：*Without X, Y cannot Z*，形成排比，增强说服力。*especially since the COVID-19 pandemic* 将问题与当代事件连接，增强现实感。

:::callout{kind=note label=教材注释}
The COVID-19 pandemic dramatically exposed the digital divide. When schools closed and moved to online learning in 2020, an estimated 1.3 billion children lacked access to home internet or devices for remote education. This "homework gap" disproportionately affected low-income and rural students, widening existing educational inequalities.
:::

---

#### 段落 4

> Bridging the digital divide requires action on multiple fronts. Governments must invest in digital infrastructure, particularly in underserved rural and remote areas. Schools and community centers can provide public internet access and digital literacy training. Technology companies can develop more affordable devices and data plans. And international organizations can coordinate efforts to ensure that the benefits of digital technology reach all of humanity, not just the privileged few.

**中文翻译：** 弥合数字鸿沟需要在多个方面采取行动。政府必须投资数字基础设施，特别是在服务不足的农村和偏远地区。学校和社区中心可以提供公共互联网接入和数字素养培训。科技公司可以开发更实惠的设备和数据套餐。国际组织可以协调各方努力，确保数字技术的惠益惠及全人类，而不仅仅是少数特权者。

**讲解：** 本段从问题转向解决方案。*Bridging the digital divide*（弥合数字鸿沟）中的 *bridging* 比喻生动——鸿沟需要桥梁。四个并列主语 *Governments / Schools and community centers / Technology companies / international organizations* 构成多主体行动方案，从国家→社区→企业→国际逐层扩大。*not just the privileged few* 与 *all of humanity* 形成对比，呼应第一段的全球视角。

---

#### 段落 5

> The digital divide is ultimately a question of justice, not technology. In a world where internet access determines one's ability to learn, work, and participate in civic life, being disconnected is not merely an inconvenience — it is a form of marginalization. Closing this gap is not just about laying cables and distributing devices; it is about ensuring that every person has the opportunity to benefit from the digital revolution. As the United Nations has declared, internet access is a basic human right, essential to the realization of other human rights in the twenty-first century.

**中文翻译：** 数字鸿沟最终是一个正义问题，而非技术问题。在一个互联网接入决定一个人学习、工作和参与公民生活能力的世界里，断开连接不仅仅是不便——它是一种边缘化。弥合这一差距不仅仅是铺设光缆和分发设备；它关乎确保每个人都有机会从数字革命中受益。正如联合国所宣布的，互联网接入是一项基本人权，对二十一世纪实现其他人权至关重要。

**讲解：** 本段将数字鸿沟从技术问题提升为正义问题，是全文的思想升华。*a question of justice, not technology* 用 *not...* 结构翻转认知框架。*being disconnected is not merely an inconvenience — it is a form of marginalization* 中的 *not merely... it is...* 递进结构与 Reading 1 的核心句式呼应。*not just about laying cables... it is about ensuring* 再次用 *not just... it is about...* 结构，从物质层面上升到权利层面。引用联合国宣言 *internet access is a basic human right* 赋予论点权威性，以最高规格收束全文。

#### 长难句解析

:::parse{label=In an era when essential services — banking, healthcare, education, government services — increasingly move online, those without internet access are effectively excluded from full participation in society.}
**结构分析：** *In an era* 为时间状语；*when essential services increasingly move online* 为定语从句修饰 *era*；破折号内 *banking, healthcare, education, government services* 为 *essential services* 的同位语；主句为 *those without internet access are effectively excluded from full participation in society*。

**翻译：** 在一个银行、医疗、教育、政务服务日益线上化的时代，那些没有互联网接入的人实际上被排斥在社会的全面参与之外。

**考点：** *when* 引导定语从句修饰名词 *era*；破折号内同位语；*those without...* 的代词 + 介词短语结构；*be excluded from*（被排斥在……之外）。
:::

:::parse{label=Closing this gap is not just about laying cables and distributing devices; it is about ensuring that every person has the opportunity to benefit from the digital revolution.}
**结构分析：** 分号连接两个并列分句。第一分句主语为动名词短语 *Closing this gap*，谓语为 *is not just about*，宾语为 *laying cables and distributing devices*（两个动名词并列）。第二分句主语为 *it*（指代 *closing this gap*），谓语为 *is about*，宾语为 *ensuring that every person has the opportunity to benefit from the digital revolution*（*that* 引导宾语从句）。

**翻译：** 弥合这一差距不仅仅是铺设光缆和分发设备；它关乎确保每个人都有机会从数字革命中受益。

**考点：** 动名词短语作主语；*not just about... it is about...* 并列递进结构；*that* 引导宾语从句；*benefit from*（从……中受益）。
:::

---

#### 主旨与写作手法

本文围绕"数字鸿沟"展开，采用"全球现状→多维分析→后果展示→解决方案→价值升华"的五段递进结构。写作手法上，作者善用数据（26亿人、三分之一人口）、排比（四组对比、三个 *Without X, Y cannot Z*、四个行动主体）、递进结构（*not merely... it is...* / *not just... it is about...*）和引用（联合国宣言）来构建论证。结尾将数字鸿沟从技术问题提升为正义和人权问题，体现科普写作"从数据到价值"的升华策略。

## 三、知识点总结

### 核心词汇对比

| 词汇 | 释义 | 课文语境 |
|---|---|---|
| *authentic* | 真实的 | 真实的人际连接 |
| *diminish* | 减弱、降低 | 技术降低交流深度 |
| *engage* | 参与、投入 | 全身心投入对话 |
| *isolate* | 孤立 | 技术使人孤立 |
| *marginalize* | 边缘化 | 数字鸿沟边缘化群体 |
| *participation* | 参与 | 社会全面参与 |

### 核心概念

- **群体性孤独（alone together）**：Sherry Turkle 提出的概念，指人们在数字时代与设备连接却与彼此断开的状态。
- **数字鸿沟（digital divide）**：已连接者与未连接者之间的差距，涉及经济、地理、教育和技能多个维度。
- **数字素养（digital literacy）**：有效使用数字技术所需的技能，是数字鸿沟的重要维度之一。
- **有意识使用（intentionality）**：Turkle 提出的解决方案核心——有意识地选择何时连接、何时断开。
- **数字包容（digital inclusion）**：确保所有人都能获得并有效使用数字技术，不仅是接入设备，更是全面参与社会生活。

### 语法要点

- **过去分词作后置定语**：*technology designed to bring us closer*（Reading 1 段落1）
- **现在分词作伴随状语**：*confusing the number of messages with the depth of understanding*（Reading 1 段落3）
- **插入语**：*Turkle argues*（Reading 1 段落1）
- **定语从句修饰时间名词**：*an era when essential services increasingly move online*（Reading 2 段落3）
- **not merely/just... it is... 递进结构**：两篇课文均使用

### 主题升华

两篇课文从不同维度诠释了"技术与人际连接"这一单元主题。Reading 1 聚焦**技术对亲密关系的侵蚀**——群体性孤独是连接时代的悖论；Reading 2 聚焦**技术不平等导致的社会排斥**——数字鸿沟是连接时代的正义问题。两者共同揭示：技术的价值不在于连接本身，而在于连接的质量和包容性。真正的连接不是更多的消息和通知，而是更深的理解和更广的参与。
