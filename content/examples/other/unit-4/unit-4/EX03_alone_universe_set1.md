## 阅读原文

> Since the dawn of civilization, humans have looked up at the stars and wondered: are we alone? For most of history, this question belonged to philosophy and religion. In the twentieth century, however, it became a scientific one. With the development of radio telescopes and space probes, humanity began its first serious search for extraterrestrial intelligence — an endeavor that combines astronomy, biology, engineering, and a generous dose of optimism.
>
> The numbers are certainly encouraging. Our Milky Way galaxy contains between 100 and 400 billion stars, and current estimates suggest that a significant fraction of these stars host planets. Of these exoplanets, astronomers have identified dozens that orbit within the habitable zone — the region around a star where temperatures allow liquid water to exist. Given these figures, the probability that at least one of these planets harbors life seems overwhelmingly high. As the astronomer Carl Sagan famously put it, "The universe is a pretty big place. If it's just us, it seems like an awful waste of space."
>
> In 1961, astronomer Frank Drake proposed an equation to estimate the number of detectable civilizations in our galaxy. The Drake Equation multiplies together factors such as the rate of star formation, the fraction of stars with planets, the number of habitable planets per star system, the probability of life evolving, the probability of intelligence emerging, the probability of communicative technology developing, and the expected lifespan of a civilization. While we can now estimate some of these factors with reasonable confidence, others remain pure speculation. The result is a number that ranges from one (we are alone) to millions.

---

**1.** What is the main idea of the passage?

A. The Drake Equation proves that millions of civilizations exist in our galaxy.

B. The search for extraterrestrial intelligence has evolved from philosophy to science, supported by encouraging data.

C. Carl Sagan was the first astronomer to search for extraterrestrial life.

D. The Milky Way galaxy contains too many stars to study effectively.

**Answer: B**

**解析**（主旨大意题）：第一段叙述了从哲学到科学的转变，第二段以数据论证搜索的合理性，第三段介绍德雷克方程作为理论框架。A项"proves"过于绝对——原文说结果"ranges from one to millions"，并非确证；C项"first astronomer"无依据，萨根只是被引用了一句名言；D项"too many to study"与原文基调相反，原文说数字"encouraging"。B项概括了三段的核心逻辑：历史转变 + 数据支撑。

---

**2.** According to the passage, the "habitable zone" refers to

A. the area of a galaxy where stars are closest together

B. the region around a star where liquid water can exist on a planet's surface

C. the zone of a planet where temperatures are suitable for human habitation

D. the distance between Earth and its nearest neighboring star

**Answer: B**

**解析**（细节理解题）：第二段明确将habitable zone定义为"the region around a star where temperatures allow liquid water to exist"。A项"stars closest together"与定义无关；C项"for human habitation"偷换概念——宜居带指行星可能存在液态水，不等同于人类可直接居住；D项"distance between stars"与原文无关。B项与原文定义完全一致。

---

**3.** The word "speculation" in the phrase "others remain pure speculation" (Paragraph 3) most likely means

A. careful observation

B. established fact

C. guessing without sufficient evidence

D. experimental verification

**Answer: C**

**解析**（词义猜测题）：第三段将德雷克方程的因子分为两类——"estimate some of these factors with reasonable confidence"与"others remain pure speculation"，形成对比。既然前者是"有合理信心可估算的"，后者则应是缺乏证据的猜测。A项"careful observation"与B项"established fact"都与"reasonable confidence"一侧重合，不构成对比；D项"experimental verification"更不是"speculation"的含义。C项"guessing without sufficient evidence"准确对应。

**4.** What can be inferred from Carl Sagan's quote "If it's just us, it seems like an awful waste of space"?

A. Sagan believed that Earth is the only planet with life in the universe.

B. Sagan was criticizing the waste of resources on space exploration.

C. Sagan found it implausible that a universe so vast would contain only one intelligent species.

D. Sagan was arguing that humans should colonize other planets to avoid wasting space.

**Answer: C**

**解析**（推理判断题）：萨根的名言出现在讨论银河系包含数千亿恒星、数十颗宜居带行星之后，作为"the probability... seems overwhelmingly high"的佐证。"awful waste of space"暗示宇宙如此之大，若只有人类一种智慧生命，那这些空间就"浪费"了——即他认为宇宙中很可能存在其他智慧生命。A项与萨根的观点恰恰相反；B项"criticizing waste of resources"曲解了"waste of space"的含义，此处指宇宙空间的"浪费"而非资源浪费；D项"colonize other planets"在原文中无依据。C项正确推断出萨根的言外之意。

---

**5.** The author's attitude toward the search for extraterrestrial intelligence is best described as

A. dismissive and skeptical

B. cautiously optimistic

C. hostile and critical

D. completely neutral

**Answer: B**

**解析**（观点态度题）：作者称搜索地外智慧是"an endeavor that combines astronomy, biology, engineering, and a generous dose of optimism"，又说"The numbers are certainly encouraging"，但同时也承认德雷克方程中部分因子是"pure speculation"，结果"ranges from one to millions"。这种既肯定数据支撑又承认不确定性的态度，属于"cautiously optimistic"（谨慎乐观）。A项"dismissive"与"encouraging"矛盾；C项"hostile"与原文基调完全不符；D项"completely neutral"忽略了作者明显的正面倾向。B项最为准确。
