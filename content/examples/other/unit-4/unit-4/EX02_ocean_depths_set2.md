## 阅读原文

> Bioluminescence is another marvel of the deep sea. In the darkness of the midnight zone, where no sunlight reaches, many organisms produce their own light through chemical reactions within their bodies. This living glow serves multiple purposes: it can lure prey, confuse predators, communicate with mates, or serve as a camouflage strategy called counter-illumination. Scientists estimate that over 90% of deep-sea organisms are bioluminescent, making it one of the most common forms of communication on the planet — yet it remains largely unstudied.
>
> Exploring this realm requires extraordinary technology. Submersibles must withstand crushing pressure, and their instruments must operate in total darkness and near-freezing temperatures. Remotely operated vehicles (ROVs) and autonomous underwater vehicles (AUVs) have become essential tools, deploying high-resolution cameras, robotic arms, and sophisticated sensors to map the seafloor and collect specimens. Each dive brings the possibility of discovering species entirely new to science — and sometimes entirely new ways of living. The deep ocean, it turns out, is not merely a place to study; it is a frontier that challenges our very definition of what life can be.
>
> For centuries, the ocean depths were a realm of myth and imagination. Sailors spoke of sea monsters and bottomless pits, while scientists believed the deep ocean was a barren void, devoid of life and light. Today, we know better — yet the truth is stranger than any legend. The deep sea is a world of breathtaking diversity, where creatures thrive under conditions that would instantly destroy most life on the surface. It is, quite simply, the largest habitat on Earth and the least explored.

**1.** Which of the following best summarizes the three paragraphs?

A. The history of deep-sea exploration from myth to modern science.

B. The biological, technological, and philosophical dimensions of deep-sea exploration.

C. The dangers of exploring the deep ocean with submersibles.

D. The role of bioluminescence in deep-sea ecosystems.

**Answer: B**

**解析**（主旨大意题）：第一段聚焦生物发光（biological dimension），第二段讨论潜水器和ROV/AUV技术（technological dimension），第三段将深海定位为"frontier that challenges our very definition of what life can be"（philosophical dimension）。A项"history"只覆盖第三段，不全面；C项"dangers"只是第二段的次要信息；D项只覆盖第一段。B项从三个维度概括三段内容，最为全面。

---

**2.** According to the passage, counter-illumination is a strategy by which deep-sea organisms

A. produce light to attract mates during mating season

B. use light to eliminate their silhouette and blend in with ambient light from above

C. emit toxic chemicals to repel predators

D. create bright flashes to temporarily blind approaching predators

**Answer: B**

**解析**（细节理解题）：第一段将counter-illumination归类为"a camouflage strategy"，即伪装策略。虽然文中未详细展开其机制，但"counter-illumination"一词本身意为"反照明"，即用自身发光来抵消来自上方的微弱光线，消除自身阴影。A项"attract mates"对应的是"communicate with mates"，而非counter-illumination；C项"toxic chemicals"在文中无依据；D项"blind predators"对应的是"confuse predators"，但机制不同。B项准确描述了反照明的伪装功能。

---

**3.** The phrase "teem with" in the sentence "the deep ocean teems with organisms" (Paragraph 3) is closest in meaning to

A. struggle against

B. compete with

C. be full of

D. adapt to

**Answer: C**

**解析**（词义猜测题）：原句"the deep ocean teems with organisms that have evolved extraordinary adaptations"出现在转折结构"Yet far from being a lifeless wasteland"之后，意在强调深海并非无生命，而是充满生物。因此"teem with"意为"充满"。A项"struggle against"与语境矛盾——后文说的是生物繁盛而非挣扎；B项"compete with"偏离原意；D项"adapt to"是后文"evolved extraordinary adaptations"的内容，非"teem with"的含义。C项"be full of"为正确同义替换。

**4.** What can be inferred from the statement "Each dive brings the possibility of discovering species entirely new to science — and sometimes entirely new ways of living"?

A. Most deep-sea species have already been catalogued by scientists.

B. The deep ocean is so well-explored that few new discoveries remain.

C. Our current knowledge of deep-sea biodiversity is still very limited.

D. New species are only found near hydrothermal vents.

**Answer: C**

**解析**（推理判断题）：句中"each dive brings the possibility"暗示每次潜水都可能发现新物种，说明当前认知非常有限。第一段也提到"it remains largely unstudied"，第三段称深海为"the least explored" habitat。A项与原文相反——如果大多数物种已被编目，就不会"each dive"都有新发现；B项与"least explored"直接矛盾；D项"only found near hydrothermal vents"过于绝对，原文未做此限制。C项是合理推断。

**5.** The author's primary purpose in writing this passage is to

A. warn readers about the dangers of deep-sea exploration

B. argue that deep-sea exploration should receive more funding

C. convey the wonder and scientific significance of the deep ocean

D. compare deep-sea exploration with space exploration

**Answer: C**

**解析**（写作目的题）：全文以"marvel""breathtaking diversity""frontier"等词营造惊叹感，同时通过化能合成、生物发光等科学发现展示深海的学术价值。A项"warn about dangers"不是文章基调；B项"argue for more funding"虽有隐含之意，但原文未直接提出资金呼吁；D项"compare with space exploration"在本文中未出现。C项"convey the wonder and scientific significance"最准确地概括了作者的写作目的。
