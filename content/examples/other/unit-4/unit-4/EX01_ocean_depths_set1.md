## 阅读原文

> For centuries, the ocean depths were a realm of myth and imagination. Sailors spoke of sea monsters and bottomless pits, while scientists believed the deep ocean was a barren void, devoid of life and light. Today, we know better — yet the truth is stranger than any legend. The deep sea is a world of breathtaking diversity, where creatures thrive under conditions that would instantly destroy most life on the surface. It is, quite simply, the largest habitat on Earth and the least explored.
>
> Sunlight penetrates only the upper 200 meters of the ocean. Below this zone, called the sunlight zone or epipelagic zone, lies a world of perpetual darkness. As one descends further, the pressure increases by one atmosphere for every 10 meters — reaching over 1,000 times atmospheric pressure at the deepest points. Temperatures hover near freezing. Yet far from being a lifeless wasteland, the deep ocean teems with organisms that have evolved extraordinary adaptations to survive and even flourish in this harsh environment.
>
> One of the most revolutionary discoveries in deep-sea biology came in 1977, when scientists exploring the Galápagos Rift found hydrothermal vents — cracks in the seafloor from which superheated, mineral-rich water gushes into the cold ocean. Around these vents, they found thriving communities of giant tube worms, clams, and shrimp — all living in complete darkness, at temperatures and pressures that would kill most surface organisms. The key to their survival was chemosynthesis: a process by which bacteria convert chemicals from the vent water into energy, forming the base of a food web entirely independent of sunlight.

**1.** What is the main idea of the passage?

A. The deep ocean is a barren void devoid of any life forms.

B. Hydrothermal vents were discovered by accident in 1977.

C. The deep sea, once thought lifeless, is a diverse habitat that challenges our understanding of life.

D. Bioluminescence is the most common form of communication among deep-sea organisms.

**Answer: C**

**解析**（主旨大意题）：文章第一段先指出深海曾被误认为荒芜虚空，随后以"Today, we know better"转折，引出深海是"a world of breathtaking diversity"和"the largest habitat on Earth and the least explored"。第二段描述极端环境，第三段以热液喷口和化能合成为例证。A项与全文核心论点相反；B项只是第三段的一个细节，不能概括全文主旨；D项在原文中未提及（生物发光在第四段才出现，且本文引用的前三段未涉及）。C项准确概括了全文从"旧观念"到"新认知"的递进主旨。

---

**2.** According to the passage, how deep can sunlight penetrate into the ocean?

A. About 100 meters.

B. About 200 meters.

C. About 1,000 meters.

D. About 10,935 meters.

**Answer: B**

**解析**（细节理解题）：第二段首句明确指出"Sunlight penetrates only the upper 200 meters of the ocean"。A项100米在文中未提及；C项1,000米是午夜带（bathypelagic zone）的起始深度，非阳光穿透深度；D项10,935米是马里亚纳海沟挑战者深渊的深度，与阳光穿透无关。B项与原文完全对应。

---

**3.** The word "barren" in the phrase "a barren void" (Paragraph 1) most likely means

A. extremely dark

B. extremely deep

C. lacking life or vegetation

D. difficult to measure

**解析**（词义猜测题）：根据上下文，"barren void"与"devoid of life and light"并列使用，且后文以"Yet far from being a lifeless wasteland"进行转折反驳，可知"barren"与"lifeless"同义。A项"dark"虽与深海有关，但"void"本身已含"空"之意，"barren"修饰的是生命层面；B项"deep"与上下文逻辑不符；D项"difficult to measure"无上下文依据。C项"lacking life or vegetation"与"devoid of life"和"lifeless"形成同义对应，为正确答案。

**Answer: C**

---

**4.** What can be inferred about the discovery of hydrothermal vent communities in 1977?

A. It confirmed that all ocean life depends on sunlight for energy.

B. It was the first time scientists had explored the Galápagos Rift.

C. It challenged the prevailing belief that life requires sunlight.

D. It proved that giant tube worms can survive in cold ocean water.

**Answer: C**

**解析**（推理判断题）：第三段指出热液喷口生物的生存依赖"chemosynthesis"——"a process by which bacteria convert chemicals from the vent water into energy, forming the base of a food web entirely independent of sunlight"。教材注释也提到"Before the 1977 discovery, scientists believed all life on Earth ultimately depended on photosynthesis"。由此可推断，这一发现挑战了"生命必须依赖阳光"的传统观念。A项与原文相反；B项"first time"在文中无依据，文中只说科学家"exploring"该裂谷；D项偷换概念，管虫生存于热液喷口附近的高温高压环境，而非普通冷水。C项为合理推断。

**5.** What is the author's attitude toward deep-sea exploration?

A. Indifferent and detached.

B. Skeptical and cautious.

C. Fascinated and forward-looking.

D. Critical and dismissive.

**Answer: C**

**解析**（观点态度题）：全文使用"breathtaking diversity""marvel""revolutionary discoveries"等积极词汇描绘深海，结尾称其为"a frontier that challenges our very definition of what life can be"，体现出作者对深海探索的浓厚兴趣和前瞻性视野。A项"indifferent"与全文的热情笔调矛盾；B项"skeptical"不符，作者并未质疑深海探索的价值；D项"critical and dismissive"与原文完全相反。C项"fascinated and forward-looking"准确反映作者态度。
