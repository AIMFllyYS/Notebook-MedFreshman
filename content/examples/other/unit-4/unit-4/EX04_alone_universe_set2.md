:::example{label=EX04 · Are We Alone in the Universe? — Set 2}

## 阅读原文

> In 1974, scientists used the Arecibo radio telescope in Puerto Rico to transmit a message into space aimed at the globular star cluster M13. The message contained information about our solar system, the chemical elements of DNA, a human figure, and the radio telescope itself. It will take 25,000 years to reach its destination and 25,000 more for any reply to return — a timeline that humbles our sense of urgency. Yet the Arecibo message was a symbolic milestone: humanity's first deliberate attempt to announce its existence to the cosmos.
>
> This leads to what is known as Fermi's paradox: if the probability of extraterrestrial intelligence is so high, why have we found no evidence of it? Proposed solutions range from the plausible to the fantastic. Perhaps intelligent civilizations are so far apart that their signals have not yet reached us. Perhaps they exist but have no desire to communicate. Perhaps, more unsettlingly, civilizations tend to destroy themselves before they can achieve interstellar communication. Or perhaps — as some scientists speculate — life itself is far rarer than the Drake Equation suggests, and we may be the only intelligent species in our galaxy, at least for now.
>
> Whether we are alone or not, the search itself has value. It drives technological innovation, deepens our understanding of life's origins, and forces us to reflect on humanity's place in the cosmos. The instruments built to listen for alien signals have also led to discoveries about pulsars, quasars, and the structure of the universe. And perhaps most importantly, the search for extraterrestrial intelligence reminds us that our own planet, with its fragile biosphere and its remarkable diversity of life, is a treasure worth protecting — whether or not anyone else is out there to notice.

---

**1.** What is the main idea of the passage?

A. The Arecibo message was a waste of scientific resources.

B. Fermi's paradox proves that extraterrestrial civilizations do not exist.

C. The search for extraterrestrial intelligence, despite the paradox of no evidence, has intrinsic value for humanity.

D. The Milky Way galaxy is too large for meaningful interstellar communication.

**Answer: C**

**解析**（主旨大意题）：第一段叙述阿雷西博信息作为里程碑，第二段提出费米悖论及多种解释，第三段以"Whether we are alone or not, the search itself has value"收束，强调搜索的内在价值。A项"waste of resources"与原文称其为"symbolic milestone"矛盾；B项"proves... do not exist"与原文"Perhaps... life itself is far rarer"的推测语气矛盾；D项"too large for meaningful communication"虽在第一段提及时间跨度，但不是全文主旨。C项准确概括了三段的递进逻辑。

---

**2.** According to the passage, how long will it take for the Arecibo message to reach its destination and for a reply to return?

A. 25,000 years.

B. 50,000 years.

C. 100,000 years.

D. The passage does not specify.

**Answer: B**

**解析**（细节理解题）：第一段明确指出"It will take 25,000 years to reach its destination and 25,000 more for any reply to return"，即25000年到达 + 25000年返回 = 50000年。A项只计算了单程；C项100000年在文中无依据；D项与原文矛盾。B项50000年为正确计算结果。

---

**3.** The word "humbles" in the phrase "a timeline that humbles our sense of urgency" (Paragraph 1) most likely means

A. increases

B. validates

C. makes something seem small or insignificant by comparison

D. accelerates

**Answer: C**

**解析**（词义猜测题）：上下文中，50000年的通信周期与人类日常的紧迫感形成巨大反差。"humbles our sense of urgency"意为让我们的紧迫感显得微不足道。A项"increases"方向相反——时间跨度不是增加而是削弱了紧迫感；B项"validates"（证实）不合逻辑；D项"accelerates"（加速）与语境矛盾。C项"makes something seem small or insignificant by comparison"准确捕捉了"humbles"在此处的修辞含义。

---

**4.** Which of the following is NOT mentioned in the passage as a possible explanation for Fermi's paradox?

A. Civilizations are too far apart for their signals to have reached us.

B. Extraterrestrial civilizations exist but choose not to communicate.

C. Civilizations tend to self-destruct before achieving interstellar communication.

D. Earth's magnetic field blocks alien signals from reaching us.

**Answer: D**

**解析**（推理判断题）：第二段列举了四种解释：①距离太远（A项）；②无意交流（B项）；③自我毁灭（C项）；④生命本身极为稀有。D项"Earth's magnetic field blocks alien signals"在原文中完全未提及，属于无中生有。A、B、C三项均在原文中有明确对应。因此D项为答案。

---

**5.** What is the author's ultimate message in the final paragraph?

A. The search for extraterrestrial intelligence is futile and should be abandoned.

B. The primary benefit of the search is the technological discoveries it produces.

C. The search for extraterrestrial intelligence ultimately reminds us to value and protect our own planet.

D. We should focus on colonizing other planets rather than searching for alien life.

**Answer: C**

**解析**（观点态度题/写作目的题）：最后一段以"perhaps most importantly"引出核心论点——搜索地外智慧"reminds us that our own planet... is a treasure worth protecting"。A项"futile"与全文基调矛盾；B项"primary benefit"不准确——技术发现只是附带收益，作者用"also"引出，而"most importantly"留给环保意识；D项"colonizing other planets"在原文中完全未提及。C项准确概括了作者在结尾处从宇宙回归地球的价值升华。
