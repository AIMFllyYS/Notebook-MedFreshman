**阅读原文**（关键段落摘录）

> **Para 5:** The history of entrepreneurship speaks of its widespread power and significance, inside and outside of the marketplace. Our lifetime has seen the cultural imprint of entrepreneurs like Steve Jobs — and the compassionate reach of social pioneers like Run Run Shaw. Their legacies are different, but the root of their success is the same: They see what others don't; they do what others won't; and they keep pushing when prudence says quit.
>
> **Para 6:** While there is more to entrepreneurship, these three behaviors are deeply ingrained in every pioneer's approach. The primary (and ultimately defining) characteristic of an entrepreneur is a deep sense of responsibility to humanity. There are some who breathe it and there are others who lack it. They are positioned at opposite ends of the entrepreneurial spectrum. You determine where your story falls.
>
> **Para 7:** This broader view does not imply everyone is an entrepreneur. It implies everyone can become one regardless of their background, occupation, or socioeconomic status. This does not require plans of global impact either. Most of us would celebrate a day without stress or a week without regret. But don't we want more than survival?
>
> **Para 9:** Listen to the voices of today's entrepreneur musicians, professors, actors, businessmen, mothers and fathers, sons and daughters. They implore you to join their cause or break new ground of your own. You are powerful, they say. You can make a difference. Ultimately, they are calling for the entrepreneurial spirit within each of us. In response, some write a check or read a book. Others step out and revolutionize their life and the lives of those around them. The latter are the flourishing ones.
>
> **Para 10:** Applying an entrepreneurial approach to all of life is not automatic, nor is it as simple as joining a good cause, though it can start there. It usually requires you to reshape rigid attitudes and reconsider old habits. These are painstaking tasks for sure, but this is definitely a more responsible way of living than settling for mediocrity. The business of living is not alien. The tools of innovation and impact are within reach of us every day. Practiced enough, a life-wide entrepreneurial approach becomes second nature. That's when the momentous can happen.

**Question 1.** According to Para 5, what do Steve Jobs and Run Run Shaw have in common despite having different legacies?

A. They both founded technology companies that revolutionized global communication.

B. They both prioritized charitable work over commercial success in their careers.

C. They shared the same root of success: seeing what others miss, doing what others avoid, and persisting beyond conventional limits.

D. They were both motivated primarily by financial reward rather than a sense of social responsibility.

**Answer: C**

**解析**：细节理解题。Para 5 原文："Their legacies are different, but the root of their success is the same: They see what others don't; they do what others won't; and they keep pushing when prudence says quit."

- **A 错误**：邵逸夫（Run Run Shaw）是香港电影业和慈善业的先驱，并非科技公司创始人；原文也未将两人都归类为科技企业家。
- **B 错误**：原文没有说两人都"优先考虑慈善而非商业"；史蒂夫·乔布斯以商业创新著称，并非以慈善为主。
- **C 正确**：与原文三个排比句直接对应——看见别人看不见的、做别人不愿做的、在审慎之声说放弃时坚持前行。
- **D 错误**：Para 6 明确指出真正的企业家特征是"a deep sense of responsibility to humanity"，而非财务动机。

**Question 2.** In Para 6, what does the author identify as the most defining characteristic of a true entrepreneur?

A. The ability to generate maximum profit for shareholders and investors.

B. An exceptional talent for identifying untapped market opportunities.

C. A deep sense of responsibility to humanity.

D. The courage to take financial risks that others are unwilling to accept.

**Answer: C**

**解析**：细节理解题。Para 6 原文："The primary (and ultimately defining) characteristic of an entrepreneur is a deep sense of responsibility to humanity."

- **A 错误**：原文没有将利润最大化列为定义性特征。
- **B 错误**：市场机会识别能力是企业家的技能之一，但原文给出的"定义性特征"是责任感，而非能力。
- **C 正确**：与原文 "a deep sense of responsibility to humanity" 精确对应，且原文用了"primary (and ultimately defining)"来强调其首要地位。
- **D 错误**：原文没有将"财务风险承担"列为定义性特征，虽然这通常是人们对企业家的刻板印象。

**Question 3.** The word "ingrained" in Para 6 ("these three behaviors are deeply ingrained in every pioneer's approach") most probably means ______.

A. temporarily adopted as a short-term strategy

B. firmly established and difficult to change

C. recently introduced as part of a modern business curriculum

D. visible only to those with specialized training in entrepreneurship

**Answer: B**

**解析**：词义猜测题。"ingrained"（根深蒂固的）指深深植根于某人思维或行为中、难以改变的特质。"deeply ingrained"是强调用法，语境中指这三种行为已成为每位先驱者方式的核心组成部分。

- **A 错误**："temporarily adopted"（临时采用）与 ingrained 的"根深蒂固、持久"含义相反。
- **B 正确**："firmly established and difficult to change"（牢固确立、难以改变）是 ingrained 的精确释义。
- **C 错误**："recently introduced"（最近引入）与 deeply ingrained（深深根植）在时间和程度上均相矛盾。
- **D 错误**："only visible to specialists"与原文语境无关，ingrained 描述行为特质，不涉及可见性。

**Question 4.** What does the author most likely mean by "The latter are the flourishing ones" at the end of Para 9?

A. Writers and book readers tend to achieve greater financial success than other types of people.

B. Those who actively transform their lives and communities through action are the ones who truly thrive.

C. People who donate money to causes are more successful than those who only engage intellectually.

D. Entrepreneurs who follow established business models are more likely to flourish than those who innovate.

**Answer: B**

**解析**：推理判断题。Para 9 中，"the latter"指代对比中的第二组人——"Others step out and revolutionize their life and the lives of those around them"（踏出去、彻底改变自己和周围人生活的人）；相对于第一组（写支票或读书的人）。作者认为真正行动、付诸改变的人才是"flourishing ones"（蓬勃发展的人）。

- **A 错误**：原文对"写书"或"读书"没有财务成功的讨论；the latter 指的是 "revolutionize their life" 的人，不特指作家。
- **B 正确**：与原文逻辑对应——通过行动改变自己和周围人生活的人，才是真正蓬勃发展的人。
- **C 错误**："write a check"（写支票/捐款）是第一组（former），而非 the latter；捐款者并未被说是更成功的。
- **D 错误**：原文没有讨论"跟随成熟商业模式"与"创新"的成功率对比。

**Question 5.** Which statement best summarizes the author's central message in paragraphs 9 and 10?

A. Reading books and donating to worthy causes are the most effective ways to develop an entrepreneurial mindset.

B. An entrepreneurial approach to life is innate and cannot be taught or developed through deliberate practice.

C. Living entrepreneurially requires active effort to overcome rigid habits, but the results are a more meaningful and impactful life.

D. The entrepreneurial spirit is ultimately reserved for those who have already achieved professional or financial success.

**Answer: C**

**解析**：综合判断题。Para 10 的核心信息：采用创业方式并非自动发生（"not automatic"），需要重塑僵化态度、重新审视旧习惯（"painstaking tasks"），但这是比安于平庸更负责任的生活方式；练习足够多，就会成为第二天性，"momentous"的事情才会发生。

- **A 错误**：Para 9 将"写支票或读书"与"步出去彻底改变"对比，暗示前者不够，真正的改变需要行动。
- **B 错误**：Para 10 明确说明创业精神可以通过练习成为"second nature"（第二天性）——说明它是可习得的。
- **C 正确**：概括了 Para 10 的完整论点——需要主动努力（克服僵化习惯）+ 结果有意义（比平庸更负责任的生活 + 重大时刻的发生）。
- **D 错误**：Para 7 明确指出创业精神对所有人开放，"regardless of their background, occupation, or socioeconomic status"——并非成功者的专利。
