**阅读原文**（关键段落摘录）

> **Para 2:** Game theory is everywhere, and you do not have to be an economist to understand its most insightful aspects. In game theory, there's a fundamental concept — the Nash equilibrium (named after the economist John Nash). What is it then? And how is it applied in the real world?
>
> **Para 3:** The definition of the Nash equilibrium is straightforward: It is a situation in which all people are using a Nash strategy. What is a Nash strategy? Simply the aim of doing what is best for yourself, taking as a given what others around you are doing. In economic terms, you are "best responding" to other people's actions in a purely individual and self-interested way.
>
> **Para 4:** So far so simple, but you may wonder how this leads to anything insightful. This is where the prisoner's dilemma comes in. In *A Beautiful Mind*, a movie inspired by John Nash's life story, Russell Crowe pronounces this memorable quote: "Adam Smith was wrong!" Well, in classical economic theory, Adam Smith stated that individuals, by pursuing their own interests, were maximising the collective welfare of society as a whole, a notion that developed throughout the allegory of an "invisible hand." The beauty of the Nash equilibrium is precisely that it shows how acting rationally from an individual's point of view can lead to collectively undesirable (or even disastrous) outcomes.
>
> **Para 5:** To illustrate this, we use the extremely famous example of the prisoner's dilemma. Imagine two burglars who are being accused of robbery by the police. The police have no evidence but decide to take both burglars to separate rooms and give them the chance to confess to their crime. They cannot communicate with each other in order to decide whether to confess or not. Each of the burglars knows the following: a) If both of them confess, they each get 5 years of prison; b) If none of them confesses, they each get 1 year of prison; c) If one of them confesses and the other does not, the confessor gets 0 year of prison and the non-confessor gets 10 years of prison.
>
> **Para 6-7:** Clearly in this situation, the collectively optimal outcome for burglars is for nobody to confess. However, no matter what the other burglar does, your best response is to confess. Since both burglars reason in the same way, they will both end up confessing, which leads to the Nash equilibrium: Both burglars confess no matter what the other burglar does, and they both end up getting 5 years of prison. Both of them would in fact be better off if they agreed not to confess, but their personal temptations make this agreement infeasible.

**Question 1.** What is the main purpose of this article?

A. To prove that Adam Smith's economic theory has been completely discredited by modern economists.

B. To illustrate how the Nash equilibrium explains situations where individual rational behavior leads to collectively suboptimal outcomes.

C. To argue that game theory should replace classical economics as the dominant framework for policy-making.

D. To demonstrate that cooperation is always the best strategy in competitive situations.

**Answer: B**

**解析**：本题考查主旨大意。文章以纳什均衡为核心，通过囚徒困境等例子说明"个人理性不等于集体最优"。

- **A 错误**：文章并没有说亚当·斯密的理论被"完全推翻"，而是指出其局限性——并非总成立。
- **B 正确**：与全文核心论点直接对应——纳什均衡揭示了个体理性行为导致集体次优结果的机制。
- **C 错误**：文章没有主张用博弈论"取代"古典经济学，二者是互补关系。
- **D 错误**：文章的结论恰恰相反——在囚徒困境中，个体理性使合作难以实现，对应选项"always"过于绝对。

**Question 2.** According to Para 5, what happens to a burglar who confesses while the other remains silent?

A. He receives the same sentence as his partner.

B. He is sentenced to 5 years in prison.

C. He is released with no prison time at all.

D. He receives a 1-year sentence as a reward for honesty.

**Answer: C**

**解析**：本题为细节理解题。Para 5 明确列出情况 c)："If one of them confesses and the other does not, the confessor gets 0 year of prison."

- **A 错误**：认罪者与不认罪者刑期截然不同（0年 vs. 10年），不相同。
- **B 错误**：5年是两人都认罪的结果，非一方认罪一方不认罪的情况。
- **C 正确**：与原文 "the confessor gets 0 year of prison" 直接对应。
- **D 错误**：原文没有提到"1年作为诚实奖励"，1年是双方都不认罪的结果。

**Question 3.** The word "infeasible" in Para 7 ("their personal temptations make this agreement infeasible") most probably means ______.

A. illegal and punishable by law

B. impossible to achieve or put into practice

C. morally unacceptable to society

D. highly risky but potentially rewarding

**Answer: B**

**解析**：本题为词义猜测题。"infeasible" = in-（否定）+ feasible（可行的），即"不可行的"。语境是：虽然双方都不认罪的协议对彼此更有利，但个人诱惑使这一协议"无法实现"。

- **A 错误**：原文没有讨论协议的法律性，"illegal"不符合语境。
- **B 正确**："impossible to achieve or put into practice"（难以实现）与 infeasible 含义精确吻合。
- **C 错误**："morally unacceptable"（道德上不可接受）在此语境下不相关；问题是实现机制，不是道德评判。
- **D 错误**："highly risky but potentially rewarding"与原文中"个人诱惑破坏协议可行性"的含义无关。

**Question 4.** What can be inferred about the Nash equilibrium from the prisoner's dilemma example?

A. It always results in the worst possible outcome for all parties involved.

B. It is a situation where each party's strategy is optimal given the other party's strategy, even if the collective result is not ideal.

C. It can only be reached when both parties agree to cooperate beforehand.

D. It is unstable and will eventually break down as parties learn to communicate.

**Answer: B**

**解析**：本题为推理判断题。根据 Para 3 的定义和 Para 6-7 的论证，纳什均衡是"每个人在给定他人策略的前提下做出对自己最优的选择"——即使集体结果不理想（各5年而非各1年）。

- **A 错误**："always results in the worst possible outcome"过于绝对——在囚徒困境中，最坏结果是10年，而均衡结果是5年，并非"最坏"。
- **B 正确**：精确概括了纳什均衡的定义：在给定他方策略的前提下，自身策略是最优的，但集体结果可能次优。
- **C 错误**：纳什均衡的关键恰恰是无需事先协商，每个人独立理性推理就会趋向均衡。
- **D 错误**：原文没有说均衡会因沟通而崩溃；实际上，正因为缺乏沟通约束，均衡才稳定。

**Question 5.** What is the author's attitude toward the application of game theory to real-world problems?

A. Skeptical — the author believes game theory is too abstract to explain real-world behavior.

B. Enthusiastic but uncritical — the author praises game theory without acknowledging its limitations.

C. Analytical and constructive — the author uses game theory to diagnose social problems and point toward solutions.

D. Pessimistic — the author concludes that collective action problems are impossible to solve.

**Answer: C**

**解析**：本题考查作者观点态度。文章从囚徒困境出发，分析了体育兴奋剂、气候政策等现实问题（诊断），并在结尾提出"legally binding agreements and communication"作为解决途径（建议）。

- **A 错误**：作者恰恰认为博弈论对现实世界极具解释力（"game theory is everywhere"）。
- **B 错误**：作者并非无批判地赞美，而是指出博弈论揭示了理性行为的局限。
- **C 正确**：文章在揭示问题（个体理性 → 集体次优）的同时，提出了解决框架（约束性协议），态度是分析性且建设性的。
- **D 错误**：结尾段指出"legally binding agreements and communication are generally the way to overcome the problem"，并不悲观。
