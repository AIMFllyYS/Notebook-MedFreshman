**阅读原文**（关键段落摘录）

> **Para 8:** The reason why this example is so famous is that it relates to many real-life phenomena in which individualistic behaviors lead to negative outcomes for society. In sports, all professional athletes would be better off if no one used performance-enhancing drugs. The problem is that when no one uses these drugs, an individual athlete might be tempted to use them in order to perform better than everyone else (the equivalent of confessing when the other burglar does not in the prisoner's dilemma). This then leads many other athletes to consume performance-enhancing drugs, and any athlete who decides not to consume these drugs would suffer from a major comparative disadvantage.
>
> **Para 9:** This also applies to environmental policies regarding CO₂ emissions. The world as a whole needs to stop climate change, but each country individually has an economic interest in emitting CO₂ for their industrial production, which leads to global inaction. You can think by yourself about the other examples I introduced: women having to put on makeup because everyone else does, students looking for internships because everyone else will have one to show on their CV. Examples are everywhere, and sometimes they entail inefficiencies that are extremely frustrating to economists.
>
> **Para 10:** The concept of the Nash equilibrium allows us to understand why some society issues require particular forms of intervention if we want to reach desirable outcomes. Legally binding agreements and communication are generally the way to overcome the problem caused by personal incentives in situations such as global warming. Of course, this is much easier said than done in a global context of economic competition where no country wants to go first in depriving themselves of polluting for production.

**Question 1.** What is the central idea of paragraphs 8 and 9?

A. Game theory proves that athletes and countries are fundamentally dishonest and will always cheat.

B. Individual self-interest, when acted upon by all parties, can produce outcomes that harm everyone in the group.

C. Environmental policy failures are caused primarily by a lack of scientific understanding of climate change.

D. Social norms such as wearing makeup and seeking internships are irrational and should be abandoned.

**Answer: B**

**解析**：本题考查主旨大意。Para 8-9 列举了体育兴奋剂、气候变化、化妆与实习三个例子，共同说明：当个体的自利行为被所有人复制时，结果对所有人都有害——这正是囚徒困境逻辑在现实中的体现。

- **A 错误**：文章说的是个体理性选择（而非"诚实"问题），运动员使用兴奋剂不是不诚实，而是个体理性的结果。
- **B 正确**：精确对应这两段的核心逻辑，即"个体最优 → 集体次优"。
- **C 错误**：文章分析的是激励结构（incentive structure），而非科学认知问题。
- **D 错误**：作者并未评判这些行为应该被"抛弃"，只是指出它们是博弈逻辑的产物，有时带来低效。

**Question 2.** According to Para 8, why might an athlete be tempted to use performance-enhancing drugs even when no one else is using them?

A. Because the rules of the sport explicitly permit the use of such substances in certain conditions.

B. Because using drugs would allow the athlete to gain an advantage over competitors who are not using them.

C. Because the athlete believes performance-enhancing drugs have no health risks.

D. Because sports governing bodies actively encourage athletes to seek competitive advantages.

**Answer: B**

**解析**：细节理解题。Para 8 原文：当没有人使用兴奋剂时，一名运动员"might be tempted to use them in order to perform better than everyone else"——即利用别人不用兴奋剂的时机，通过使用来获得超越所有人的优势。

- **A 错误**：原文没有提到规则许可，情境是规则之外的个人选择。
- **B 正确**：与原文 "perform better than everyone else" 直接对应——在别人不使用时，使用者可获得额外竞争优势。
- **C 错误**：原文没有讨论运动员对健康风险的看法。
- **D 错误**：原文没有提到体育机构鼓励此类行为，恰恰相反。

**Question 3.** The phrase "comparative disadvantage" in Para 8 most probably means ______.

A. a failure to meet the absolute minimum performance standard required for competition

B. a weaker position relative to others who have chosen a different strategy

C. a legal penalty imposed on athletes who refuse to undergo drug testing

D. a psychological feeling of inferiority unrelated to actual performance outcomes

**Answer: B**

**解析**：词义猜测题。"comparative disadvantage"（比较劣势）是经济学和竞争分析中的标准术语，指相对于选择不同策略的他方而处于劣势。语境：不使用兴奋剂的运动员相对于使用者处于劣势。

- **A 错误**："absolute minimum standard"描述的是绝对标准，而 comparative 强调的是相对关系。
- **B 正确**："a weaker position relative to others"（相对于他人处于更弱的位置）精确对应 comparative disadvantage 的含义。
- **C 错误**：原文没有提到法律处罚，disadvantage 指竞争层面的劣势，非法律惩罚。
- **D 错误**："心理上的自卑感"是主观感受，而 comparative disadvantage 指客观竞争地位的劣势。

**Question 4.** Based on Para 9 and 10, what does the author suggest is necessary to solve problems caused by the prisoner's dilemma in international contexts?

A. Each country should independently set more ambitious emission targets without waiting for others.

B. International scientific bodies should be given the power to override national governments on environmental matters.

C. Legally binding agreements combined with open communication are needed to align individual incentives with collective goals.

D. Economic competition should be eliminated so that countries can cooperate freely without strategic concerns.

**Answer: C**

**解析**：推理判断题。Para 10 原文："Legally binding agreements and communication are generally the way to overcome the problem caused by personal incentives in situations such as global warming."

- **A 错误**：Para 10 指出"no country wants to go first"，说明单边行动在现实中难以实现，不是作者推荐的方案。
- **B 错误**：原文没有提到赋予国际科学机构凌驾于国家政府之上的权力。
- **C 正确**：与原文 "Legally binding agreements and communication" 直接对应，这是作者明确给出的解决方向。
- **D 错误**："消除经济竞争"是过于理想化且与原文无关的论断；原文承认竞争是现实，并在此前提下探讨约束机制。

**Question 5.** What does the author's final remark — "this is much easier said than done" — reveal about his view on solving collective action problems?

A. The author is confident that legally binding agreements will eventually solve all global coordination failures.

B. The author dismisses binding agreements as ineffective and calls for a completely different approach.

C. The author acknowledges that while the theoretical solution is clear, real-world implementation faces significant political obstacles.

D. The author believes that economic competition is a greater obstacle than political will in addressing global warming.

**Answer: C**

**解析**：观点态度题。"Easier said than done"（说起来容易做起来难）是承认现实障碍的习语。结合语境——任何国家都不愿率先限制自身的工业排放——作者表达的是：解决方案（约束性协议）在理论上清晰，但在现实政治经济环境下实施极为困难。

- **A 错误**：作者并没有表示"乐观信心"，"easier said than done"明显传递了保留和现实主义的态度。
- **B 错误**：作者没有"否定"约束性协议，只是承认其推行困难。
- **C 正确**：准确捕捉了作者的态度——理论解法已知，现实执行受制于各国政治经济博弈。
- **D 错误**：原文强调的是"no country wants to go first"（政治意愿问题），而非纯粹的经济竞争；且"更大障碍"的比较在文中没有依据。
