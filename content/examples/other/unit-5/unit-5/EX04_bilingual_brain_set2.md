:::example{label=EX04 · The Bilingual Brain — Set 2}

## 阅读原文

> Perhaps even more remarkably, bilingualism appears to offer protection against cognitive decline in old age. Studies have found that bilingual patients develop symptoms of dementia an average of four to five years later than monolinguals — a delay more effective than any known drug. Researchers believe this is because the enhanced executive function built up over a lifetime of bilingualism creates a "cognitive reserve" — a buffer that helps the brain compensate for age-related deterioration.
>
> The implications of these findings extend far beyond individual health. In an increasingly globalized world, bilingualism is an asset not only for communication but for cognitive resilience. Schools that promote bilingual education are not merely teaching a second language — they are strengthening their students' minds. And in a world where mental sharpness is increasingly valued, the bilingual brain may be one of our most powerful tools for navigating the complexities of modern life.
>
> Critics caution, however, that the cognitive benefits of bilingualism are not unlimited. The advantages are most pronounced in people who use both languages regularly and in diverse contexts. Passive knowledge of a second language — the ability to understand but rarely speak it — may not produce the same neurological benefits. Furthermore, the severity of cognitive decline depends on many factors, including genetics, education, and lifestyle, of which bilingualism is only one.

---

**1.** According to Paragraph 1, bilingualism delays dementia symptoms by an average of

A. one to two years
B. two to three years
C. four to five years
D. ten or more years

**Answer: C**

**解析**（细节理解题）：第一段明确指出"bilingual patients develop symptoms of dementia an average of four to five years later than monolinguals"。A、B、D项的数字均与原文不符。C项与原文完全一致。

---

**2.** The term "cognitive reserve" refers to

A. the stored vocabulary of a second language

B. a buffer of cognitive abilities that helps compensate for brain deterioration

C. the reserved intelligence that monolinguals possess

D. a medical treatment for dementia patients

**Answer: B**

**解析**（词义猜测题）：第一段将"cognitive reserve"定义为"a buffer that helps the brain compensate for age-related deterioration"。A项将概念缩小为词汇量；C项与原文矛盾（cognitive reserve 来自双语经验）；D项是药物治疗，与原文"more effective than any known drug"的对比矛盾。B项与原文定义一致。

---

**3.** What does the author suggest about bilingual education in Paragraph 2?

A. It is a waste of educational resources.

B. It only benefits students who plan to work internationally.

C. It strengthens students' minds beyond just teaching a second language.

D. It should replace all monolingual education programs.

**Answer: C**

**解析**（推理判断题）：第二段指出"Schools that promote bilingual education are not merely teaching a second language — they are strengthening their students' minds"。A项与原文精神相反；B项"only benefits"过于狭隘；D项"replace all"过于绝对，原文未建议。C项准确反映了原文的递进结构 *not merely... they are...*。

---

**4.** According to Paragraph 3, which of the following is true about the cognitive benefits of bilingualism?

A. The benefits are unlimited regardless of how languages are used.

B. Passive knowledge of a second language produces the same benefits as active use.

C. Regular and diverse use of both languages maximizes the cognitive benefits.

D. Bilingualism is the single most important factor in preventing cognitive decline.

**Answer: C**

**解析**（细节理解题）：第三段指出"The advantages are most pronounced in people who use both languages regularly and in diverse contexts"，且"Passive knowledge... may not produce the same neurological benefits"。A项"unlimited"与原文"not unlimited"矛盾；B项与原文矛盾；D项"single most important factor"与原文"bilingualism is only one"矛盾。C项与原文完全一致。

---

**5.** The author's overall tone in this passage can best be described as

A. enthusiastic and uncritical
B. balanced and evidence-based
C. skeptical and dismissive
D. emotional and subjective

**Answer: B**

**解析**（观点态度题）：作者在第一、二段呈现双语益处的科学证据，第三段引入批评者的审慎观点，指出益处的局限性。这种既展示证据又承认局限的写法体现平衡、基于证据的态度。A项"uncritical"与第三段矛盾；C项"dismissive"与前两段矛盾；D项"emotional"与全文科学语气不符。B项 *balanced and evidence-based* 准确描述作者态度。
