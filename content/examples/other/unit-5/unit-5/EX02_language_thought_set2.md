## 阅读原文

> Critics of the Sapir-Whorf hypothesis argue that the effects of language on thought are modest and limited to specific domains. They point out that bilinguals can easily translate between languages, which suggests that thoughts exist independently of any particular language. Moreover, pre-linguistic infants and non-human animals demonstrate sophisticated cognitive abilities — recognizing faces, solving problems, and navigating space — without possessing language at all. If thought requires language, how could infants and animals think?
>
> The current consensus among researchers is more nuanced than either extreme. Language does not imprison thought, but it does bias it. The categories and distinctions encoded in our language subtly influence what we notice, how we categorize information, and what we remember — but they do not determine what we can think. As the linguist Roman Jakobson famously put it: "Languages differ essentially in what they must convey, not in what they may convey." In other words, language shapes the default settings of our attention, but we can always override those settings when we choose to.
>
> The practical implications of linguistic relativity extend beyond academic debate. In education, understanding how language shapes thought can help teachers design more effective multilingual curricula. In international relations, awareness of linguistic differences can foster cross-cultural communication and reduce misunderstandings. And in technology, designers of artificial intelligence must consider how the language of their systems shapes the decisions those systems make.

---

**1.** According to the critics in Paragraph 1, which of the following supports the idea that thought is independent of language?

A. Infants cannot recognize faces without language.

B. Bilinguals can translate between languages easily.

C. Animals lack any cognitive abilities.

D. Language is essential for problem-solving.

**Answer: B**

**解析**（细节理解题）：第一段指出批评者认为"bilinguals can easily translate between languages, which suggests that thoughts exist independently of any particular language"。A项与原文矛盾（infants *demonstrate sophisticated cognitive abilities*）；C项与原文矛盾；D项与原文矛盾。B项与原文完全一致。

---

**2.** The word "nuanced" in "more nuanced than either extreme" (Paragraph 2) is closest in meaning to

A. simpler
B. more extreme
C. more subtle and differentiated
D. more controversial

**Answer: C**

**解析**（词义猜测题）：*nuanced* 意为"微妙的、有细微差别的"，与 *either extreme*（任一极端）形成对比，表示结论比极端观点更精细。A项simpler方向相反；B项more extreme方向相反；D项more controversial无依据。C项 *more subtle and differentiated*（更微妙和有区分度的）最接近。

---

**3.** Roman Jakobson's quote "Languages differ essentially in what they must convey, not in what they may convey" suggests that

A. some languages are superior to others in expressiveness

B. languages differ in their obligatory categories, not their expressive potential

C. all languages can convey exactly the same things in the same way

D. language completely limits what speakers can think about

**Answer: B**

**解析**（推理判断题）：Jakobson 的名言区分了"必须传达"（obligatory categories，如某些语言强制标记时态或性别）和"可以传达"（expressive potential，所有语言都能表达的概念）。A项"superior"与原文精神相反；C项"in the same way"过于绝对；D项是强版决定论，已被否定。B项准确解读了名言的含义。

---

**4.** According to Paragraph 3, understanding linguistic relativity can help in all of the following EXCEPT

A. designing better multilingual curricula in education

B. reducing misunderstandings in international relations

C. improving the physical performance of athletes

D. considering how AI systems make decisions

**Answer: C**

**解析**（细节排除题）：第三段列举了三个应用领域：education（multilingual curricula）、international relations（cross-cultural communication）、technology（AI design）。A、B、D项分别对应这三个领域。C项"improving physical performance of athletes"在文中完全未提及，与语言和思维的关系无关。

---

**5.** What is the author's overall message in this passage?

A. Language completely determines human thought.

B. Language has no effect on how humans think.

C. Language influences thought tendencies but does not imprison the mind.

D. The Sapir-Whorf hypothesis should be rejected entirely.

**Answer: C**

**解析**（主旨大意题）：第二段的核心句"Language does not imprison thought, but it does bias it"概括了全文主旨。A项是强版决定论，已被否定；B项与全文例证矛盾；D项过于绝对，作者接受弱版假说。C项 *influences thought tendencies but does not imprison the mind* 准确概括了作者平衡的立场。
