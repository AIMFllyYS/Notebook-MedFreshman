## 阅读原文

> In a world where more than half the population speaks at least two languages, bilingualism is not the exception — it is the norm. Yet for decades, researchers debated whether growing up with two languages was a cognitive advantage or a cognitive burden. Early studies in the mid-twentieth century suggested that bilingual children lagged behind their monolingual peers in vocabulary and verbal intelligence. These findings fueled concerns that bilingualism confused children and hindered their academic progress.
>
> Modern research has overturned those early findings. Using brain-imaging techniques such as fMRI, scientists have discovered that bilinguals and monolinguals process language differently. In bilinguals, both languages are constantly active, even when only one is being used. This means that a bilingual brain is perpetually managing two linguistic systems — selecting the appropriate language and inhibiting the other. This constant juggling act, researchers believe, is what gives bilinguals their cognitive edge.
>
> The cognitive benefits of bilingualism are most evident in what psychologists call "executive function" — the brain's control system for managing attention, suppressing distractions, and switching between tasks. In laboratory tests, bilinguals consistently outperform monolinguals on tasks that require ignoring irrelevant information or switching rules. For example, in the Stroop test — where participants must name the color of a word while ignoring the word's meaning — bilinguals are faster and more accurate, suggesting superior inhibitory control.

---

**1.** What is the main purpose of the passage?

A. To argue that monolingualism is superior to bilingualism.

B. To explain how bilingualism benefits cognitive function.

C. To prove that all children should learn three languages.

D. To describe the history of brain-imaging technology.

**Answer: B**

**解析**（主旨大意题）：文章第一段回顾早期对双语的负面认知，第二段以现代研究推翻旧观念，第三段具体阐述双语对执行功能的益处。全文围绕"双语如何有益认知功能"展开。A项与全文核心相反；C项"three languages"在文中未提及；D项brain-imaging只是工具，非文章主题。B项准确概括全文主旨。

---

**2.** According to the passage, why did early studies show bilingual children "lagging behind"?

A. Because bilingualism genuinely impairs cognitive development.

B. Because the studies had methodological flaws confusing language with socioeconomic status.

C. Because bilingual children have smaller vocabularies in each language.

D. Because bilingual children cannot learn grammar rules properly.

**Answer: B**

**解析**（推理判断题）：第一段提到早期研究发现双语儿童"lagged behind"，但第二段以"Modern research has overturned those early findings"推翻了这些结论。结合教材注释可知，早期研究的方法论缺陷在于将语言地位与社会经济地位混淆。A项与全文核心矛盾；C项和D项在文中未提及。B项为正确推断。

---

**3.** The phrase "juggling act" in Paragraph 2 is used to describe

A. the difficulty of learning to juggle as a hobby

B. the bilingual brain's constant management of two language systems

C. the confusion bilinguals experience when speaking

D. the process of translating between languages

**Answer: B**

**解析**（词义猜测题）：*juggling act* 出现在"This constant juggling act, researchers believe, is what gives bilinguals their cognitive edge"中，指代前文所述的"perpetually managing two linguistic systems — selecting the appropriate language and inhibiting the other"。A项字面理解，错误；C项"confusion"与原文矛盾；D项"translating"缩小了范围。B项准确理解了比喻的含义。

---

**4.** According to Paragraph 3, the Stroop test measures a person's ability to

A. read words aloud quickly

B. name colors while ignoring the meaning of color words

C. match colors with their corresponding words

D. memorize lists of color names

**Answer: B**

**解析**（细节理解题）：第三段明确指出"in the Stroop test — where participants must name the color of a word while ignoring the word's meaning"。A项只是读词，忽略了关键冲突；C项是匹配而非命名颜色；D项是记忆任务，与Stroop测试无关。B项与原文完全一致。

---

**5.** What can be inferred about bilinguals' performance on the Stroop test?

A. They perform worse than monolinguals because of language interference.

B. They perform about the same as monolinguals.

C. They perform better due to superior inhibitory control.

D. Their performance depends on which language they are using.

**Answer: C**

**解析**（推理判断题）：第三段指出"bilinguals are faster and more accurate, suggesting superior inhibitory control"。A项与原文相反；B项与原文矛盾；D项在文中未提及。C项为原文直接支持的推断。
