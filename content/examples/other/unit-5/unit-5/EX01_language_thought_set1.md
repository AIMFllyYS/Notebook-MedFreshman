:::example{label=EX01 · Does Language Shape the Way We Think? — Set 1}

## 阅读原文

> For centuries, philosophers and linguists have debated a deceptively simple question: does the language we speak shape the way we think? The idea that language determines thought — known as linguistic determinism — was first proposed by Benjamin Lee Whorf and his mentor Edward Sapir in the early twentieth century. Their hypothesis suggested that speakers of different languages perceive the world in fundamentally different ways because their languages impose different categories on reality.
>
> Consider how different languages describe colors. In English, the basic color terms include separate words for "blue" and "green." In some languages, however, a single word covers both colors. Research has shown that speakers of languages with separate terms for blue and green can distinguish between the two shades more quickly than speakers whose language uses one word. This suggests that language does not merely label what we see — it actively shapes how we categorize and remember visual information.
>
> Spatial language provides another striking example. Some languages, such as Guugu Yimithirr spoken in northern Australia, do not use words like "left" and "right." Instead, speakers use absolute directions — north, south, east, and west — even when describing the position of an object inside a room. As a result, speakers of such languages maintain a constant awareness of cardinal directions that most speakers of English or Chinese never develop.

---

**1.** What is the main idea of the passage?

A. All languages categorize colors in the same way.

B. Language influences how speakers perceive and categorize the world.

C. The Sapir-Whorf hypothesis has been completely disproven.

D. Bilingual speakers think differently from monolingual speakers.

**Answer: B**

**解析**（主旨大意题）：文章第一段提出语言是否塑造思维的问题，第二、三段分别以颜色词和空间语言为例证，说明语言确实影响使用者的感知和分类方式。A项与原文相反；C项过于绝对；D项在本文前三段中未提及。B项准确概括全文主旨。

---

**2.** According to the passage, speakers of Guugu Yimithirr differ from English speakers in that they

A. cannot distinguish between blue and green

B. use absolute directions instead of "left" and "right"

C. have a larger vocabulary for describing colors

D. perceive time differently from English speakers

**Answer: B**

**解析**（细节理解题）：第三段明确指出 Guugu Yimithirr 语"do not use words like 'left' and 'right'"，而是使用"absolute directions — north, south, east, and west"。A项错误，颜色区分问题在第二段讨论的是其他语言；C项无原文依据；D项在文中未提及。B项与原文完全一致。

---

**3.** The word "impose" in "their languages impose different categories on reality" (Paragraph 1) is closest in meaning to

A. remove
B. suggest
C. force upon
D. explain

**Answer: C**

**解析**（词义猜测题）：*impose* 在此语境中意为"强加、施加"，即语言将其范畴强加于现实。A项remove方向相反；B项suggest语气过弱；D项explain不如C项准确。C项 *force upon*（强加于）最接近原文含义。

---

**4.** What can be inferred from the color experiment described in Paragraph 2?

A. Language completely determines what colors we can see.

B. Speakers without separate color terms cannot see blue and green.

C. Having separate linguistic labels may enhance the speed of visual discrimination.

D. The experiment proves that linguistic determinism is correct.

**Answer: C**

**解析**（推理判断题）：第二段指出有独立颜色词的使用者"distinguish between the two shades more quickly"，说明语言标签可能增强视觉辨别的速度。A项"completely determines"过于绝对；B项与原文矛盾；D项过于绝对，原文支持弱版假说。C项为合理推断。

---

**5.** Which of the following best describes the author's attitude toward the Sapir-Whorf hypothesis?

A. Completely supportive
B. Completely dismissive
C. Cautiously accepting of a modified version
D. Uninterested and neutral

**Answer: C**

**解析**（观点态度题）：作者在第一段介绍假说，第二、三段提供支持例证，第四段引入批评，第五段提出平衡结论——"Language does not imprison thought, but it does bias it"。这表明作者既不完全支持也不完全否定，而是接受一个修正后的版本。A、B项过于极端；D项"uninterested"与作者详细论述的态度不符。C项准确描述作者态度。
