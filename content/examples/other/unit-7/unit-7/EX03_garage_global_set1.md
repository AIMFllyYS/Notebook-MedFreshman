## 阅读原文

> Some of the world's most influential companies began in garages, dorm rooms, and small workshops. Apple, Amazon, Google, and Hewlett-Packard all trace their origins to humble spaces where founders worked with limited resources but unlimited ambition. These stories have become the modern equivalent of the American Dream — proof that with the right idea, the right team, and the right timing, anyone can build something that changes the world. But behind every success story lie hundreds of failures, and the path from garage to global is neither straight nor guaranteed.
>
> What enables some startups to succeed where most fail? Researchers who study entrepreneurship point to several common factors. First, successful founders possess a deep understanding of a real problem — not a hypothetical need, but a pain point that people experience daily. Second, they build prototypes quickly and test them with real users, iterating based on feedback rather than assumptions. Third, they remain flexible, willing to pivot when the original plan is not working. And fourth, they persist through setbacks with a determination that borders on stubbornness.
>
> The role of timing is often underestimated. Many successful innovations succeeded not because they were the first to attempt something, but because they arrived at the right moment — when technology, market conditions, and consumer readiness aligned. YouTube was not the first video-sharing platform, but it launched when broadband internet was becoming widespread. Facebook was not the first social network, but it arrived when college students were hungry for digital connection. Innovation, it turns out, is as much about being ready as it is about being right.

---

**1.** What is the main purpose of the passage?

A. To list all companies that started in garages.

B. To explain the factors that contribute to startup success.

C. To prove that garage startups always succeed.

D. To discourage people from starting businesses.

**Answer: B**

**解析**（主旨大意题）：文章第一段以车库创业故事引入，第二段分析成功因素，第三段强调时机作用，整体目的是解释初创公司成功的因素。A项"all companies"过于绝对；C项"always succeed"与"behind every success story lie hundreds of failures"矛盾；D项与全文鼓励创业精神的基调矛盾。B项准确概括全文目的。

---

**2.** According to Paragraph 2, which of the following is NOT a common factor of successful startups?

A. Deep understanding of a real problem

B. Quick prototyping and testing with real users

C. Unlimited financial resources from the start

D. Willingness to pivot when the original plan fails

**Answer: C**

**解析**（细节排除题）：第二段列举了四个成功因素：理解真实问题、快速原型测试、保持灵活（pivot）、坚持。A、B、D项分别对应这些因素。C项"unlimited financial resources"与第一段"limited resources but unlimited ambition"矛盾，且在第二段中未提及。

---

**3.** The word "pivot" in "willing to pivot when the original plan is not working" (Paragraph 2) most likely means

A. to give up and start over completely
B. to change direction or strategy based on feedback
C. to continue with the original plan despite difficulties
D. to sell the company to a larger competitor

**Answer: B**

**解析**（词义猜测题）：*pivot* 在创业语境中意为"转向"——根据市场反馈调整方向。A项"give up and start over"过于极端；C项"continue with the original plan"与pivot含义相反；D项"sell the company"与pivot无关。B项 *change direction or strategy based on feedback* 最接近原文含义。

---

**4.** What point does the author make with the examples of YouTube and Facebook in Paragraph 3?

A. Being the first to market guarantees success.

B. Being the first is less important than arriving at the right time.

C. Video-sharing and social networking are the only profitable industries.

D. Technology companies always succeed regardless of timing.

**Answer: B**

**解析**（推理判断题）：第三段指出YouTube和Facebook"were not the first"但"arrived at the right moment"，说明时机比先发更重要。A项"being the first guarantees success"与原文矛盾；C项"only profitable industries"过于绝对；D项"always succeed regardless of timing"与全文强调时机的论点矛盾。B项准确概括了例证的含义。

---

**5.** The statement "Innovation is as much about being ready as it is about being right" suggests that

A. being right is more important than being ready

B. being ready is more important than being right

C. both readiness and correctness are equally important for innovation

D. neither readiness nor correctness matters for innovation

**Answer: C**

**解析**（句意理解题）：*as much... as...* 结构表示两者同等重要，即"准备就绪"与"正确"对创新同等重要。A项和B项都偏向一方，与 *as much... as...* 的等量含义矛盾；D项"neither matters"与全文矛盾。C项 *both are equally important* 准确解释了句意。
