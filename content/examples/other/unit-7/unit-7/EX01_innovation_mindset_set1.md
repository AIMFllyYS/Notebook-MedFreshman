:::example{label=EX01 · The Innovation Mindset — Set 1}

## 阅读原文

> What separates innovators from the rest of us? It is not intelligence, resources, or luck — though all of these help. According to decades of research in psychology and business, the key difference is a particular way of thinking: the innovation mindset. This mindset is characterized by curiosity, a willingness to experiment, and perhaps most importantly, a fundamentally different relationship with failure. Where most people see failure as something to be avoided, innovators see it as information — a necessary step on the path to discovery.
>
> Consider how innovators approach problems. When faced with a challenge, most people look for conventional solutions — approaches that have worked before. Innovators, by contrast, start by questioning the problem itself. Is this the right problem to solve? Are the assumptions behind it valid? Could the problem be reframed in a way that opens up new possibilities? This habit of questioning assumptions is what allows innovators to see opportunities where others see obstacles.
>
> The innovation mindset also involves a distinctive approach to collaboration. Innovators do not work in isolation; they build diverse teams that bring together different perspectives, skills, and experiences. Research consistently shows that diverse teams outperform homogeneous ones in creative problem-solving, not because individual members are smarter, but because the friction between different viewpoints generates more novel ideas.

---

**1.** What is the main idea of the passage?

A. Innovation requires high intelligence and abundant resources.

B. The innovation mindset — characterized by curiosity, experimentation, and a healthy relationship with failure — distinguishes innovators from others.

C. Innovation can only happen in diverse teams.

D. Failure is the most important factor in innovation.

**Answer: B**

**解析**（主旨大意题）：文章第一段定义创新思维，第二段描述其问题处理方式，第三段描述其协作方式，核心是创新思维的特征使创新者与众不同。A项与"It is not intelligence, resources, or luck"矛盾；C项"only in diverse teams"过于绝对；D项将失败单独列为最重要因素，忽略了好奇心和实验意愿。B项准确概括全文主旨。

---

**2.** According to the passage, how do innovators differ from most people in their approach to problems?

A. Innovators always find conventional solutions faster.

B. Innovators start by questioning the problem itself rather than looking for conventional solutions.

C. Innovators avoid asking questions and prefer to work alone.

D. Innovators rely on luck rather than systematic thinking.

**Answer: B**

**解析**（细节理解题）：第二段明确对比"most people look for conventional solutions"与"Innovators... start by questioning the problem itself"。A项与原文矛盾（创新者不寻找常规方案）；C项与原文矛盾（创新者质疑问题、建立多元团队）；D项与"It is not... luck"矛盾。B项与原文完全一致。

---

**3.** The word "conventional" in "conventional solutions" (Paragraph 2) is closest in meaning to

A. creative and unusual
B. traditional and commonly accepted
C. complex and sophisticated
D. new and experimental

**Answer: B**

**解析**（词义猜测题）：*conventional* 意为"传统的、常规的"，与后文创新者的"questioning"形成对比。A项"creative and unusual"方向相反；C项"complex"与原文含义无关；D项"new and experimental"是创新者的特征而非conventional的含义。B项 *traditional and commonly accepted* 最接近。

---

**4.** According to Paragraph 3, why do diverse teams outperform homogeneous ones?

A. Because their individual members are more intelligent.

B. Because they have more financial resources.

C. Because friction between different viewpoints generates more novel ideas.

D. Because they avoid all forms of conflict.

**Answer: C**

**解析**（细节理解题）：第三段明确指出"not because individual members are smarter, but because the friction between different viewpoints generates more novel ideas"。A项与"not because individual members are smarter"矛盾；B项在文中未提及；D项与"creative friction"概念矛盾。C项与原文完全一致。

---

**5.** What does the author mean by "innovators see it [failure] as information"?

A. Failure provides data that helps guide future efforts.

B. Failure is enjoyable and should be celebrated.

C. Failure means the innovator should give up.

D. Failure is irrelevant to the innovation process.

**Answer: A**

**解析**（句意理解题）：第一段将失败定义为"a necessary step on the path to discovery"，第四段进一步解释"Each failure eliminates a wrong path and brings the innovator closer to the right one"，即失败提供信息以指导未来方向。B项"enjoyable"过于极端；C项"give up"与全文精神矛盾；D项"irrelevant"与原文矛盾。A项 *provides data that helps guide future efforts* 准确解释了"information"的含义。
