## 阅读原文

> The journey from garage to global also requires a transformation in mindset. What works for a team of five in a garage does not work for a company of five hundred in an office. Founders must learn to delegate, to build systems that scale, and to shift from doing everything themselves to enabling others to do their best work. This transition — from maker to manager, from experimenter to strategist — is where many founders stumble. The skills that made them great innovators are not always the skills that make them great leaders.
>
> Despite the challenges, the garage-to-global story remains powerful because it speaks to something deep in human nature: the desire to create, to build, and to leave a mark on the world. Not every startup will become the next Apple or Google, and that is fine. The true value of entrepreneurship lies not only in the companies it creates but in the mindset it cultivates — a mindset of curiosity, resilience, and the belief that the status quo is never inevitable. In a world of rapid change, that mindset may be the most important product of all.
>
> Critics argue that the garage narrative is overly romanticized. They point out that many successful startups were founded by people with significant advantages — elite education, family wealth, industry connections. The garage, in some cases, was less a sign of humble beginnings than a temporary workspace before serious investment arrived. Nevertheless, the story endures because it offers something that statistics cannot: hope.

---

**1.** According to Paragraph 1, why do many founders "stumble" during the transition from garage to global?

A. Because they lack sufficient funding to grow.

B. Because the skills that make great innovators are not always the skills that make great leaders.

C. Because they refuse to hire new employees.

D. Because the market conditions suddenly change.

**Answer: B**

**解析**（细节理解题）：第一段明确指出"This transition — from maker to manager, from experimenter to strategist — is where many founders stumble"且"The skills that made them great innovators are not always the skills that make them great leaders"。A项"funding"在文中未提及；C项"refuse to hire"与"learn to delegate"矛盾；D项"market conditions"与本段主题无关。B项与原文完全一致。

---

**2.** The phrase "from maker to manager, from experimenter to strategist" describes

A. the physical relocation of a company
B. the mindset transformation required for scaling a startup
C. the process of hiring new employees
D. the failure of a startup

**Answer: B**

**解析**（短语理解题）：两组对比描述的是创始人角色的转变——从亲自做事（maker/experimenter）到管理他人（manager/strategist），即扩大规模所需的思维转变。A项"physical relocation"过于字面；C项"hiring"只是转变的一部分；D项"failure"与原文矛盾。B项 *mindset transformation required for scaling* 准确概括了短语含义。

---

**3.** According to Paragraph 2, the true value of entrepreneurship lies in

A. only the companies it creates
B. only the mindset it cultivates
C. both the companies created and the mindset cultivated
D. the financial returns for investors

**Answer: C**

**解析**（细节理解题）：第二段指出"The true value of entrepreneurship lies not only in the companies it creates but in the mindset it cultivates"，使用 *not only... but...* 结构表示两者都是价值所在。A项和B项都只涵盖一方面；D项"financial returns"在文中未提及。C项 *both* 准确反映了 *not only... but...* 的并列含义。

---

**4.** What do critics argue in Paragraph 3?

A. The garage narrative accurately represents all startup origins.

B. The garage story is overly romanticized and ignores founders' advantages.

C. Every successful startup was founded by people with no advantages.

D. The garage narrative should be banned.

**Answer: B**

**解析**（细节理解题）：第三段指出批评者认为"the garage narrative is overly romanticized"且"many successful startups were founded by people with significant advantages — elite education, family wealth, industry connections"。A项与"overly romanticized"矛盾；C项"no advantages"与原文矛盾；D项"banned"过于极端。B项与原文完全一致。

---

**5.** Despite the critics' concerns, why does the garage story endure according to the passage?

A. Because it is statistically proven to be accurate.

B. Because it offers hope, which statistics cannot provide.

C. Because all startups actually do begin in garages.

D. Because investors require a garage story before funding.

**Answer: B**

**解析**（细节理解题）：第三段最后一句指出"the story endures because it offers something that statistics cannot: hope"。A项"statistically proven"与批评者的观点矛盾；C项"all startups"过于绝对；D项"investors require"在文中未提及。B项与原文完全一致。
