## 阅读原文

> Perhaps the most counterintuitive aspect of the innovation mindset is its relationship with failure. In most organizations, failure is punished — it carries stigma, damages careers, and discourages risk-taking. Innovators invert this logic. They understand that failure is not the opposite of success but a component of it. Thomas Edison's famous remark — "I have not failed. I've just found 10,000 ways that won't work" — captures this mindset perfectly. Each failure eliminates a wrong path and brings the innovator closer to the right one.
>
> Developing an innovation mindset is not easy. It requires unlearning habits of thought that have been reinforced by years of education and social conditioning. Schools reward getting the right answer, not asking the right question. Workplaces reward consistency and predictability, not experimentation and surprise. Yet in a world where change is accelerating and the future is increasingly unpredictable, the innovation mindset may be the most valuable skill we can cultivate — not just for entrepreneurs and engineers, but for anyone who wants to thrive in the twenty-first century.
>
> Organizations can foster the innovation mindset by creating cultures that celebrate experimentation, tolerate failure, and reward learning. Google's famous "20% time" policy, which allowed employees to spend one day a week on personal projects, produced some of the company's most successful products, including Gmail and Google News. Such policies signal that innovation is not just permitted but expected.

**1.** According to Paragraph 1, how do innovators view the relationship between failure and success?

A. Failure is the opposite of success and should be avoided at all costs.

B. Failure is a component of success, not its opposite.

C. Failure has no relationship to success.

D. Success is impossible without first experiencing catastrophic failure.

**Answer: B**

**解析**（细节理解题）：第一段明确指出"failure is not the opposite of success but a component of it"。A项与原文直接矛盾；C项"no relationship"与原文矛盾；D项"catastrophic failure"过于极端，原文说的是每次失败排除一条错误路径。B项与原文完全一致。

---

**2.** The Edison quote "I have not failed. I've just found 10,000 ways that won't work" is used to illustrate

A. Edison's lack of success in his experiments

B. the idea that each failure provides useful information

C. the importance of persistence over intelligence

D. the need to try at least 10,000 times before succeeding

**Answer: B**

**解析**（推理判断题）：引语紧接"failure is not the opposite of success but a component of it"，随后解释"Each failure eliminates a wrong path and brings the innovator closer to the right one"。A项与爱迪生的成就矛盾；C项虽相关但非引语的核心含义；D项"at least 10,000 times"过于字面化。B项 *each failure provides useful information* 准确解读了引语的含义。

---

**3.** What does the author suggest about schools and workplaces in Paragraph 2?

A. They actively promote the innovation mindset.

B. They reward habits that are contrary to the innovation mindset.

C. They have no effect on how people think about failure.

D. They should be abolished to encourage innovation.

**Answer: B**

**解析**（推理判断题）：第二段指出"Schools reward getting the right answer, not asking the right question"和"Workplaces reward consistency and predictability, not experimentation and surprise"，说明学校和职场的奖励机制与创新思维相反。A项与原文矛盾；C项与"reinforced by years of education and social conditioning"矛盾；D项"abolished"过于极端。B项准确概括了原文含义。

---

**4.** According to Paragraph 3, Google's "20% time" policy demonstrates that

A. employees should spend all their time on personal projects

B. organizations can foster innovation by allowing time for experimentation

C. Google is the only company that values innovation

D. personal projects always lead to successful products

**Answer: B**

**解析**（推理判断题）：第三段以Google的"20% time"为例说明"Organizations can foster the innovation mindset by creating cultures that celebrate experimentation"。A项"all their time"与"one day a week"矛盾；C项"the only company"过于绝对；D项"always lead to"过于绝对。B项准确反映了例证的含义。

---

**5.** What is the author's overall message in this passage?

A. Innovation is a natural talent that cannot be learned.

B. The innovation mindset can be cultivated and is valuable for everyone in the twenty-first century.

C. Organizations should punish failure to encourage success.

D. Only entrepreneurs and engineers need an innovation mindset.

**Answer: B**

**解析**（主旨大意题）：第二段结尾明确指出"the innovation mindset may be the most valuable skill we can cultivate — not just for entrepreneurs and engineers, but for anyone who wants to thrive in the twenty-first century"。A项"cannot be learned"与"cultivate"矛盾；C项与全文核心论点相反；D项与"not just for entrepreneurs and engineers"矛盾。B项准确概括了作者的核心主张。
