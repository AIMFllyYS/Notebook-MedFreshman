:::example{label=EX03 · The Digital Divide — Set 1}

## 阅读原文

> While much of the world takes internet access for granted, a significant portion of humanity remains unconnected. According to the International Telecommunication Union, approximately 2.6 billion people — roughly one-third of the global population — have never used the internet. This gap between the connected and the unconnected is known as the "digital divide," and its consequences extend far beyond the inconvenience of being unable to stream videos or check social media.
>
> The digital divide is not a single gap but a complex web of inequalities. It separates rich from poor, urban from rural, young from old, and educated from uneducated. In developing nations, the cost of devices and data plans puts internet access out of reach for millions. In rural areas, the physical infrastructure — fiber-optic cables, cell towers, reliable electricity — simply does not exist. And even where infrastructure is available, digital literacy — the skills needed to use technology effectively — remains a significant barrier.
>
> The consequences of the digital divide are profound. In an era when essential services — banking, healthcare, education, government services — increasingly move online, those without internet access are effectively excluded from full participation in society. Children without home internet cannot complete online homework assignments, falling behind their connected peers. Job seekers without digital skills cannot apply for positions that require online applications.

---

**1.** What is the main idea of the passage?

A. Internet access is available to everyone in the world.

B. The digital divide is a complex problem with serious social consequences.

C. The digital divide only affects developing countries.

D. Technology has solved the problem of global inequality.

**Answer: B**

**解析**（主旨大意题）：文章第一段介绍数字鸿沟的全球规模，第二段分析其多维性，第三段阐述其社会后果。A项与"2.6 billion people have never used the internet"矛盾；C项"only affects developing countries"过于绝对，原文还提到rural areas和elderly people；D项与全文核心矛盾。B项准确概括全文主旨。

---

**2.** According to the passage, approximately how many people have never used the internet?

A. 1.3 billion
B. 2.6 billion
C. 5.2 billion
D. 7.8 billion

**Answer: B**

**解析**（细节理解题）：第一段明确指出"approximately 2.6 billion people — roughly one-third of the global population — have never used the internet"。A、C、D项的数字均与原文不符。B项与原文完全一致。

---

**3.** According to Paragraph 2, all of the following are barriers to internet access EXCEPT

A. the cost of devices and data plans

B. lack of physical infrastructure in rural areas

C. insufficient digital literacy skills

D. lack of interest in using technology

**Answer: D**

**解析**（细节排除题）：第二段列举了三层障碍：经济（cost of devices and data plans）、物理（infrastructure — fiber-optic cables, cell towers, reliable electricity）、技能（digital literacy）。A、B、C项分别对应这三层障碍。D项"lack of interest"在文中完全未提及，不属于原文所述的障碍。

---

**4.** The phrase "effectively excluded from full participation in society" (Paragraph 3) suggests that without internet access, people

A. choose not to participate in society

B. are physically removed from society

C. cannot fully take part in modern social and economic life

D. are legally barred from using public services

**Answer: C**

**解析**（短语理解题）：*effectively excluded from full participation* 意为"实际上被排斥在全面参与之外"，即因缺乏互联网而无法参与现代社会的经济和社会生活。A项"choose not to"与原文矛盾（是被排斥而非主动选择）；B项"physically removed"过于字面；D项"legally barred"在文中无依据。C项 *cannot fully take part in modern social and economic life* 准确解释了短语含义。

---

**5.** What can be inferred about children without home internet from the passage?

A. They perform better in school because they are not distracted.

B. They fall behind their connected peers in completing assignments.

C. They have more face-to-face social interactions.

D. They are less interested in technology careers.

**Answer: B**

**解析**（推理判断题）：第三段指出"Children without home internet cannot complete online homework assignments, falling behind their connected peers"。A项与"falling behind"矛盾；C项和D项在文中未提及。B项为原文直接支持的推断。
