## 阅读原文

> Bridging the digital divide requires action on multiple fronts. Governments must invest in digital infrastructure, particularly in underserved rural and remote areas. Schools and community centers can provide public internet access and digital literacy training. Technology companies can develop more affordable devices and data plans. And international organizations can coordinate efforts to ensure that the benefits of digital technology reach all of humanity, not just the privileged few.
>
> The digital divide is ultimately a question of justice, not technology. In a world where internet access determines one's ability to learn, work, and participate in civic life, being disconnected is not merely an inconvenience — it is a form of marginalization. Closing this gap is not just about laying cables and distributing devices; it is about ensuring that every person has the opportunity to benefit from the digital revolution. As the United Nations has declared, internet access is a basic human right, essential to the realization of other human rights in the twenty-first century.
>
> Progress is being made, but it is uneven. Some countries have launched ambitious national broadband plans, while others struggle with basic electricity infrastructure. The COVID-19 pandemic accelerated digital adoption but also widened the divide, as those with internet access adapted to remote work and learning while those without fell further behind. The challenge ahead is not only technological but political and social: ensuring that the digital revolution leaves no one behind.

---

**1.** According to Paragraph 1, which of the following is NOT mentioned as a way to bridge the digital divide?

A. Government investment in infrastructure

B. Schools providing internet access and training

C. Technology companies developing affordable devices

D. Individuals buying more expensive smartphones

**Answer: D**

**解析**（细节排除题）：第一段列举了四个行动主体：Governments（invest in infrastructure）、Schools and community centers（provide internet access and training）、Technology companies（develop affordable devices）、international organizations（coordinate efforts）。A、B、C项分别对应前三个主体。D项"individuals buying more expensive smartphones"在文中完全未提及，且与"affordable"的方向相反。

---

**2.** The author's statement "The digital divide is ultimately a question of justice, not technology" suggests that

A. technology is irrelevant to the digital divide

B. the digital divide is primarily a moral and social issue

C. justice can be achieved through technology alone

D. the digital divide cannot be solved

**Answer: B**

**解析**（句意理解题）：*a question of justice, not technology* 意为数字鸿沟本质上是正义问题而非纯粹的技术问题。A项"irrelevant"过于极端，技术仍是解决方案的一部分；C项"through technology alone"与"not technology"矛盾；D项"cannot be solved"与全文的解决方案导向矛盾。B项 *primarily a moral and social issue* 准确解释了作者的观点。

---

**3.** What does the United Nations declaration about internet access imply?

A. Internet access is a luxury that only wealthy nations can afford.

B. Internet access is essential for exercising other human rights.

C. Internet access should be controlled by governments.

D. Internet access is less important than other basic needs.

**Answer: B**

**解析**（推理判断题）：第二段指出联合国宣布"internet access is a basic human right, essential to the realization of other human rights in the twenty-first century"。A项"luxury"与"basic human right"矛盾；C项"controlled by governments"在文中无依据；D项"less important"与"essential"矛盾。B项 *essential for exercising other human rights* 与原文"essential to the realization of other human rights"完全一致。

---

**4.** According to Paragraph 3, how did the COVID-19 pandemic affect the digital divide?

A. It narrowed the divide by encouraging more people to get online.

B. It had no significant effect on the digital divide.

C. It widened the divide as connected people adapted while others fell behind.

D. It completely eliminated the digital divide.

**Answer: C**

**解析**（细节理解题）：第三段指出"The COVID-19 pandemic accelerated digital adoption but also widened the divide, as those with internet access adapted to remote work and learning while those without fell further behind"。A项"narrowed"与原文"widen"矛盾；B项"no significant effect"与原文矛盾；D项"completely eliminated"与原文矛盾。C项与原文完全一致。

---

**5.** The author's overall tone in this passage can best be described as

A. optimistic and unconcerned
B. urgent but hopeful
C. pessimistic and resigned
D. neutral and detached

**Answer: B**

**解析**（观点态度题）：作者指出数字鸿沟的严重性（"question of justice"、"form of marginalization"）体现紧迫感，同时列举解决方案和"Progress is being made"体现希望。A项"unconcerned"与紧迫语气矛盾；C项"resigned"与解决方案导向矛盾；D项"detached"与作者明确的立场矛盾。B项 *urgent but hopeful* 准确描述了作者的态度。
