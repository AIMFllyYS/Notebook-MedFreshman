:::example{label=EX01 · Alone Together — Set 1}

## 阅读原文

> We live in an age of unprecedented connectivity. Smartphones, social media, and instant messaging allow us to reach anyone, anywhere, at any time. Yet beneath this surface of constant connection, a quieter story is unfolding. Sherry Turkle, a psychologist at MIT, calls it "alone together" — a state in which we are increasingly connected to devices and disconnected from each other. We text during dinner, scroll during conversations, and check notifications during meetings. The very technology designed to bring us closer, Turkle argues, is pushing us apart.
>
> Turkle's research reveals a troubling pattern. In interviews with hundreds of families, she found that children often feel competing with a parent's phone for attention. "My dad texts during my soccer games," one teenager reported. "He's physically there, but he's not really there." This phrase — "physically there, but not really there" — captures the essence of the problem. Physical proximity no longer guarantees emotional presence.
>
> The consequences extend beyond family dynamics. In the workplace, constant connectivity erodes the boundary between work and personal life. Among friends, superficial digital interactions — likes, emojis, and brief comments — increasingly replace deeper forms of communication. We mistake the frequency of contact for the quality of connection, confusing the number of messages with the depth of understanding.

---

**1.** What does Sherry Turkle mean by "alone together"?

A. People are physically isolated but digitally connected.

B. People are increasingly connected to devices yet disconnected from each other.

C. People prefer to be alone rather than with others.

D. People feel lonely even when surrounded by friends.

**Answer: B**

**解析**（概念理解题）：第一段明确将"alone together"定义为"a state in which we are increasingly connected to devices and disconnected from each other"。A项颠倒了关系（原文是连接设备、断开彼此）；C项"prefer to be alone"与原文矛盾；D项虽相关但不够精确，未点明设备连接的核心。B项准确概括了 Turkle 的概念。

---

**2.** The teenager's statement "He's physically there, but he's not really there" (Paragraph 2) suggests that

A. the father is absent from soccer games

B. physical presence does not equal emotional presence

C. the teenager dislikes his father

D. technology has no effect on family relationships

**Answer: B**

**解析**（推理判断题）：第二段紧接引语后解释"Physical proximity no longer guarantees emotional presence"，即物理在场不等于情感在场。A项与"physically there"矛盾；C项在文中无依据；D项与全文核心论点矛盾。B项准确解读了引语的含义。

---

**3.** The word "erodes" in "constant connectivity erodes the boundary between work and personal life" (Paragraph 3) is closest in meaning to

A. strengthens
B. gradually destroys
C. clearly defines
D. temporarily removes

**Answer: B**

**解析**（词义猜测题）：*erodes* 本义为"侵蚀"，在此比喻"逐渐破坏"工作与个人生活之间的边界。A项strengthens方向相反；C项clearly defines与"侵蚀"矛盾；D项temporarily removes过于短暂，*erodes* 强调渐进的破坏。B项 *gradually destroys*（逐渐破坏）最接近原文含义。

---

**4.** What does the author mean by "We mistake the frequency of contact for the quality of connection"?

A. More messages always mean better relationships.

B. People confuse how often they communicate with how deeply they connect.

C. Frequency and quality are the same thing.

D. Digital communication is always lower quality than face-to-face.

**Answer: B**

**解析**（句意理解题）：*mistake A for B* 意为"将A误认为B"，即人们将联系频率（frequency of contact）误认为连接质量（quality of connection）。A项"always mean"是作者批评的错误认知；C项"are the same thing"正是作者反驳的观点；D项"always"过于绝对。B项准确解释了句意。

---

**5.** Which of the following best describes Turkle's attitude toward technology?

A. Technology should be completely abandoned.

B. Technology is inherently harmful to human relationships.

C. Technology is useful but should be used more mindfully.

D. Technology has no significant impact on human connection.

**Answer: C**

**解析**（观点态度题）：第四段明确指出"Turkle is not anti-technology"且"She acknowledges that digital tools have made life more convenient"，同时建议"develop more mindful habits"。A项与"not anti-technology"矛盾；B项"inherently harmful"过于绝对；D项与全文论述矛盾。C项 *useful but should be used more mindfully* 准确概括了 Turkle 的平衡立场。
