:::example{label=EX01 Silicon Valley工作文化阅读理解（第一套）}

**阅读原文**（关键段落摘录）

> **Para 1:** You stroll into the office a little past 9 a.m. You are wearing weekend casual, even though it is a Wednesday. The office kitchen has coffee and juice, which are free, as are breakfast and lunch. The office is lined with screens where your remote colleagues might pop up as talking heads. You find a desk — no one has assigned seats here — where you put down your laptop, and put on your headphones to tune out the world around you for the next 12 hours.
>
> **Para 2:** Sounds like Silicon Valley around 2009, right? Well, surprise — this is Corporate America in 2020.
>
> **Para 3:** In recent years, the Silicon Valley work culture has spread across the nation. United Shore, a mortgage company in Michigan, has its own in-office escape room full of puzzles and ready for team-building exercises. Then there are the seemingly generous policies around vacation days and time off. Nationwide, an insurance company, gives employees the option to take two Fridays off per month in its Ohio headquarters. This is not just to be nice. "We think we'll get a productivity lift from this perk," Gale King, the company's chief administrative officer, told Columbus Business First. Even super-old companies are bending toward Silicon Valley's new rules. Cargill, a major distributor of agricultural products, recently redesigned its offices with an open layout and adopted a more liberal remote work policy.
>
> **Para 5:** Everything from casual dress codes to free office meals and the rise of remote work has been driven by Silicon Valley. But Silicon Valley's biggest export, Robbins says, is the collapsing barrier between work and life. As the boundaries between work and life become blurred, everyone works all the time.
>
> **Para 6:** Offices used to be like prisons, but at least they had a clear purpose. You wouldn't hang out in a cubicle farm, let alone spend time there on weekends. Then some tech companies came along and reinvented the rat race into something with purpose and, along the way, confused work with the rest of life. Now, your co-workers are supposed to feel like a family. Hierarchies have been flattened, conventional job titles replaced by ones like "wizard" and "ninja." And forget about work-life balance. It's all about work-life integration. Why else would the office have on-site acupuncture, nap pods, and free dinner after 7 p.m.? Even policies like unlimited vacation days, popularized by some media companies, don't actually lead to a more relaxed, sun-kissed workforce. A 2017 investigation found that employees who labor under such policies take fewer days off.

**Questions**

---

**Question 1.** What is the main purpose of the opening paragraph (Para 1)?

A. To compare the working conditions of Silicon Valley employees with those of Wall Street.
B. To illustrate a typical day at a traditional American office in the 1990s.
C. To paint a vivid picture of a modern office environment that will later be identified as widespread.
D. To argue that free food and flexible seating are the most important workplace perks.

**Answer: C**

**解析**: 开头段落（Para 1）详细描绘了一个休闲着装、免费餐饮、无固定座位的现代办公室场景，紧接着 Para 2 揭晓：这不是 2009 年的硅谷，而是 2020 年的普通美国企业。作者用这种"误导性开头"制造悬念，再揭示此类文化已大范围扩散，服务于全文主旨。

- **A 错误**：原文没有对比硅谷与华尔街员工，Wall Street 在文中从未出现。
- **B 错误**：Para 1 描述的场景通过 Para 2 的转折明确说明是 2020 年的现实，而非 1990 年代的传统办公室。
- **C 正确**：Para 1 的生动描绘是为 Para 2 的"惊喜揭晓"服务，进而引出全文关于硅谷工作文化扩散的论点。
- **D 错误**：Para 1 仅列举了多种现代办公室特征，并未就哪项福利最重要作出排序或论断。

---

**Question 2.** According to Para 3, why did Nationwide give its employees the option to take two Fridays off per month?

A. To follow a government regulation on working hours in Ohio.
B. To compete with Silicon Valley companies for talent.
C. To boost employee productivity through this perk.
D. To reward workers who had completed exceptional projects.

**Answer: C**

**解析**: Para 3 原文明确引用了 Nationwide 首席行政官 Gale King 的话："We think we'll get a productivity lift from this perk."（我们认为这项福利会提升生产力。）这是公司推出该政策的直接原因。

- **A 错误**：原文没有提到俄亥俄州的任何政府工时规定。
- **B 错误**：原文提到是硅谷模式向外扩散，但 Nationwide 提供这项福利的动机是提高生产力，并非为了和科技公司抢人才。
- **C 正确**：与原文引语直接对应——"We think we'll get a productivity lift from this perk。"
- **D 错误**：原文没有提到这是对特定员工项目成就的奖励，该政策面向所有员工。

---

**Question 3.** It can be inferred from Para 6 that unlimited vacation day policies tend to result in which of the following?

A. Employees feeling more valued and taking longer holidays.
B. Employees actually working more and taking less time off.
C. Companies saving money on paid vacation reimbursements.
D. A healthier work-life balance among all levels of staff.

**Answer: B**

**解析**: Para 6 结尾写道："Even policies like unlimited vacation days, popularized by some media companies, don't actually lead to a more relaxed, sun-kissed workforce. A 2017 investigation found that employees who labor under such policies take fewer days off."（无限假期政策并不能带来更放松的员工，调查发现实行此类政策的员工反而请假更少。）由此推断，"无限假期"实际上导致员工工作更多、休假更少。

- **A 错误**：与原文结论相反，原文明确说员工"take fewer days off"（请假更少）。
- **B 正确**：与原文"take fewer days off"直接对应，即员工实际上工作更多，休假更少。
- **C 错误**：原文并未讨论公司的财务节省，推断范围超出原文。
- **D 错误**：Para 5 和 Para 6 反复强调工作与生活边界的模糊，本段结论也说明无限假期无助于实现工作与生活平衡。

---

**Question 4.** The word "bending" in Para 3 ("Even super-old companies are bending toward Silicon Valley's new rules") most probably means ______.

A. opposing
B. misunderstanding
C. adapting to
D. profiting from

**Answer: C**

**解析**: "bending toward"在此处的语境是"连老牌企业都在向硅谷的新规则……"，搭配 Cargill 公司重新设计办公室、采用远程办公政策的具体例子，可知其含义是"倾向于、顺应、转变以适应"。

- **A 错误**："opposing"（反对）与语境相反——Cargill 恰恰是主动采纳了硅谷模式。
- **B 错误**："misunderstanding"（误解）语境不符，原文是在说公司行为上的转变，而非认知上的偏差。
- **C 正确**："adapting to"（适应、顺应）与"bending toward"在此语境下同义，指老牌公司调整自身以跟上硅谷工作文化。
- **D 错误**："profiting from"（从中获利）意思范围不符，原文强调的是文化层面的跟随，而非经济收益。

---

**Question 5.** What is the author's overall attitude toward the spread of Silicon Valley work culture?

A. Enthusiastically supportive, praising its creativity and employee-friendly perks.
B. Mostly neutral, merely documenting the cultural shift without passing judgment.
C. Critical and skeptical, suggesting it blurs work-life boundaries and harms workers.
D. Indifferent, believing that work culture changes have little real impact on employees.

**Answer: C**

**解析**: 作者对硅谷工作文化扩散的态度贯穿全文。Para 5 指出"Silicon Valley's biggest export…is the collapsing barrier between work and life"（最大的输出是工作与生活边界的崩塌）；Para 6 将此描述为"confused work with the rest of life"（把工作与生活混为一谈），并揭示无限假期反而让员工请更少假；Para 8 和 Para 9 进一步批评这种文化是为资本服务的"hustle culture"（奋斗文化）。整体基调批判且怀疑。

- **A 错误**：原文没有对这些福利表示热情赞扬，而是多次揭示其负面效果（如无限假期的调查结果）。
- **B 错误**：作者使用了大量带有倾向性的措辞（"confused work with the rest of life""something rotten at the core"），绝非中立记录。
- **C 正确**：作者通过多个角度（工作与生活边界模糊、假期政策反效果、Dan Lyons 的亲身经历）一贯表达批判与质疑态度。
- **D 错误**：原文大量篇幅讨论工作文化对员工的真实影响，作者绝非漠然。
