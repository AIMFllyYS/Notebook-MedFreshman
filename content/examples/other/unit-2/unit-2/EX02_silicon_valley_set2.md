**阅读原文**（关键段落摘录）

> **Para 7:** Fewer people have been more vocal opponents of this 24/7 work culture than Dan Lyons, a former journalist who left the newsroom to work at start-ups in the mid-2000s. The experience was so unpleasant that he soon quit his tech job, which he took advantage of in his new job writing for the television series Silicon Valley. The series appears to be an absurdist parody to anyone outside of the tech world, but they are portraits too real to many people inside of it.
>
> **Para 8:** Lyons believes these new-age corporate practices, along with perks like free snacks or beer on tap, are simply a misdirection from something rotten at the core. He blames worker unhappiness not just on Silicon Valley's work culture but also on its business model — one he calls "shareholder capitalism." The modern tech company is obsessed with growth and profit, at the expense of its employees and to the benefit of its investors.
>
> **Para 9:** Worst of all, the tech world has managed to recast this workaholism for someone else's profit as something desirable: hustle culture. It's replaced the 9-to-5 with the 996 — that is, 9 a.m. to 9 p.m., six days a week. An entrepreneur even said: "Nobody ever changed the world on 40 hours a week."
>
> **Para 10:** Can we unwind the work culture we've created? Maybe. In recent months, people in Silicon Valley have begun to redraw the boundaries — or at least acknowledge the absurdity of the norm. When the Verge published an investigation into the work environment at Away, a suitcase start-up, it caused enough of a reaction that the company's CEO resigned. A number of Away's employees described abusive messages from the CEO, being asked to work through vacations, and a culture of bullying — once considered normal, now considered unacceptable. Then again, other people in Silicon Valley simply shrugged. Austen Allred, the CEO of Lambda School, tweeted that the allegations shouldn't seem surprising: "It's probably 99 percent of all companies, actually."
>
> **Para 11:** That's enough to make someone wish they were back in the 1950s. Of course, it's not like work culture was perfect half a century ago. As Ron Friedman writes in his 2014 book The Best Place to Work: The Art and Science of Creating an Extraordinary Workplace, there are trade-offs to everything: "Cubicles are depressing. Private offices are isolating. Open spaces are distracting." But maybe it's time to reimagine a world where the office is just the office — a place you can actually get work done, and then eventually leave.

**Questions**

---

**Question 1.** According to Para 7, what did Dan Lyons do after quitting his tech job?

A. He returned to his previous career as a journalist at a major newspaper.
B. He founded his own start-up company in Silicon Valley.
C. He wrote for a TV series that dramatized Silicon Valley culture.
D. He published a book criticizing shareholder capitalism.

**Answer: C**

**解析**: Para 7 原文明确说明："he soon quit his tech job, which he took advantage of in his new job writing for the television series Silicon Valley."（他很快辞掉科技工作，并将这段经历用于为电视剧《硅谷》编剧。）

- **A 错误**：原文说他离开了新闻业（"left the newsroom"）去创业公司工作，辞职后转向的是编剧，而非回归新闻业。
- **B 错误**：原文说他是去已有的创业公司工作，辞职后并没有创立自己的公司。
- **C 正确**：与原文"writing for the television series Silicon Valley"直接对应。
- **D 错误**：在文中，是 Para 8 介绍了 Lyons 的观点，但原文并未提及他写了一本关于股东资本主义的书。将批评观点误认为是书名是典型干扰项。

---

**Question 2.** According to Para 8, what does Dan Lyons believe is the fundamental cause of worker unhappiness in tech companies?

A. The shortage of qualified employees in the technology sector.
B. The company's obsession with growth and profit at employees' expense.
C. The poor quality of free snacks and other minor workplace perks.
D. The rigid and hierarchical management structures within tech firms.

**Answer: B**

**解析**: Para 8 原文写道："He blames worker unhappiness not just on Silicon Valley's work culture but also on its business model — one he calls 'shareholder capitalism.' The modern tech company is obsessed with growth and profit, at the expense of its employees and to the benefit of its investors."（他将员工的不幸归咎于硅谷工作文化，以及它以员工利益为代价追求增长与利润的商业模式。）

- **A 错误**：原文没有提及人才短缺问题。
- **B 正确**：与原文"obsessed with growth and profit, at the expense of its employees"直接对应。
- **C 错误**：原文将零食和福利称为"misdirection"（转移注意力的手段），并非问题根源，Lyons 本人也明确说这些是"掩盖核心腐败的障眼法"。
- **D 错误**：原文实际上提到科技公司扁平化了层级（Para 6："Hierarchies have been flattened"），管理层级僵化并非 Lyons 批评的重点。

---

**Question 3.** In Para 9, the term "hustle culture" most probably refers to ______.

A. A movement encouraging employees to leave toxic workplaces.
B. The promotion of extreme overwork as a desirable and even admirable lifestyle.
C. A management strategy that rewards efficiency over long working hours.
D. The cultural practice of negotiating aggressively for higher salaries.

**Answer: B**

**解析**: Para 9 原文将"hustle culture"（奋斗文化）界定为：科技行业将"为他人利润加班"（workaholism for someone else's profit）包装成令人向往的东西，以"996"（早9到晚9，一周六天）取代传统朝九晚五，并引用创业者的话"Nobody ever changed the world on 40 hours a week"加以佐证。

- **A 错误**：与语境完全相反，"hustle culture"鼓励的是更拼命地工作，而非逃离有毒职场。
- **B 正确**：与原文"recast this workaholism…as something desirable"（将工作狂文化重塑为令人向往的东西）一致。
- **C 错误**：原文强调的是时间投入（996），而非效率优先的管理策略。
- **D 错误**："hustle culture"在本文指的是工作时间和强度，与薪资谈判无关。

---

**Question 4.** What is the significance of mentioning the Away start-up incident in Para 10?

A. It proves that all Silicon Valley companies are guilty of illegal labor practices.
B. It shows that some toxic behaviors once seen as normal are now being challenged.
C. It demonstrates that media investigations always lead to corporate reform.
D. It suggests that the CEO of Away was uniquely ruthless compared to peers.

**Answer: B**

**解析**: Para 10 描述了《The Verge》对 Away 公司的调查引发强烈反响，CEO 最终辞职，并指出那些曾被视为正常的行为（"once considered normal"）如今已被视为不可接受（"now considered unacceptable"）。这说明对硅谷毒性文化的反思已开始出现。

- **A 错误**："all Silicon Valley companies"属于过度推断，且原文还引用了 Lambda School CEO 的话表示此类现象普遍存在，而非违法，也未涉及法律层面。
- **B 正确**：与原文"once considered normal, now considered unacceptable"直接对应，体现了文化观念的转变。
- **C 错误**：原文并未说媒体调查"总是"（always）能推动改革，事实上同段还提到"other people in Silicon Valley simply shrugged"（其他人只是耸耸肩）。
- **D 错误**：Lambda School CEO Austen Allred 的推文暗示这类行为在公司中极为普遍（"99 percent of all companies"），说明 Away CEO 并非特例。

**Question 5.** How does the author organize the overall argument in this passage?

A. By presenting two opposing viewpoints and then offering a compromise solution.
B. By describing a cultural phenomenon, exploring its negative effects, and ending with cautious hope for change.
C. By tracing the historical development of American work culture from the 1950s to the present day.
D. By using a series of personal anecdotes to build an emotional argument against overwork.

**Answer: B**

**解析**: 全文结构清晰：Para 1–2 以场景描写引入现象；Para 3–4 展示硅谷文化的扩散；Para 5–6 揭示其负面影响（工作与生活边界消失、假期政策适得其反）；Para 7–9 通过 Dan Lyons 等人的批评深挖问题根源；Para 10–11 以 Away 事件为例，提出变革的可能性，并以"reimagine a world where the office is just the office"结尾，表达谨慎的改变期望。

- **A 错误**：文章并非"两种对立观点+折中方案"的结构，而是在批判硅谷文化的主线下，最后提出一点反思与期望。
- **B 正确**：概括了文章"呈现现象→揭示问题→谨慎展望"的三段式论证逻辑。
- **C 错误**：文章并非历史线性叙述，1950 年代仅在最后一段被提及作对比，并非文章的组织框架。
- **D 错误**：文章主要依靠新闻报道、调查数据和专家观点，Dan Lyons 的经历只是其中一个例证，并非以个人故事为主的情感论证。
