**阅读原文**（关键段落摘录）

> **Para 1–2:** Growing up, most other teenagers I knew were wallpapering their rooms with pictures of superstars. But not me. My walls were plastered with high-fashion magazine spreads. For me, fashion was what life should be like, just amplified. A world worth diving into and living in, albeit through images. I spent years sitting on my bed, doing homework and glancing up at those fashion-plastered walls. But I never once gave even a single thought that fashion could be a career option.
>
> **Para 3:** Because I had always done well in science and my father was a reputable dentist, I decided to study to become a doctor. I was good with my hands, so my love of art sparked a more specific interest in plastic surgery — a specialty that, in my mind, combined the best of both worlds, beauty and science. I loved that a plastic surgeon was in the business of making people feel better about themselves, and I was certain that it was the right path for me.
>
> **Para 4:** After high school, I interned for a nearby plastic surgeon. I had a front-row seat in the operating room, watching cosmetic surgeries that all looked so perfect. That experience, plus my grades, earned me a full four-year merit scholarship to the University of Maryland. I declared my major in neurobiology and physiology and put up with eight a.m. labs four days a week. I took the dreaded MCATs during my junior year and landed a six-week summer internship at a hospital in Long Island. I was so excited that I'd been accepted into the program and confident that the experience would prove valuable and educational.
>
> **Para 6:** Our program was meant to show us what it was really like being a doctor, and each morning exposed us to difficult cases. By the time lunch came around, we couldn't wait to sit down together in the cafeteria to relax and reflect on our experiences that morning. Listening to the heated discussion, I slowly began to realize that I didn't share the passion or enthusiasm the others had for what we were doing. But how could that be? I loved everything I was learning in school and during my plastic surgery internship, but hated the practical applications of those lessons in a hospital. It didn't make sense. But an uncomfortable feeling started to worry me, and with every day that passed, it became more difficult to ignore.
>
> **Para 9:** Sticking with medicine would have been the easy path, actually. But when everything in your mind is screaming NO, you can't ignore it and you shouldn't ignore it. I couldn't live my life with regret. My dream had changed and it wasn't my fault. That's precisely why you intern: to test the water. So, I had to choose to shut the door on medicine.

**Questions**

---

**Question 1.** What does the narrator reveal about her relationship with fashion during her teenage years?

A. She actively pursued fashion as a hobby by designing her own clothes.
B. She admired fashion deeply through images but never considered it as a career.
C. She was inspired by fashion magazines to become a professional photographer.
D. She was less interested in fashion than her peers, who all wanted to work in the industry.

**Answer: B**

**解析**: Para 1–2 明确描述了叙述者的情况：她把时尚杂志贴满了墙（"My walls were plastered with high-fashion magazine spreads"），认为时尚是"a world worth diving into and living in"，但同时明确指出"I never once gave even a single thought that fashion could be a career option"（从未想过时尚可以成为职业选项）。

- **A 错误**：原文只提到她欣赏时尚杂志图片，没有提及她自己设计服装作为爱好。
- **B 正确**：与原文"I never once gave even a single thought that fashion could be a career option"完全对应，她欣赏但不曾将其视为职业可能。
- **C 错误**：原文的叙述者最终走向了时尚（fashion），而非摄影师，且原文没有提到摄影师 Arthur Elgort 对她职业选择的直接启发是在高中阶段。
- **D 错误**：原文恰恰相反——是其他青少年贴明星海报，而叙述者贴的是时尚杂志，说明她对时尚的热情高于同龄人，而非低于。

**Question 2.** According to Para 4, what were the TWO main factors that earned the narrator a merit scholarship to the University of Maryland?

A. Her internship experience and her performance on the MCATs.
B. Her high school grades and her internship at a plastic surgeon's office.
C. Her declared major in neurobiology and her four-day-a-week lab attendance.
D. Her recommendation letters and her volunteer work at a local hospital.

**Answer: B**

**解析**: Para 4 原文写道："That experience, plus my grades, earned me a full four-year merit scholarship to the University of Maryland."（那次实习经历，加上我的成绩，为我赢得了马里兰大学的四年全额奖学金。）"That experience"指的是在整形外科医生处的实习（"I interned for a nearby plastic surgeon"）。

- **A 错误**：MCAT 考试是在获得奖学金、进入大学之后的大三年级才参加的，并非获得奖学金的原因。
- **B 正确**：与原文"That experience（整形外科实习）, plus my grades（成绩）"一一对应。
- **C 错误**：神经生物学专业和实验室出勤是进入马里兰大学之后的事情，并非获得奖学金的因素。
- **D 错误**：原文完全没有提到推荐信或志愿者工作。

**Question 3.** The phrase "to test the water" in Para 9 most probably means ______.

A. to conduct scientific experiments in a laboratory setting
B. to assess a situation carefully before making a commitment
C. to practice swimming before entering a competitive race
D. to verify whether one's financial resources are sufficient for a career change

**Answer: B**

**解析**: Para 9 中叙述者说："That's precisely why you intern: to test the water."（实习的意义正在于此：试探虚实。）这里的"test the water"是常见英语习语，意为在正式投入之前先尝试了解情况，评估某件事是否适合自己。结合语境，实习就是在真正进入医学职业之前的"试探"。

- **A 错误**："test the water"是习语，与实验室科学实验无关，此选项对习语作了字面误读。
- **B 正确**：与习语"test the water/waters"的标准含义一致——在做出承诺之前谨慎评估情况。
- **C 错误**：同样是字面误读，将"water"理解为游泳的水，与语境无关。
- **D 错误**：原文谈论的是职业热情的匹配，而非财务资源的充足与否。

**Question 4.** It can be inferred from Para 6 that the narrator's discomfort with medicine stemmed mainly from ______.

A. her inability to understand the complex medical cases presented each morning.
B. the gap between her love of medical theory and her lack of enthusiasm for its real-world practice.
C. her conflict with other interns who were more passionate and competitive.
D. the physical demands of working long hospital hours starting at six a.m. each day.

**Answer: B**

**解析**: Para 6 写道："I loved everything I was learning in school and during my plastic surgery internship, but hated the practical applications of those lessons in a hospital."（我热爱在学校和整形外科实习中学到的一切，但讨厌在医院里实际应用这些知识。）这清晰地揭示了理论热情与实践厌恶之间的鸿沟。

- **A 错误**：原文没有说叙述者看不懂病例，她的问题是缺乏热情，而非能力不足。
- **B 正确**：与原文"loved everything I was learning…but hated the practical applications"精确对应。
- **C 错误**：原文提到其他实习生热情地讨论，但并未描述叙述者与他们存在冲突。
- **D 错误**：Para 5 提到早上六点起床，但Para 6 中叙述者的核心问题是热情的缺失，而非身体上的疲劳。

**Question 5.** What is the narrator's attitude toward her decision to leave medicine, as expressed in Para 9?

A. Deeply regretful, as she feels she wasted three years of her life.
B. Conflicted and uncertain, unable to decide what to do next.
C. Resolute and self-forgiving, accepting that her dream had legitimately changed.
D. Relieved but guilty, particularly about disappointing her parents.

**Answer: C**

**解析**: Para 9 叙述者表达了坚定的态度："I couldn't live my life with regret. My dream had changed and it wasn't my fault."（我无法带着遗憾生活，我的梦想改变了，这不是我的错。）"it wasn't my fault"体现了自我宽恕，"I had to choose to shut the door on medicine"体现了坚定的决心。

- **A 错误**：原文明确说"I couldn't live my life with regret"（我不能带着遗憾生活），表明她拒绝后悔，与"深感遗憾"相反。
- **B 错误**：虽然 Para 8 表达了内心挣扎，但 Para 9 是叙述者已经做出决定之后的陈述，她的态度是坚定的，而非犹豫不决。
- **C 正确**：与原文"it wasn't my fault"（自我宽恕）和"I had to choose to shut the door on medicine"（坚定决心）完全吻合。
- **D 错误**：叙述者并未在 Para 9 表达对父母的愧疚，对父母的担忧在后续段落（Para 10–14）才展开，且即便如此也没有"内疚"（guilty）的情绪。
