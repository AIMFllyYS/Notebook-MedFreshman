**阅读原文**（关键段落摘录）

> **Para 7:** I also hated that it didn't matter what I wore each day. I missed my clothes. It was like someone had cut my wings. Every Friday afternoon when I came home from the hospital, I couldn't wait to shower and pick out my outfit for the night. The medical wardrobe is clearly not a reason to give up on a profession, but I was looking for any excuse I could find to convince myself that the career path I was on was the wrong one.
>
> **Para 8:** I asked myself: Were these reasons big enough to quit medicine? I'd just spent three years working toward a career that I felt like I no longer wanted. But the thought of shifting gears so drastically seemed insane — and shifting them to what? Could I really walk away from a goal I'd had for such a long time? And what would I tell my parents?
>
> **Para 9:** Sticking with medicine would have been the easy path, actually. But when everything in your mind is screaming NO, you can't ignore it and you shouldn't ignore it. I couldn't live my life with regret. My dream had changed and it wasn't my fault. That's precisely why you intern: to test the water. So, I had to choose to shut the door on medicine.
>
> **Para 14:** With that comment I got up and left the room. My head was swirling. I had spilled the beans but I didn't feel any better. I was very anxious about my mother's question. Her concern was legitimate. After all, I had only a year of college left, yet I had no idea what it actually was that I wanted to do.
>
> **Para 15:** Weeks later, when I was back at school sitting on my bed in my apartment, I glanced at my shelf, where a copy of Arthur Elgort's Models Manual was lying. I bought that book in my senior year of high school. Elgort, a fashion photographer, filled the pages with stunning images of supermodels. Completely unreachable, awe-inspiring beauty. I had flipped through that book countless times before. But something was different now. Something felt more important. Maybe I could do this.
>
> **Para 16:** I suddenly flashed back to my magazine-wallpapered room and it all became crystal clear. Those magazine pages had always been there, right under my nose: Fashion was the answer.

**Questions**

---

**Question 1.** What is the main idea of this passage?

A. A student's academic journey through medical school and her eventual graduation as a doctor.
B. A personal account of discovering one's true career path after following a misdirected dream.
C. A comparative study of careers in medicine versus careers in the fashion industry.
D. An argument for why internships are more valuable than formal university education.

**Answer: B**

**解析**: 全文是叙述者第一人称回忆录：她受父亲影响选择学医，经过高中实习、大学奖学金、医院实习之后，发现自己对医学缺乏热情；历经内心挣扎和告知父母的痛苦，最终被一本时尚摄影书触发回忆，顿悟时尚才是真正的答案。全文主旨是"在走错路之后找到真正的职业方向"。

- **A 错误**：叙述者最终放弃了医学，并没有成为医生。
- **B 正确**：涵盖了全文核心——错误路径（医学）→ 发现真相（时尚）的个人成长叙事。
- **C 错误**：文章并非对两种职业的系统比较，而是一位叙述者的个人经历。
- **D 错误**：实习是叙述者发现"错误"的手段，但文章并未以此为论点进行论证。

**Question 2.** In Para 7, the narrator says "it was like someone had cut my wings." This expression most likely suggests that ______.

A. she had suffered a physical injury that prevented her from working efficiently.
B. she felt that the medical environment was suppressing a fundamental part of her identity.
C. her colleagues at the hospital were jealous of her ambition and tried to hold her back.
D. the hospital's strict dress code had caused her emotional distress unrelated to her career.

**Answer: B**

**解析**: "cut my wings"（剪掉翅膀）是比喻表达，暗指被剥夺了自由或个性表达的机会。结合 Para 7 的语境，叙述者因为穿手术服、无法穿自己的衣服而感到失去自我，暗示医学环境压制了她身份认同中至关重要的一部分——对美与时尚的热爱。

- **A 错误**："wings"是比喻，并非身体受伤，原文也没有任何身体受伤的描述。
- **B 正确**：与整段的情感逻辑一致——穿统一的手术服代表着她无法表达自己的个性和热爱，医学环境压制了她的本真自我。
- **C 错误**：原文没有提到任何同事嫉妒或阻碍她，整段只有叙述者自己的内心独白。
- **D 错误**：叙述者自己也承认"The medical wardrobe is clearly not a reason to give up on a profession"（着装问题显然不是放弃职业的理由），说明她意识到衣服只是一个借口，深层问题是职业方向错误，而非单纯的着装情绪困扰。

**Question 3.** The phrase "spilled the beans" in Para 14 most probably means ______.

A. accidentally knocked over food in the hospital cafeteria during lunch.
B. revealed a secret or disclosed information that was previously kept private.
C. made a serious mistake that damaged her relationship with her parents.
D. completed a difficult task that had been weighing on her mind for a long time.

**Answer: B**

**解析**: "spill the beans"是英语固定习语，意为"泄露秘密、说出隐情"。Para 14 的语境是叙述者刚刚向父母坦白了她不想继续学医的想法（即 Para 10–13 的对话），之后感叹"I had spilled the beans but I didn't feel any better"（我说出来了，但并没有感觉好些）。这里"the beans"指的是她长期压在心里的"不想学医"这一秘密。

- **A 错误**：对习语的字面曲解，将"beans"理解为真正的豆子，与语境完全脱节。
- **B 正确**：与"spill the beans"的标准习语含义一致，且与段落语境完全吻合。
- **C 错误**：虽然她向父母坦白确实令她焦虑，但"spill the beans"本身不含"犯错误"的意思，也没有描述亲子关系受损。
- **D 错误**："spill the beans"侧重于"说出/披露"的行为，而非"完成艰难任务"的意涵。

**Question 4.** What triggered the narrator's final realization that fashion was her true calling?

A. A conversation with her mother about the need to find a new direction.
B. A visit to a fashion exhibit that reminded her of her teenage passion.
C. Seeing Arthur Elgort's Models Manual on her shelf and flashing back to her magazine-covered walls.
D. Reading a fashion magazine article that listed careers in the industry.

**Answer: C**

**解析**: Para 15 描述了叙述者回到学校宿舍，偶然看到书架上一本高中购买的《Models Manual》，翻看其中的时尚摄影图片后，感到"something was different now. Something felt more important. Maybe I could do this."随后 Para 16 写道，她"flashed back to my magazine-wallpapered room"，一切豁然开朗。触发点正是那本书和由此引发的童年记忆。

- **A 错误**：母亲的问题（"But what are you going to do?"）让她焦虑，并未触发顿悟，顿悟发生在数周之后她独自在宿舍时。
- **B 错误**：原文没有提到任何时尚展览，叙述者是在宿舍房间里独自看书时顿悟的。
- **C 正确**：与 Para 15–16 的叙事完全对应——书→回忆童年贴满杂志的墙→"Fashion was the answer"。
- **D 错误**：触发叙述者顿悟的是一本时尚摄影书（Models Manual），而非一篇杂志文章；原文也没有提到任何列出时尚职业的文章。

**Question 5.** The ending of Para 16 — "Those magazine pages had always been there, right under my nose: Fashion was the answer" — implies which of the following about the narrator's journey?

A. She wasted many years because she failed to notice obvious signs pointing her to fashion early on.
B. The clues to her true passion had always existed in her life, but she only recognized them in retrospect.
C. She regrets not having studied fashion design instead of attending the University of Maryland.
D. Fashion magazines were the most important educational resource throughout her academic career.

**Answer: B**

**解析**: "right under my nose"（就在眼皮底下）意指那些线索一直都在，只是她当时没有意识到。Para 1–2 描述了她青少年时代贴满时尚杂志的墙，Para 15 描述高中时买的时尚摄影书。这些"线索"始终存在，只有经历了错误的职业探索之后，她才得以回望并理解其意义。结尾的"crystal clear"（豁然开朗）传达了一种顿悟感，而非悔恨。

- **A 错误**："wasted many years"带有强烈的悔恨含义，但原文结尾的情绪是顿悟与释然（"it all became crystal clear"），而非悔恨。Para 9 中叙述者也明确说"I couldn't live my life with regret"。
- **B 正确**：与"right under my nose"的含义完全对应——线索早已存在，只是在回望时才看清。这正是这类"成长发现"叙事的经典结构。
- **C 错误**：原文中叙述者并没有对就读马里兰大学或学习神经生物学表示悔恨，在大学的经历（实习）正是帮助她认清自己的途径。
- **D 错误**：原文没有将时尚杂志定性为"最重要的教育资源"，这是对"那些杂志页面一直都在"这一表达的过度引申。
