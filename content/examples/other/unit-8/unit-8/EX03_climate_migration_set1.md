## 阅读原文

> Climate change is no longer a distant threat — it is a present reality, and one of its most visible consequences is the displacement of human populations. Rising sea levels, prolonged droughts, intensifying storms, and changing rainfall patterns are making some regions of the world increasingly uninhabitable. The World Bank estimates that, without urgent action, climate change could displace more than 200 million people by 2050. These climate migrants are not fleeing war or persecution — they are fleeing the environment itself.
>
> Climate migration takes many forms. In the Pacific Islands, rising seas are swallowing entire communities, forcing residents to relocate to higher ground or abandon their homelands altogether. In the Sahel region of Africa, desertification is destroying farmland, driving rural populations to cities that are already struggling to provide basic services. In Bangladesh, saltwater intrusion from rising seas is contaminating drinking water and destroying rice paddies, pushing farmers to migrate to urban centers. In each case, the pattern is the same: environmental degradation destroys livelihoods, and people move to survive.
>
> The consequences of climate migration are complex and far-reaching. When large populations move, they place enormous pressure on host communities, straining infrastructure, housing, and resources. In some cases, climate migration can exacerbate existing social and political tensions, potentially leading to conflict. Yet climate migrants often fall through the cracks of international law, as the 1951 Refugee Convention does not recognize environmental factors as grounds for refugee status.

---

**1.** What is the main idea of the passage?

A. Climate change has no effect on human migration patterns.

B. Climate change is driving mass human displacement, with complex consequences and inadequate legal protection.

C. Climate migrants are the same as traditional refugees fleeing war.

D. Climate migration only affects island nations.

**Answer: B**

**解析**（主旨大意题）：文章第一段介绍气候移民的规模，第二段展示不同形式，第三段分析后果和法律缺失。A项与全文核心矛盾；C项与"not fleeing war or persecution — they are fleeing the environment itself"矛盾；D项"only island nations"与萨赫勒和孟加拉国的案例矛盾。B项准确概括了全文三个层面的主旨。

---

**2.** According to the World Bank estimate cited in Paragraph 1, how many people could be displaced by climate change by 2050?

A. 2 million
B. 20 million
C. 200 million
D. 2 billion

**Answer: C**

**解析**（细节理解题）：第一段明确指出"climate change could displace more than 200 million people by 2050"。A、B、D项的数字均与原文不符。C项与原文完全一致。

---

**3.** According to Paragraph 2, what is the common pattern across all three cases of climate migration?

A. People migrate because of political persecution.

B. Environmental degradation destroys livelihoods, and people move to survive.

C. People voluntarily choose to move for better job opportunities.

D. Governments force people to relocate against their will.

**Answer: B**

**解析**（细节理解题）：第二段最后一句明确总结"In each case, the pattern is the same: environmental degradation destroys livelihoods, and people move to survive"。A项与"not fleeing war or persecution"矛盾；C项"voluntarily choose"与"forcing residents"和"pushing farmers"矛盾；D项"governments force"在文中未提及。B项与原文完全一致。

---

**4.** The phrase "fall through the cracks of international law" (Paragraph 3) suggests that climate migrants

A. are well protected by existing international law

B. are not recognized or protected by current international legal frameworks

C. have broken international laws

D. are hiding from law enforcement

**Answer: B**

**解析**（短语理解题）：*fall through the cracks* 意为"被遗漏、未被覆盖"，后文解释"the 1951 Refugee Convention does not recognize environmental factors as grounds for refugee status"。A项与原文矛盾；C项"broken laws"与原文无关；D项"hiding"与原文无关。B项 *not recognized or protected by current international legal frameworks* 准确解释了短语的含义。

---

**5.** What can be inferred about the 1951 Refugee Convention from the passage?

A. It provides comprehensive protection for all types of migrants.

B. It was designed before climate change became a significant factor and does not address environmental displacement.

C. It explicitly excludes all non-European refugees.

D. It has been recently updated to include climate migrants.

**Answer: B**

**解析**（推理判断题）：第三段指出"the 1951 Refugee Convention does not recognize environmental factors as grounds for refugee status"，暗示该公约制定时（1951年）气候变化尚未成为移民因素，因此未涵盖环境流离失所。A项与"does not recognize"矛盾；C项"excludes all non-European"过于绝对，虽公约最初限于欧洲但后来扩展；D项"recently updated"与原文矛盾。B项为合理推断。
