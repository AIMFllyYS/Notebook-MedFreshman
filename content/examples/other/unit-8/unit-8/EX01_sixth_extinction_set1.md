:::example{label=EX01 · The Sixth Extinction — Set 1}

## 阅读原文

> Earth has experienced five mass extinction events in its 4.5-billion-year history. The most famous, the Cretaceous extinction 66 million years ago, wiped out the dinosaurs and nearly 75% of all species. Each of these events was caused by natural catastrophes — asteroid impacts, volcanic eruptions, or climate shifts. Today, scientists warn that we are in the midst of a sixth mass extinction, and this time the cause is not an asteroid or a volcano. It is us.
>
> The current extinction rate is staggering. According to the IPBES, approximately one million species face extinction, many within decades. The primary drivers are well documented: habitat destruction, overexploitation, pollution, invasive species, and climate change. What makes the sixth extinction unique is not its causes — which are familiar — but its pace. Species that evolved over millions of years are disappearing in a single human lifetime, a speed that leaves no time for evolutionary adaptation.
>
> The consequences of biodiversity loss extend far beyond the disappearance of individual species. Ecosystems depend on complex webs of interaction, and when key species are removed, the effects cascade throughout the system. The loss of pollinators threatens global food production. The degradation of coral reefs endangers the livelihoods of 500 million people. Biodiversity is not merely a matter of preserving beautiful animals — it is the infrastructure of life itself.

---

**1.** What is the main idea of the passage?

A. Mass extinctions are natural events that occur regularly throughout Earth's history.

B. Human activity is driving a sixth mass extinction at an unprecedented pace, with consequences for all life on Earth.

C. The Cretaceous extinction was the most destructive event in Earth's history.

D. Biodiversity loss only affects wild animals, not humans.

**Answer: B**

**解析**（主旨大意题）：文章第一段将第六次灭绝与前五次对比，指出"the cause is... us"；第二段强调灭绝速度之快；第三段阐述后果的系统性。A项只概括了前五次灭绝的背景，忽略了第六次的核心论点；C项白垩纪灭绝只是例证，非主旨；D项与第三段"endangers the livelihoods of 500 million people"矛盾。B项准确概括全文主旨。

---

**2.** According to the passage, what makes the sixth extinction different from the previous five?

A. It is caused by an asteroid impact.

B. It is caused by human activity and occurs at a much faster pace.

C. It only affects marine species.

D. It is less severe than the previous extinctions.

**Answer: B**

**解析**（细节理解题）：第一段指出前五次由自然灾难引起，第六次"It is us"；第二段指出"What makes the sixth extinction unique is not its causes... but its pace"。A项与前五次的原因混淆；C项"only marine species"与原文矛盾；D项"less severe"与"one million species face extinction"矛盾。B项准确概括了两个区别：原因（人类活动）和速度（更快）。

---

**3.** The word "staggering" in "The current extinction rate is staggering" (Paragraph 2) is closest in meaning to

A. slowly decreasing
B. shockingly high
C. relatively stable
D. difficult to measure

**Answer: B**

**解析**（词义猜测题）：*staggering* 意为"令人震惊的"，在此形容灭绝速率之高令人难以置信。A项"slowly decreasing"方向相反；C项"relatively stable"与"one million species face extinction"矛盾；D项"difficult to measure"与原文"well documented"矛盾。B项 *shockingly high* 最接近原文含义。

---

**4.** What does the author mean by "Biodiversity is not merely a matter of preserving beautiful animals — it is the infrastructure of life itself"?

A. Biodiversity only matters because animals are beautiful.

B. Biodiversity is essential for the functioning of life-supporting systems, not just an aesthetic concern.

C. Infrastructure projects should focus on building animal sanctuaries.

D. Beautiful animals are more important than other species.

**Answer: B**

**解析**（句意理解题）：*not merely... it is...* 结构将生物多样性从审美问题（preserving beautiful animals）提升为生存基础设施（infrastructure of life itself）。A项与 *not merely* 矛盾；C项将"infrastructure"字面化，偏离比喻含义；D项"more important"与原文精神矛盾。B项 *essential for the functioning of life-supporting systems, not just an aesthetic concern* 准确解释了句意。

---

**5.** According to Paragraph 3, the loss of pollinators such as bees and butterflies

A. has no effect on human society

B. threatens global food production

C. only affects the beauty of gardens

D. is caused by climate change alone

**Answer: B**

**解析**（细节理解题）：第三段明确指出"The loss of pollinators, such as bees and butterflies, threatens global food production"。A项与原文矛盾；C项"beauty of gardens"缩小了影响范围；D项"climate change alone"过于绝对，第二段列举了五个驱动因素。B项与原文完全一致。
