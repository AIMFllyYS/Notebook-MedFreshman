:::example{label=EX04 · Climate Change and Human Migration — Set 2}

## 阅读原文

> Addressing climate migration requires both mitigation and adaptation. Mitigation means reducing the greenhouse gas emissions that drive climate change, thereby slowing the environmental changes that force people to move. Adaptation means helping vulnerable communities prepare for and cope with the impacts that are already inevitable — building seawalls, developing drought-resistant crops, and creating planned relocation programs for communities that cannot be saved. Both strategies are essential: mitigation addresses the root causes, while adaptation addresses the consequences. Neither alone is sufficient.
>
> The story of climate migration is ultimately a story about justice. Those who contribute least to climate change — the poorest people in the poorest nations — are often the ones most affected by its consequences. A farmer in Bangladesh produces a tiny fraction of the carbon emissions of an average American, yet it is the farmer who loses his land to rising seas. Addressing this injustice requires international cooperation on a scale humanity has never achieved: wealthy nations must reduce emissions dramatically, provide financial support for adaptation in vulnerable countries, and develop legal frameworks to protect those who are displaced. The alternative — a world where hundreds of millions of climate migrants have nowhere to go — is too dire to contemplate.
>
> Progress is being made, though slowly. The Paris Agreement commits nations to limit warming to well below 2°C, and the Green Climate Fund provides support to developing countries for both mitigation and adaptation. However, current pledges fall far short of what is needed. The gap between what science demands and what politics delivers remains the greatest obstacle to addressing climate migration effectively.

---

**1.** According to Paragraph 1, what is the difference between mitigation and adaptation?

A. Mitigation addresses the root causes of climate change, while adaptation addresses its consequences.

B. Mitigation is more important than adaptation.

C. Mitigation involves building seawalls, while adaptation involves reducing emissions.

D. Mitigation is for wealthy nations, while adaptation is for poor nations.

**Answer: A**

**解析**（细节理解题）：第一段明确指出"mitigation addresses the root causes, while adaptation addresses the consequences"。B项"more important"与"Both strategies are essential"和"Neither alone is sufficient"矛盾；C项将两者内容颠倒（seawalls是adaptation，reducing emissions是mitigation）；D项"for wealthy/poor"在文中未提及。A项与原文完全一致。

---

**2.** The example of "a farmer in Bangladesh" in Paragraph 2 is used to illustrate

A. that Bangladesh is the only country affected by climate change

B. the injustice that those who contribute least to climate change are most affected

C. that farmers produce more carbon emissions than average Americans

D. that Bangladesh has effective adaptation strategies

**Answer: B**

**解析**（推理判断题）：第二段指出"Those who contribute least to climate change... are often the ones most affected"，随后以孟加拉国农民为例："produces a tiny fraction of the carbon emissions of an average American, yet it is the farmer who loses his land"。A项"only country"过于绝对；C项与"tiny fraction"矛盾；D项"effective adaptation"在文中未提及。B项准确概括了例证的含义。

---

**3.** According to Paragraph 2, what must wealthy nations do to address climate injustice?

A. Nothing, since they are not affected by climate change.

B. Reduce emissions dramatically, provide financial support for adaptation, and develop legal frameworks for displaced people.

C. Close their borders to all climate migrants.

D. Take over the governments of vulnerable countries.

**Answer: B**

**解析**（细节理解题）：第二段明确列举了富裕国家的三项义务："reduce emissions dramatically, provide financial support for adaptation in vulnerable countries, and develop legal frameworks to protect those who are displaced"。A项与全文精神矛盾；C项"close borders"与"develop legal frameworks to protect"矛盾；D项"take over governments"在文中完全未提及。B项与原文完全一致。

---

**4.** What does the author identify as "the greatest obstacle to addressing climate migration effectively" in Paragraph 3?

A. Lack of scientific understanding of climate change.

B. The gap between what science demands and what politics delivers.

C. The refusal of developing countries to cooperate.

D. The absence of technology to reduce emissions.

**Answer: B**

**解析**（细节理解题）：第三段最后一句明确指出"The gap between what science demands and what politics delivers remains the greatest obstacle"。A项"lack of scientific understanding"与"what science demands"矛盾（科学已有明确要求）；C项"refusal of developing countries"在文中未提及；D项"absence of technology"与原文矛盾（技术存在但政治意愿不足）。B项与原文完全一致。

---

**5.** The author's overall tone in this passage can best be described as

A. completely hopeless and despairing
B. urgent but acknowledging progress, with a call for greater action
C. optimistic that the problem is already solved
D. indifferent and detached from the issue

**Answer: B**

**解析**（观点态度题）：作者在第一段提出解决方案，第二段强调正义和紧迫性，第三段承认"Progress is being made, though slowly"但指出"current pledges fall far short"。A项"completely hopeless"与"Progress is being made"矛盾；C项"already solved"与"fall far short"矛盾；D项"indifferent"与全文的紧迫语气矛盾。B项 *urgent but acknowledging progress, with a call for greater action* 准确描述了作者的态度。
