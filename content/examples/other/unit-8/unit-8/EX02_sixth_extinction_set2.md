## 阅读原文

> The question is not whether we can stop the sixth extinction — we cannot, for many species are already beyond saving. The question is whether we can slow it down enough to preserve the essential functions of Earth's ecosystems. This requires urgent action on multiple fronts: protecting remaining habitats, reducing carbon emissions, transforming agriculture and fishing practices, and combating the illegal wildlife trade. Each of these actions is challenging, but together they offer a path toward what scientists call "bending the curve" — slowing the rate of biodiversity loss and giving ecosystems time to adapt.
>
> Ultimately, the sixth extinction is not just an environmental crisis — it is a moral one. It raises questions about what kind of species we want to be: one that destroys the web of life that sustains it, or one that learns to live in harmony with the natural world. The choices we make in the next few decades will determine not only the fate of millions of species but the legacy we leave for future generations. For the first time in Earth's history, a single species has the power to cause a mass extinction — and the power to prevent one. Whether we use that power wisely is the defining question of our age.
>
> Some scientists argue that technology may offer solutions that were unavailable in previous extinction events. Gene banking, captive breeding programs, and rewilding initiatives are already being used to save endangered species. Artificial intelligence and satellite monitoring are helping track deforestation and illegal fishing in real time. Yet technology alone cannot solve a problem that is fundamentally about human values and choices.

---

**1.** According to Paragraph 1, what is the realistic goal for addressing the sixth extinction?

A. Completely stopping all species extinction.

B. Slowing the extinction rate enough to preserve essential ecosystem functions.

C. Cloning extinct species to restore biodiversity.

D. Waiting for nature to recover on its own.

**Answer: B**

**解析**（细节理解题）：第一段明确指出"The question is not whether we can stop the sixth extinction — we cannot"和"The question is whether we can slow it down enough to preserve the essential functions"。A项与"we cannot"矛盾；C项"cloning"在第一段未提及；D项"waiting for nature"与"urgent action"矛盾。B项与原文完全一致。

---

**2.** The phrase "bending the curve" in Paragraph 1 refers to

A. completely reversing the trend of biodiversity loss

B. slowing the rate of biodiversity loss to give ecosystems time to adapt

C. creating a graph to track extinction rates

D. building physical barriers to protect endangered species

**Answer: B**

**解析**（短语理解题）：第一段将"bending the curve"定义为"slowing the rate of biodiversity loss and giving ecosystems time to adapt"。A项"completely reversing"与"we cannot stop"矛盾；C项"creating a graph"过于字面化；D项"physical barriers"偏离比喻含义。B项准确解释了"bending the curve"的含义。

---

**3.** What does the author mean by "the sixth extinction is not just an environmental crisis — it is a moral one"?

A. Environmental crises are less important than moral crises.

B. The extinction crisis raises fundamental ethical questions about humanity's relationship with nature.

C. Moral people do not cause environmental crises.

D. The crisis is caused by immoral individuals, not systems.

**Answer: B**

**解析**（句意理解题）：*not just... it is...* 递进结构将环境危机提升为道德危机，后文以"what kind of species we want to be"的设问展开伦理思考。A项"less important"偏离原文的递进关系；C项和D项过于简化，原文讨论的是人类整体的道德选择而非个体道德。B项 *raises fundamental ethical questions about humanity's relationship with nature* 准确解释了句意。

---

**4.** According to Paragraph 3, how is technology being used to address biodiversity loss?

A. Technology is completely useless in addressing extinction.

B. Gene banking, captive breeding, AI monitoring, and satellite tracking are being used.

C. Technology has already solved the extinction crisis.

D. Technology is only used to create new species.

**Answer: B**

**解析**（细节理解题）：第三段列举了多种技术应用：gene banking、captive breeding programs、rewilding initiatives、artificial intelligence、satellite monitoring。A项与原文矛盾；C项"already solved"与"technology alone cannot solve"矛盾；D项"create new species"在文中未提及。B项准确概括了第三段列举的技术应用。

---

**5.** What is the author's overall message in this passage?

A. The sixth extinction is unstoppable, so there is no point in trying.

B. While we cannot stop the sixth extinction, we can slow it through urgent action, and the moral imperative to do so is the defining question of our age.

C. Technology alone will solve the biodiversity crisis.

D. Only governments are responsible for addressing extinction.

**Answer: B**

**解析**（主旨大意题）：文章第一段提出减缓目标，第二段将问题提升为道德选择，第三段承认技术的作用但强调"technology alone cannot solve"价值问题。A项"no point in trying"与全文行动导向矛盾；C项与"technology alone cannot solve"矛盾；D项"only governments"过于狭隘，原文强调的是全人类的选择。B项准确概括了全文的平衡立场和道德呼吁。
