## 阅读原文

> **The Commencement Speech You'll Never Hear** — Jacob Neusner
>
> We the faculty take no pride in our educational achievement with you. We have prepared you for a world that does not exist, indeed, that cannot exist. You have spent four years supposing that failure leaves no record. You have learned at Brown that when your work goes poorly, the painless solution is to drop out. But starting now, in the world to which you go, failure marks you. Confronting difficulty by quitting leaves you changed. Outside Brown, quitters are no heroes.
>
> With us you could argue about why errors were not errors, why mediocre work really was excellent, why you could take pride in routine and slipshod presentations. Most of you, after all, can look back on honor grades for most of what you have done. So, here grades can have meant little in distinguishing the excellent from the ordinary. But tomorrow, in the world to which you go, you had better not defend errors but learn from them. You will be ill-advised to demand praise for what does not deserve it, and abuse those who do not give it.
>
> For years we have created an altogether forgiving world, in which whatever slight effort you gave was all that was needed. When you did not keep appointments, we made new ones. When your work came in beyond the deadline, we pretended not to care.
>
> Worse still, when you were boring, we acted as if you were saying something important. When you were garrulous and talked to hear yourselves talk, we listened as if it mattered. When you tossed on our desks writing upon which you had not labored, we read it and even responded, as though you earned a response. When you were dull, we pretended you were smart. When you were predictable, unimaginative, and routine, we listened as if to new and wonderful things. When you demanded free lunch, we served it. And all this why?
>
> Despite your fantasies, it was not even that we wanted to be liked by you. It was that we did not want to be bothered, and the easy way out was pretense: smiles and easy Bs.
>
> "College has spoiled you by reading papers that don't deserve to be read, listening to comments that don't deserve a hearing, paying attention even to the lazy, ill-informed and rude. We had to do it, for the sake of education. But nobody will ever do it again. College has deprived you of adequate preparation for the last 50 years. It has failed you by being easy, free, forgiving, attentive, comfortable, interesting, unchallenging fun. Good luck tomorrow."
>
> That is why, on this commencement day, we have nothing in which to take much pride.
>
> Oh, yes, there is one more thing. Try not to act toward your co-workers and bosses as you have acted toward us. I mean, when they give you what you want but have not earned, don't abuse them, insult them, act out with them your parlous relationships with your parents. This too we have tolerated. It was, as I said, not to be liked. Few professors actually care whether or not they are liked by peer-paralyzed adolescents, fools so shallow as to imagine professors care not about education but about popularity. It was, again, to be rid of you. So go, unlearn the lies we taught you. To life!

**1.** What is the author's primary purpose in delivering this speech?

A. To congratulate the graduating students on four years of hard work and academic achievement.

B. To warn students that the real world will not be as lenient and forgiving as their college experience has been.

C. To criticize the university's grading system and advocate for stricter academic policies.

D. To express the faculty's disappointment in individual students who dropped out of courses.

**Answer: B**

**解析**：本题为**主旨大意题**，考查作者写作目的。

**正确答案 B** 的依据：文章开篇即点明主旨——"We have prepared you for a world that does not exist"（第1段），并在随后各段反复强调大学营造了一个"altogether forgiving world"，而现实世界并非如此。作者用对比手法将大学环境（宽容、纵容、不惩罚失败）与即将面对的真实世界（"failure marks you"，"quitters are no heroes"）进行对照，意在警示学生真实世界不会重复大学的宽纵待遇。

**A 选项错误**：文章第一句便是"We the faculty take no pride in our educational achievement with you"，明确否定了"恭喜"或"为你们骄傲"的立场，与A项完全相悖。

**C 选项错误**：作者批评的是大学整体的宽纵文化，而非专门针对"评分制度"。文中提到"grades can have meant little"，但这只是宽纵现象之一，并非作者要"倡导更严格评分政策"的核心诉求，超出文本范围。

**D 选项错误**：文章批评的是普遍的学生群体（"most of you"），并非"individual students who dropped out"。作者是在批判一种系统性、制度性的纵容，而非针对某些具体个人的退课行为。

**2.** According to paragraph 3, how did the faculty respond when students missed appointments?

A. They reported the absences to the academic administration.

B. They deducted points from the students' final grades.

C. They rescheduled the appointments without complaint.

D. They sent written warnings to the students involved.

**Answer: C**

**解析**：本题为**细节理解题**，考查对第3段具体信息的提取。

**正确答案 C** 的依据：原文第3段明确写道："When you did not keep appointments, we made new ones."（当你们不履约时，我们另约新时间。）这正是选项C"rescheduled the appointments without complaint（重新安排约会，且未作任何抱怨）"的准确对应。

**A 选项错误**：原文中完全没有提及向学校管理部门汇报缺席的内容。此选项属于无中生有的干扰项。

**B 选项错误**：原文不仅未提及扣分，反而在下文（第5段）提到教授们给出"easy Bs"，说明成绩处理是宽松而非惩罚性的，与扣分相反。

**D 选项错误**：原文没有任何关于"书面警告"的描述。这是一个合理但完全不符合文本的干扰项，利用读者对常见学校规章的联想来制造迷惑。

**3.** The word "garrulous" (paragraph 4) most probably means __________.

A. aggressive and confrontational in debate

B. excessively talkative, especially about trivial matters

C. confused and unable to express ideas clearly

D. shy and reluctant to participate in discussion

**Answer: B**

**解析**：本题为**词义猜测题**，考查根据上下文推断词义的能力。

**正确答案 B** 的依据：第4段原文为："When you were garrulous and talked to hear yourselves talk, we listened as if it mattered."关键线索是紧随其后的同位短语"talked to hear yourselves talk"（说话是为了听自己说话），这形象地描绘了一种沉迷于自我表达、话语内容却无实质意义的状态。结合"we listened as if it mattered"（我们假装这些话有意义地倾听），说明"garrulous"指的是话多而内容空洞，即**喋喋不休、废话连篇**，与B项"过度健谈，尤其是谈论琐事"吻合。

**A 选项错误**："aggressive and confrontational"（好斗、对抗性强）与语境不符。文中语境是课堂上学生滔滔不绝地自说自话，并非描述争论或对抗行为。

**C 选项错误**："confused and unable to express ideas clearly"（混乱、无法清晰表达）与"garrulous"意思相反。garrulous的人表达流畅，只是话太多，而非表达不清。

**D 选项错误**："shy and reluctant to participate"（害羞、不愿参与）与文意完全相反。能"talked to hear yourselves talk"的学生显然并不沉默内敛。

**4.** According to paragraph 5, the real reason why professors gave students "easy Bs" was that __________.

A. they genuinely believed the students deserved high grades for their efforts.

B. they hoped students would remember them fondly after graduation.

C. they wanted to avoid the trouble of dealing with underperforming students.

D. they were following the university's official policy on grade inflation.

**Answer: C**

**解析**：本题为**细节推断题**，考查对作者明确陈述动机的理解。

**正确答案 C** 的依据：第5段原文写道："Despite your fantasies, it was not even that we wanted to be liked by you. It was that we did not want to be bothered, and the easy way out was pretense: smiles and easy Bs."（尽管你们幻想如此，我们甚至并非想得到你们的喜欢。而是我们不想被麻烦，最省事的办法就是假装：笑脸和轻松给出的B。）"did not want to be bothered"直接对应C项"避免麻烦"的含义。

**A 选项错误**：原文第5段用"Despite your fantasies"暗示学生的期望是幻觉，并且通篇强调学生的作品"不值得被阅读"、努力程度不足，教授并不真心认为学生配得上高分。

**B 选项错误**：第5段开头明确否认了这一点——"it was not even that we wanted to be liked by you"，直接推翻了B项的说法。

**D 选项错误**：文中完全没有提及大学官方政策或"成绩通胀政策"（grade inflation policy），属于无中生有的干扰项。

**5.** What is the author's attitude toward the students at the end of the speech (paragraph 9)?

A. Warm and encouraging, wishing them success in the professional world.

B. Sarcastic and dismissive, revealing that the faculty's tolerance was purely self-serving.

C. Regretful and apologetic, feeling guilty for not having challenged students more.

D. Neutral and detached, simply offering practical advice for workplace behavior.

**Answer: B**

**解析**：本题为**态度立场题**，考查对作者语气和情感立场的整体判断。

**正确答案 B** 的依据：第9段结尾处，作者揭示教授们容忍学生恶劣行为的真正原因——"It was, again, to be rid of you."（还是为了摆脱你们。）并以"So go, unlearn the lies we taught you. To life!"收尾，语气尖锐讽刺。全文反复用反语与讽刺手法，如"we listened as if it mattered""we pretended you were smart"等，表明作者对学生的态度是**讽刺而轻蔑**的，同时揭示教授的宽纵完全出于自私（省事、摆脱麻烦），并非真正关心学生。

**A 选项错误**：文章通篇没有任何温情或鼓励的语气。"Good luck tomorrow"（引用Daniel教授语）表面像是祝福，但结合上下文（"College has failed you"）明显是反语。

**C 选项错误**：作者并未表现出"愧疚"或"道歉"的情感。他用的是指责性和揭露性语气，将责任更多地归咎于学生的懒惰和自以为是，而非对教授们的自我批评。

**D 选项错误**："neutral and detached"（中立、超然）与全文强烈的讽刺语气和情感色彩完全不符。作者措辞激烈，使用"fools so shallow""peer-paralyzed adolescents"等极具情感倾向的词汇，绝非中立。
