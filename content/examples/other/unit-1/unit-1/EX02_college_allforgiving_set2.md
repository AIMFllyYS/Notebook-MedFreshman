## 阅读原文

> **College: An All-Forgiving World?** — Ida Timothee
>
> In "The commencement speech you'll never hear," Jacob Neusner argues that we have been made to believe, according to our college experience, that "failure leaves no record" and that things can be easily achieved. It seems to Neusner that college is not a good preparatory school for life because it is making us ready "for a world that does not exist."
>
> There's no doubt that Neusner should have taken a closer look at what college life is really like before formulating such a strong opinion about it. He is completely ignoring all the pressures and hard times students go through to make it at college. It is not the way he describes it at all.
>
> Is college not preparing us for real life, as Neusner puts it? Is what we are experiencing something not useful to learn for the real world? These are the questions that pop into my mind when I think about what Neusner says. I think that he is very wrong. The college years, for many of us, are when we start to be independent, make crucial decisions on our own, and become responsible for them. At college, we must learn to budget our time (and money!) and to be tolerant (otherwise we couldn't survive in a crowded dorm room!). We meet people from different parts of the world that broaden our view of the world itself and help us understand each other better. If these things are not useful for the real world, then I don't know what could be.
>
> Neusner believes that in college we are trained to think that "failure leaves no record" because we can supposedly get away with mistakes easily. I have news for him. If you fail a test, you can't take it again, and the teacher won't erase the grade even if he thinks you will hate him for the rest of your life. If you drop out of a class, next semester you will have to take more courses. If you get low grades, your chances of getting into a fine graduate school are almost none. If your grade point average is not reasonably high for a number of classes, you just don't get your degree. When midterms and finals come, no one can avoid taking them. When the going gets tough, the tough have to get down to business because, unlike what Neusner believes, college does not give "painless" solutions to mistakes. It is not "an altogether forgiving world," and by no means have teachers "pretended not to care" when deadlines are not kept or when things aren't done at the time they are supposed to be.
>
> To me, living in a crowded dorm room, having a one-day reading period before finals, tons of reading, papers, and midterms due the same week are not exactly my idea of "easy, free, forgiving, attentive, comfortable, interesting, unchallenging fun."

---

**1.** What is Ida Timothee's main argument in this passage?

A. She agrees with Neusner that college students are lazy and unprepared for real life.

B. She contends that Neusner's portrayal of college life is inaccurate and overlooks real challenges students face.

C. She argues that universities should reform their grading systems to better reflect student performance.

D. She believes that college teaches students the wrong values, just as Neusner suggests.

**Answer: B**

**解析**：本题为**主旨大意题**，考查对作者核心立场的整体把握。

**正确答案 B** 的依据：文章开篇（第2段）作者写道："There's no doubt that Neusner should have taken a closer look at what college life is really like before formulating such a strong opinion about it. He is completely ignoring all the pressures and hard times students go through to make it at college."明确表示Neusner在发表这么强烈的观点之前，应该更仔细地了解大学生活的实际状况，他完全忽视了学生在大学中所承受的压力与艰辛。随后第3、4、5段分别从独立性培养、后果机制和日常压力等维度反驳Neusner，始终围绕"他的描述不准确"这一核心立场展开。

**A 选项错误**：Timothee与Neusner的观点是对立的，而非认同。她在第3段明确说"I think that he is very wrong"，全文都在反驳Neusner，并非赞同他的观点。

**C 选项错误**：文章中没有任何关于"改革评分制度"的建议或呼吁。这是一个超出文本范围的干扰项，利用读者对教育改革话题的联想来迷惑。

**D 选项错误**：D项表达的立场（大学教了错误的价值观）与Neusner一致，而非与Timothee一致，正好是Timothee竭力反驳的观点，与全文主旨相悖。

**2.** According to paragraph 3, which of the following is listed as a benefit of the college experience?

A. Students learn how to compete with peers from the same hometown.

B. Students develop independence and learn to make responsible decisions.

C. Students gain access to prestigious internship opportunities.

D. Students are shielded from the pressures of real-world decision-making.

**Answer: B**

**解析**：本题为**细节理解题**，考查对第3段具体列举内容的识别。

**正确答案 B** 的依据：原文第3段写道："The college years, for many of us, are when we start to be independent, make crucial decisions on our own, and become responsible for them."（对我们许多人来说，大学时期是我们开始独立、自主做出关键决定并为之承担责任的阶段。）B项"Students develop independence and learn to make responsible decisions"正是对这句话的准确转述。

**A 选项错误**：原文提到的是"meet people from different parts of the world"（与来自世界各地的人相识），并非"与来自同一家乡的同学竞争"。"same hometown"是对"different parts of the world"的曲解，属于张冠李戴的干扰。

**C 选项错误**：文章完全没有提到实习机会（internship opportunities），属于无中生有的干扰项。

**D 选项错误**：此选项的意思恰好与Neusner的批判观点接近（大学屏蔽了学生对真实世界的感知），而Timothee的论点是相反的——大学让学生接触真实挑战，而非保护他们不受压力。D项与作者立场背道而驰。

**3.** In paragraph 4, Timothee uses a series of "If...then" structures mainly to __________.

A. illustrate that college students face real and lasting consequences for poor academic performance.

B. suggest that university regulations are too harsh and should be relaxed.

C. admit that some of Neusner's criticisms about college standards are partially valid.

D. show that students have many opportunities to recover from academic mistakes.

**Answer: A**

**解析**：本题为**推断题**，考查对作者写作手法和论证意图的理解。

**正确答案 A** 的依据：第4段中，Timothee连续使用条件句式："If you fail a test, you can't take it again... If you drop out of a class, next semester you will have to take more courses. If you get low grades, your chances of getting into a fine graduate school are almost none. If your grade point average is not reasonably high... you just don't get your degree."每一个"If"句都指向一个**真实的、持续性的负面后果**，目的是反驳Neusner所说的"failure leaves no record"，证明大学里的失败确实会留下记录并影响未来。

**B 选项错误**：Timothee恰恰相反——她用这些条件句来**强调**大学规定的严格性和后果的严重性，并非主张"应该放松管制"。

**C 选项错误**：文章中Timothee从未承认Neusner的批评"部分有效"。她在第2段明确说"I think that he is very wrong"，第4段的论证是对Neusner的全面反驳，而非部分接受。

**D 选项错误**：这些"If"句列举的全是**无法弥补的后果**（考试失败不能重考、GPA低就进不了研究生院、绩点不够就拿不到学位），并非表明"有很多机会弥补"。D项是对原文逻辑方向的逆转。

**4.** The phrase "I have news for him" (paragraph 4) suggests that the author __________.

A. intends to share a recent news article that supports Neusner's argument.

B. is about to present evidence that directly contradicts Neusner's claims.

C. wants to inform Neusner about changes in university admissions policies.

D. feels sympathetic toward Neusner and hopes to provide him with useful information.

**Answer: B**

**解析**：本题为**语义推断题**，考查对习语用法及上下文语气的判断。

**正确答案 B** 的依据："I have news for him"是英语中常见的口语化表达，含有"让我来纠正你的错误认知"的语气，后面通常紧跟反驳性内容。原文中这句话之后，Timothee立即列举了一系列大学里确实存在不可避免后果的例子（"If you fail a test, you can't take it again..."），这些都是针对Neusner"failure leaves no record"观点的直接反驳证据。因此B项"about to present evidence that directly contradicts Neusner's claims"准确描述了该短语的功能。

**A 选项错误**："I have news for him"在此语境下并非指分享新闻报道（news article），而是一个习语表达。且后续内容是Timothee自己的亲身经历和论述，并非引用报道，也不支持Neusner观点。

**C 选项错误**：文章根本没有涉及"大学招生政策变化"的话题，此选项属于无中生有的干扰。

**D 选项错误**：从全文语气来看，Timothee对Neusner的态度是批评性和对抗性的（"He is completely ignoring..."，"I think that he is very wrong"），并非同情或善意提供帮助。

**5.** Which of the following best describes the tone of the last paragraph?

A. Resigned and sorrowful, accepting that college life is indeed too easy.

B. Ironic and emphatic, using Neusner's own words to refute his characterization of college.

C. Humorous and light-hearted, mocking Neusner's lack of firsthand experience.

D. Objective and scholarly, presenting statistical evidence to disprove Neusner's claims.

**Answer: B**

**解析**：本题为**语气/态度题**，考查对第5段写作风格和论证策略的理解。

**正确答案 B** 的依据：第5段原文为："To me, living in a crowded dorm room, having a one-day reading period before finals, tons of reading, papers, and midterms due the same week are not exactly my idea of 'easy, free, forgiving, attentive, comfortable, interesting, unchallenging fun.'"作者在此**直接引用Neusner的原话**（即第一篇文章第7段中Daniel教授的引语），并将其与自己真实的大学生活体验并置，产生强烈的**反讽效果**——宿舍拥挤、期末复习期只有一天、多科同周截止……这些压力与"easy, free, forgiving"的描述形成鲜明对比，通过引用对方原词来反驳对方，手法讽刺而有力。

**A 选项错误**："resigned and sorrowful"（认命、悲伤）完全不符合本段甚至全文的语气。Timothee的语气自始至终是主动反驳的、有力的，而非消极接受的。

**C 选项错误**：虽然可以感受到Timothee语气中有轻微的反讽意味，但将其定性为"humorous and light-hearted"（幽默轻松）过于轻化了文章的反驳力度和严肃性。"mocking"（嘲弄）也过度解读了作者态度，原文更接近"ironically refuting"（反讽式反驳）。

**D 选项错误**："objective and scholarly"（客观学术）与本段的第一人称亲身经历叙述风格不符，且本段没有任何统计数据，属于错误描述。
